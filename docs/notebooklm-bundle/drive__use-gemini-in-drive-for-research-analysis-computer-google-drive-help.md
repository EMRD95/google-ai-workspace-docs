# Use Gemini in Drive for research & analysis

**Important**: To use Gemini in Drive, you must have an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan. [Learn more about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129?hl=en&co=DASHER._Family%3DBusiness-Enterprise).

## Overview
- Group related files, folders, or emails (e.g., financial reports, pitch decks) as sources.
- Ask Gemini questions to connect the dots across your data.
- Capabilities:
  - Analyze insights across multiple folders, shared drives, Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, PDFs, Gmail, Google Chat, and Google Calendar.
  - Identify patterns, compare file versions, verify compliance against internal policies.
  - Get answers with direct citations to original documents.
  - Save grouped sources as a **Project** – a focused knowledge base.
  - Share a project with others.
  - Create new Google documents and images from Gemini outputs.

## Examples of Prompts
> "Synthesize the client feedback from Q1 meeting notes and write a list of specific requirements I should bring up on today's call based on our latest product deck."

> "Identify the top 3 risks mentioned across the Q2 operational updates," or "Compare the Q3 budget forecast sheet against our Q4 projected spend presentations."

> "Read the notes from Tuesday's marketing meeting sync and summarize all open action items that pertain to the social team."

## How to Start an “Ask Gemini” Session

### From a specific file or folder
1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. Navigate to the folder you want to analyze.
3. In the toolbar at the top, click **Ask Gemini** (sparkle icon).  
   → Opens a full-screen workspace with the selected files listed as your sources.

### From scratch
1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. At the top right, click **Ask Gemini** (sparkle icon).  
   → Full-screen workspace opens.
   - **Default settings**: Search across other Workspace apps (Drive, Gmail, Chat, Calendar) is turned **on**. Web search is **off**.

## Resuming Conversations
Gemini automatically saves your conversation history.

1. Click **Ask Gemini** at the top right.
2. In the left panel, click **History**.
3. Your history view depends on how you opened Gemini:
   - **From a Folder or Project**: previous conversations related to those sources appear under **Suggested**.
   - **From anywhere else in Drive**: conversations appear under **All**, sorted by most recent.
4. Continue where you left off.

## Drive Projects – Save & Share Sources
- Projects group all chats about specific sources, making it easy to find conversations.
- You can have multiple unique conversations using the same foundational documents.
- Share a project with your team so everyone can use the same set of documents.
- [Learn how to share your Gemini research with Drive projects](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/16686465).

## Managing Sources and Search Settings
You can control which sources Gemini uses when answering.

### Active Sources
- **Add sources**: In the left panel, under “Your sources” or “Project sources,” click **Add**.
  - **Tip**: You can add specific Gmail emails as sources.
- **Remove sources**: Hover over a source and click **Remove** (X icon). This removes the source from the analysis.
- **Temporarily exclude a source**: Uncheck the box next to the source. Gemini won’t use it, but it stays in the list.

### Suggested Sources
Gemini may suggest additional files based on your current sources or project name.  
**Important**: Suggested files are **not used** until you explicitly add them.

- To view suggestions: click **Suggested** in the left panel to expand the section.
- To add a suggestion: click **Add** next to the file to move it into active sources.

### Broadening Your Search
To let Gemini pull context from your entire Google Workspace:

1. In the left panel, toggle on **Let Gemini search for sources**.
2. Optionally, fine‑tune by checking/unchecking:
   - **Drive** – wider Drive content beyond pinned sources.
   - **Chat, Calendar, Gmail** – recent communications and meeting schedules.
   - **Web** – supplemental public internet data.
   - The primary toggle remains on; you only uncheck specific locations to limit.

[Learn more about search settings](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/16813283).

## Verifying Answers with Citations
- Hover over a citation number (e.g., `[1]`) at the end of a sentence to see which file provided the information.
- Click the file chip in the citation to open the original document in a new tab.
