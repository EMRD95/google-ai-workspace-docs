# Generate & Insert an Image

## Where It Works

- Google Slides
- Google Vids

## Steps

1. On your computer, open:
   - a presentation in **Google Slides**, or
   - a video in **Google Vids**.
2. Click the slide or scene where you want to add the image.
3. Open the image generation tool:
   - In **Slides**:  
     **Insert → Help me visualize → Image**
   - In **Vids**:  
     **Insert → Generate an image**
4. In the right panel, enter a prompt.
5. Optionally customize:
   - **Style** using **Add a style**
   - **Aspect ratio**
6. Click **Create**.
7. Preview the generated image by clicking it.
8. To insert it:
   - Click the image in the right panel.
   - In the preview window, click **Insert**.

## Example Prompts

Original examples from the article:

- “A beautiful photograph of a Hawaiian beach at sunset with palm trees in the distance.”
- “A plate of freshly baked blueberry muffins on a wooden kitchen table next to a large window.”
- “Watercolor painting of hydrangeas in a water pitcher.”
- “A futuristic cityscape with skyscrapers and flying cars set at night with HD details and shadows.”

## Aspect Ratio Options

| Option | Ratio |
|---|---:|
| **Square** | 1:1 |
| **Wide** | 16:9 |
| **Tall** | 9:16 |

## Prompting Tip

For better results, avoid figurative or vague language. Include concrete details such as:

- Subject
- Setting
- Distance to subject
- Materials
- Background

Example:

> “A close up of a boat made out of wood on a lake surrounded by trees at sunrise.“

# Edit an Image

## Steps

1. Open a presentation in **Google Slides** or a video in **Google Vids**.
2. Click an image.
3. Select **Edit image**.
4. Enter a prompt describing the desired change.
5. Click **Create**.
6. Preview the result by clicking the generated image.
7. To replace the original, click **Replace with image**.

## Optional Insert Choices

Next to **Replace with image**, click the down arrow to choose:

- **Insert as image**
- **Insert as background**

You can also close the preview with **Close**.

## Example Edit Prompts

- “Place this image within a frame hanging in an art gallery.”
- “Add a fluffy white cat.”

# Create a Slide in Google Slides — Beta

## Important Notes

- Available **only in Google Slides**.
- This feature generates an **image of a slide**, based on:
  - the active slide, and
  - the presentation content.
- It is a **beta** feature and may make mistakes.
- To generate an editable slide instead, use the separate **Generate a slide** feature.

## Steps

1. Open **Google Slides**.
2. Click a slide.
3. Go to:  
   **Insert → Help me visualize → Slide**
4. In the side panel, enter a prompt for the slide.
5. Click **Create**.
6. Optionally edit the generated slide image using another prompt.
7. Select the generated slide image when satisfied.
8. Choose:
   - **Insert as new slide**, or
   - **Insert on this slide**

## Recommended Prompt Details

More detailed prompts usually produce better results. Include:

- Title and body text
- Goal
- Overall tone
- Layout and visual style
- Technical specifics, such as fonts

## Example Edit Prompt

- “Update the title header to be a lighter shade of blue and underline it.”
