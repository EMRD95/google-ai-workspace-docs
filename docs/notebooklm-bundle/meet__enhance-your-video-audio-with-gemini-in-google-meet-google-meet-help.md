## Key Requirements & Availability

> **Important:**
>
> - This feature requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan.
> - Sometimes, you may not be able to generate a background because Smart feature control is disabled.

These Gemini-powered Meet features may also be available through **Google Workspace Experiments**, Google’s early access testing program.

---

## Features Covered

Gemini in Google Meet can enhance:

- **Studio look** — AI improves video quality affected by low light or low-quality webcams.
- **Studio lighting** — Simulates studio-quality lighting with adjustable position, brightness, and color.
- **Studio sound** — AI improves poor audio quality from Bluetooth headsets and dial-in participants.
- **Cloud-based visual effects processing** — Offloads certain video effects to the cloud to save device resources.

# Studio Look & Studio Lighting Requirements

## Supported Browsers / Platforms

You need a browser version that supports enhanced video:

- **Windows, Mac, or Linux**
  - Chrome **M114+**
  - Firefox **110+**
- **ChromeOS**
  - Version **M114+**
- **Windows or Mac**
  - Edge Chromium **114+**

Additional requirements:

- Browser must support **WebGL**
- **Hardware acceleration** must be turned on
- For **studio lighting**, a sufficiently powerful processor is required

---

## Studio Lighting Processor Requirement

> **Important:** In order to view and use this feature, you must have a processor that is equivalent or more performant than Intel Core i9-9880H (8C/16T), Intel Core i5-1235U (2P8E/12T), AMD Ryzen 5 5500U (6C/12T), and Apple M1 (4P4E/8T).

---

## Turn On Hardware Acceleration in Chrome

To enable hardware acceleration on Windows or Mac:

1. In Chrome, click **Menu** → **Settings**.
2. On the left, next to **Advanced**, click the down arrow.
3. Click **System**.
4. If available, turn on **Use hardware acceleration**.
5. Restart Chrome.

---

## Check WebGL Support

You can check WebGL support at:

- https://webglreport.com/?v=2

Verify that:

> “Major Performance Caveat” is marked as “No”.

Notes:

- If a device is unstable or causes crashes, the browser may disable WebGL.
- You can’t force WebGL on unstable WebGL devices.
- Updating graphics card drivers or restarting the browser may help.

# Cloud-Based Effects Processing

Google Meet can process some video effects in the cloud to help devices run real-time video more efficiently.

## Benefits

Cloud-based processing can help:

- Save battery
- Save processor power
- Optimize video meeting quality

## How It Works

Meet automatically adjusts between device-based and cloud-based effects processing.

It prioritizes cloud-based processing for:

- Devices with **4-core CPUs or lower**
- Devices with **fewer than 8 logical cores**

## Availability

> **Important:** This feature is currently available for Windows, ChromeOS, and Linux machines that comply with device-based processing requirements.

Cloud-accelerated visual effects are available in eligible regions:

- Central Asia
- Europe
- Northern Africa
- North America
- Southeastern Asia

Support for additional regions is being added.

# Turn On Studio Look

## What Studio Look Does

Studio look uses AI to improve Google Meet video quality by fixing issues caused by:

- Low light
- Low-quality webcams

The enhancement happens automatically in the cloud and does **not impact device performance**.

## Steps to Enable Studio Look

1. On your computer, open https://meet.google.com/.
2. Select a video meeting.
3. In the green room before joining, or during the meeting, open the **Effects** panel:
   - In the green room: click **Apply visual effects** on the bottom right of your self-view.
   - During a meeting: hover over your self-view and click the effects icon in the center.
   - Or click **More options** → **Apply visual effects**.
4. Under **Appearance**, turn on **Studio look**.

# Turn On Studio Lighting

## What Studio Lighting Does

Studio lighting simulates studio-quality lighting and lets you adjust:

- Lighting position
- Brightness
- Color

## Steps to Enable Studio Lighting

1. On your computer, open https://meet.google.com/.
2. Select a video meeting.
3. In the green room before joining, or during the meeting, open the **Effects** panel.
4. Click **More options** → **Settings**.
   - In the green room, you can also click the effects icon on the bottom right of your self-view.
   - During a meeting, hover over your self-view and click the effects icon.
   - Or click **More options** → **Apply Visual Effects**.
5. Under **Appearance**, turn on **Studio lighting**.
