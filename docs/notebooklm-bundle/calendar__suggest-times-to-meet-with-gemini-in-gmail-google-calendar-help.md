# Suggest Times to Meet with Gemini in Gmail — Summary

**Source:** Google Calendar Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/16865189?hl=en

## Key Excerpts

> **Important:** This feature requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan. [Learn about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13952129).

> To set up meetings with one or more people in Gmail, use the “Help me schedule” feature when you draft a message or reply. Based on you and your guests’ availability, Gemini in Gmail generates a list of times when you’re all available.

## What the Feature Does

Gemini in Gmail can help schedule meetings directly from an email draft or reply using **Help me schedule**. It checks availability for you and invited guests, then inserts suggested meeting times into the email.

## Requirements

- Requires an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan.
- Available in **Gmail on a computer**.
- Used while composing or replying to an email.

## Insert Suggested Times in a Gmail Message

1. On your computer, go to [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/).
2. Create or reply to an email with one or more recipients.
3. At the bottom of your message, click **Help me schedule**.
   - If you can’t find it, click **More options** → **Help me schedule**.
4. Review the suggested times Gemini generates.
5. Click **Send** at the bottom of the email.

### Tips

- A maximum of **20 guests** can be added to a single schedule.
- If email recipients change after the scheduling card is inserted, the guest list **does not update automatically**.
  - You must **re-insert the schedule**.
- Gemini in Gmail proposes **4 time slots by default**.

## Update the Suggested Times Card

Hover over **Edit in Calendar** and choose one of the available options:

- **Meeting duration:** Change the meeting length.
- **Time range:** Adjust the time range used to find available meeting times.
- **Change to text format:** Send meeting times as plain text instead of a suggestion card.
- **More options:** Insert a booking page from your appointment schedule.

## Update Suggested Times Based on Guest Calendars

You can manually review and refine suggestions based on guest calendars.

### Steps

1. In the suggested times card, click **Edit in Calendar**.
2. In the **“Share your availability”** window, you can:
   - **Check availability:** View availability for any guest calendar you have access to.
   - **Change meeting duration:** Adjust the meeting length.
   - **Add guest time zone:** Include a guest’s time zone.
   - **Add or remove guests:** Modify the guest list on the right side.
     - Suggestions update automatically based on the new guest list.
   - **Review time slots:** Check proposed meeting times when calendars are busy.

### Time Slot Colors

- **Green:** All guests are available.
- **Amber:** One or more guests have a conflict.

If amber conflicts appear, a banner prompts you to manually select time slots. Use the calendar grid to ensure the chosen time works for priority guests.

3. Click **Propose times** at the bottom right.

## Book Time from Someone Else’s Suggested Times

If someone sends you suggested times, you can book a preferred time directly from the email.

Once booked:

- A Google Calendar event is created.
- All guests receive an event invitation.

### Steps

1. In the email, select a suggested time.
2. On the **“Book your preferred time”** page, choose:
   - **Book time for all guests** if there are no conflicts.
   - **View all times on calendar** if there are conflicts or you want another time.
     - Then select a new time slot in the calendar window.
