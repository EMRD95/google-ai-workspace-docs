# Use Gemini Apps with a Work or School Google Account — Business / Enterprise Summary

**Source:** Google Gemini Apps Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/14620100?hl=en

---

## Core Takeaways

- Most users with a **work or school Google Account** can access Gemini Apps.
- **Feature availability, model access, usage limits, terms, and data handling** depend on the user’s **Google Workspace license**.
- Gemini Apps may be available either as:
  - an **additional service**, or
  - a **core Google Workspace service** with **enterprise-grade security and privacy**.
- To use Gemini Apps with a work account, users must be **18 or older**.
- Usage limits apply across devices and Gemini entry points, including:
  - [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com/)
  - Gemini mobile app
  - Gemini in Chrome

> **Important:** “Gemini is AI and can make mistakes, including about people. Some content may not be suitable for everyone.”

## Context Window

| Access Level | Context Size |
|---|---|
| Standard / Pro levels | **32 thousand** |
| Higher tiers | **1 million** |

### What a Context Window Means

A context window is Gemini’s “reading capacity” when processing prompts, files, and conversations.

> “The context window is like the reading capacity. It determines how much content can be understood together at one time.”

### Why a Larger Context Window Matters

A larger context window allows Gemini to process more content at once.

Example:

> “With a 1M token context window, Gemini can understand up to 1,500 pages of text or 30,000 lines of code.”

If the context window is exceeded, responses may fail to account for all uploaded or provided content, especially in large files with details spread throughout.
