# Build or edit entire spreadsheets with Gemini in Sheets

> **Important:**

## Use Gemini in Sheets

1. Open [Google Sheets](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets) on your computer.  
2. The side panel opens automatically with **Build**.  
   - Type a prompt to edit or create a spreadsheet.  
3. *(Optional)* Answer any clarification questions that appear.  
4. Review the **plan** and **template outline** that Gemini provides.  
5. *(Optional)* Manage data sources:  
   - **Add sources** – click the button to include referenced data.  
   - **Clear** – remove all added sources.

## Sample Prompts to Ask Gemini

### Generate a new sheet
- `“Create a sheet to track my monthly expenses with columns for date, category, amount and notes.”`
- `“Create a table for a project tracker in Google Sheets with columns for task, assignee, deadline and status.”`
- `“Create a 10-day itinerary for a first-time visitor covering Tokyo, Kyoto and Osaka.”`

### Complete end-to-end tasks
- `“Run a full analysis on the data set to help me make decisions about my business.”`
- `“Create a visual dashboard of this sales data set.”`
- `“Update my budget based on last year’s revenue.”`

**Tip:** If more information is needed, follow the prompts to help Gemini deliver a more accurate result.

## Stop Gemini in Sheets

- **Press the stop button** inside the side panel.  
- **Submit a new prompt** – this restarts the generation process for a fresh sheet.

## Give Feedback

Rating a suggestion:

1. Click **Good suggestion** 👍 or **Bad suggestion** 👎 at the bottom right of the generated text.  
2. If you choose **Bad suggestion**, you can:  
   - Select the issue you found.  
   - Enter additional feedback.  
   - *(Optional)* Review the data that will be attached with your feedback.  
     - Untick **“Attach collected data to your feedback”** if you don’t want to include it.  
3. Click **Next**.  
4. Review additional context; untick **“Additional context”** if you prefer not to share it.  
5. Click **Submit**.

For general feedback: **Help → Help Sheets improve**.  
To report a legal issue: [create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

## Tips for Writing Prompts for Gemini

Four key areas make an effective prompt. You don’t need all four, but combining them yields better results:

| Area | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Persona** | Tell Gemini what role to play for context on behavior and tone. | *I am a customer service representative.* |
| **Task** | Specify what to do using an action verb (summarize, write, create, etc.). | *Help me craft an empathetic email response.* |
| **Context** | Provide relevant background information. Attach files using `@` followed by the file name to include more details. | *The customer ordered a pair of headphones that arrived damaged.* |
| **Format** | Be explicit about the desired structure (bullets, paragraphs, tables, etc.). | *Include a paragraph that acknowledges their frustration and 3 bullet points with potential resolutions.* |

**Combined prompt from the table:**

> I am a customer service representative. Help me craft an empathetic email response. The customer ordered a pair of headphones that arrived damaged. Include a paragraph that acknowledges their frustration and 3 bullet points with potential resolutions.

### Additional Best Practices

- **Use natural language:** Write as you speak, in full sentences.
- **Be specific and iterate:**
  - Provide as much context as possible.
  - Attach relevant documents by typing `@` to select from suggestions or type a file name.
  - Use descriptive prompts to clarify audience, purpose, and goals.
- **Make it a conversation:** Refine results with follow-up prompts. Build on previous outputs and use an iterative process of review and refinement.

# Use enhanced Smart Fill with AI in Google Sheets (Workspace Experiments)

> **Important:** This feature is part of the Google Workspace Experiments trusted tester program, currently available only to selected users in US English. Availability may vary by country. To check access, open a new sheet and look for the **`Help me organize`** sidebar on the right.

## How It Works

Enhanced Smart Fill uses AI to detect relationships between partially filled column pairs and instantly predicts the remaining values. It works with text columns only (not numeric/date columns) and requires:

- At least **3 example rows** with **2 distinct column values** to be filled.
- **English** text values.

### Triggering Suggestions

- Suggestions appear after an edit or **live while you type** (as soon as a prediction is available).
- A colored outline shows the range that Sheets can predict; hover over the suggestion icon to see the fill preview.

### Accepting or Rejecting

- **Accept:** Click the suggestion bubble.
- **Reject:** Click the cancel icon on the expanded suggestion pill.

## Example Use Cases

- Categorize feedback by theme
- Organize news articles by topic
- Convert addresses to a consistent format
- Extract phone numbers from text fields

## Giving Feedback

1. Click the three-dot menu on any suggestion.
2. Select **`Send feedback`**.

For general feedback: go to **Help** → **`Help Sheets improve`**.  
For legal issues: use [this form](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420) or **`Send feedback`** → **`Report an issue`** / **`Suggest an idea`** → click **`Legal Help`** at the bottom of the sidebar.

## Turning Off Enhanced Smart Fill

You can disable only the AI-enhanced suggestions while keeping standard Smart Fill active.

### From a cell suggestion
1. Click the three-dot menu on any suggestion.
2. Uncheck **`Enable enhanced Smart Fill suggestions`**.

### From the menu
1. Go to **Tools** → **`Suggestion controls`**.
2. Uncheck **`Enable enhanced Smart Fill suggestions`**.

To re-enable, follow the same path and re-check the option.

> **Note:** When turned off, all undecided suggestions are removed from all sheets. This **does not** disable standard [Smart Fill](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/9914525) (e.g., extracting first names from full names).  
> Exiting the Labs program permanently revokes access to all Labs features and cannot be reversed. Learn about [exiting Labs](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447104#labs-opt-out).

## Important Limitations & Data Practices

- **Only English** text values are supported.
- Suggestions are not professional advice and may be inaccurate or inappropriate.
- Google processes data according to the [Workspace Experiments Privacy Notice & Terms](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447401). Data may be read by human reviewers, but if Google-selected input is used to generate output, the content is **aggregated/pseudonymized** before review (unless provided directly via feedback).
- Avoid entering personal, confidential, or sensitive information.
- Your feedback helps improve the feature and broader AI efforts.

# Use the AI function in Google Sheets (Workspace Experiments)

## Capabilities

You can use the AI function to:

- **Generate text** – tailored to your data using Gemini.
- **Summarize information** – extract key takeaways from spreadsheet content.
- **Categorize information** – group data and detect patterns (includes sentiment analysis).
- **Analyze sentiment** – classify the emotional tone of text.
- **Access real-time information** – fetch up-to-date answers from Google Search.

## Basic Usage

1. Open a [Google Sheets](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/u/0/) spreadsheet.
2. In a cell, enter either **`=AI()`** or **`=Gemini()`**.  
   Example:  
   `=AI(“Generate slogan for event in 10 words or less”, A2)`  
   Or go to **Insert → Function → AI**.
3. Select the cell(s) containing the AI function.
4. Click **Generate and Insert**.
5. *Optional:* Click **Refresh and Insert** to regenerate.

> **Tip:** When you generate or refresh, the cell edit is attributed to you in version history.

## Adding an AI Column to a Table

- Click **Insert AI column right** at the top right of the table’s last column.
- The first non-header row displays the AI function automatically.  
- Enter a prompt in that row; then you can autofill the rest of the column.

## Fill Columns with Gemini

*Also available for Google AI Ultra and Google AI Pro accounts.*

- **Drag-and-drop fill:** If a column has at least one completed cell, a new drag entry point lets you fill the rest based on table context.
- **Prompt-based filling:** For an empty multi-cell selection, use the one-click “Fill” entry point. Choose automatic fill (if the column already has a completed cell) or enter a custom prompt.

## Syntax

```excel
AI(“prompt”,[optional range])
```

- **prompt:** A description of the action you want (in quotes).  
- **optional range:** The cells the prompt should reference when generating output. **Strongly recommended** to improve accuracy.

## Detailed Examples

### Generate New Text
```excel
=AI(“Generate slogan for event in 10 words or less”, A2)
=AI("Create an email to the reviewer addressing specific items in their reviews.", A2:G2)
=AI("develop a list of keywords for the job title based on the summary of duties.", A2:C2)
```

### Summarize Input
```excel
=AI("For the customer, write a one sentence summary of their feedback.", A2:D2)
=AI("You are the owner of a pet sitting business. Write a 2 sentence summary for the customer about their pet’s last stay. Be a little funny.", F2)
=AI("List in bullet points the main themes of the book summary.", D2)
```

### Categorize Inputs
```excel
=AI("Classify the preview as either a spam email or not a spam email.", D2)
=AI("Categorize the customer inquiry as a compliment, exchange request, or return request.",C2)
=AI("Classify the restaurant by which New York City borough it belongs to. Use the neighborhood to help.", A2:C2)
```

### Analyze Sentiment
```excel
=AI("Classify the sentiment of the realtor analysis.", D2)
=AI("Perform sentiment analysis on the emails sent by the customers to the barbershop.", C2)
=AI("Classify the body of the email, as either positive, negative, or neutral.", D2)
```

### Fetch Real-Time Information
```excel
=AI("What is the current capital of Kazakhstan?", A2)
=AI("What is the population of the list capital city?", A2:B2)
=AI("What is the birthday of the author who wrote the associated book. Only output the date", A2:B2")
```

## Handling Non‑contiguous Ranges

Use concatenation inside the prompt to reference multiple separate ranges, but be aware that changes to those ranges will **not** trigger a refresh automatically. You must regenerate manually.

Example:  
```excel
=AI("Find the major themes in the customer feedback of "&B2&" using the comments: "&D2&"")
```

- Click **Good suggestion** 👍 or **Bad suggestion** 👎 at the bottom right of the generated output.

  - Prompts
  - Ranges (only the referenced cells, without their context)
  - Outputs

- For a good suggestion: click **Submit**.
- For general feedback: **Help → Help Sheets improve**.
- For legal issues: [create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

> **Important:** Feedback may be human‑readable. Do not submit personal, confidential, or sensitive information.
