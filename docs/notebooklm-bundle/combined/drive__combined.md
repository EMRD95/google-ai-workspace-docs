## Feature Availability
- Requires an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI plan** (see [Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129))
- Also available through **Google Workspace Experiments** (trusted tester program for personal accounts)

## Supported Languages
- Gemini in Drive side panel supports a subset of languages. [Full list](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13607340).

## What You Can Do with Gemini in Drive
- Summarize a long document or an entire folder
- Discuss a topic by synthesizing info from one or more files (across Drive or within a specific folder)
- Retrieve quick facts about a project without opening documents
- Move or create Google files and folders

## Get Started with Gemini in Drive

1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/)
2. Click **Ask Gemini** (star icon) at the top right
3. In the side panel, choose a suggested prompt or write your own
4. Optional: to clear history, click **More options** → **Clear history**

> **Important:** Conversation history is lost when you reload the browser, close/reopen the document, or go offline.

### Prompt Examples
- *Select a suggestion* – pick from the list; choose **More suggestions** for more; replace placeholder text and press **Enter**
- *Enter your own* – type a prompt and press **Enter**

## Things You Can Ask Gemini

### Search for Files
- Click **Ask Gemini** and describe the file by content (e.g., “Find the spreadsheet with Q3 revenue”)

### Ask Questions About a Specific File
- Click **Ask Gemini**, then use **Add Sources** or type **@** + file name
  - Example: `"Summarize the key points in @Sales Team Meeting Notes"`
- After response: click **Sources** to see referenced files; use **View more**, **Retry** for new text.

> **Tip:** Remove selected sources with **Clear selected sources**.

Other file-level prompts:
- “When is our contract with Vendor A expiring?”
- “Summarize this file for me in bullet points”
- “How many customer sign-ups did we get for this test application?”
- “Who wrote the Project B design doc?”

### Ask Questions About a Specific Folder
- Click **Ask Gemini**, add folder with `@` + folder name
  - Example: `"Find my files for Project Clover in @2024 Projects"`
- After response, **Sources** shows which files were used.

Folder-level prompts:
- “When is the next product review based on this folder?”
- “Tell me about @[Contract folder]”
- “What are the documents from Alice in this folder?”

### Summarize a File or Folder
- Use `@` to specify the file/folder, optionally combine with a summarize prompt.
  - Example: `"Summarize the key points in @Sales Team Meeting Notes"`
- For summaries, **Sources** indicates files used; use **Retry** for new version.

### Summarize Information Across Multiple Files
- Ask broad questions without `@` to search across all accessible files.
  - Examples:
    - “Help me understand our team’s 2024 strategy.”
    - “Summarize project X.”
- Response includes **Sources**, feedback buttons, and **Retry**.

### Move Files
- Use `@` to mention up to **5 files** and a target folder.
  - Examples:
    - “Move @Acme-Results to @Clients-Acme folder.”
    - “Move @Acme-Drafts and @Acme-Results to @Clients-Acme folder.”
- Press Enter, then click **Move** in the preview card. Permissions may change.

### Create New Files & Folders
- Prompt to create folders or files (if no name given, Gemini prompts for one)
  - “Create a folder called Design.”
  - “Create a sheet called Acme Campaign Planning.”
- Click **Create** in the preview card; new item is placed in the current folder (if open with edit access) or in My Drive.

## Manage summary cards on PDFs and videos

When you open a PDF or a video with a transcript in Drive, a summary card may appear to give you a quick overview of the file.

To turn off summary cards:

1. Open a PDF or video file.
2. On the summary card or in the file toolbar at the top, click **More**.
3. Click **Disable summary cards**.

To turn summary cards back on:

1. Open a PDF or video file.
2. In the file toolbar at the top, click **More**.
3. Click **Enable summary cards**.

## Key Points

> **Important:**
>
> - Gemini in file viewer currently supports PDF and video file types.
> - Some Gemini features are only available in English.

Gemini can be used inside the Google Drive file viewer to interact with supported files, especially **PDFs** and **videos**. It can summarize content, answer questions, help create new content, combine information from other Drive files, and identify action items.

---

## What You Can Do with Gemini in Drive File Viewer

Gemini supports several workflows for supported file types:

- **Summarize:** Get a quick overview of PDFs and videos.
- **Ask questions:** Find specific information or insights from a PDF or video.
- **Create new content:** Use a PDF to make something new, such as:
  - A study plan
  - An email draft
- **Bring in more information:** Combine the PDF with other files in Google Drive by typing `@`.
- **Identify action items:** Use meeting recordings to check whether you missed important action items.

---

## Feature Availability

Gemini in Drive file viewer is not universally available.

Requirements and availability notes:

- Requires an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan.
- Supported languages vary by Gemini feature.
- Available through **Google Workspace Experiments**, a trusted tester program for users with personal accounts to try new AI features.

To check whether you have Workspace Experiments access:

1. Open a new presentation in **Google Slides**.
2. Look for a bottom bar indicating Workspace Experiments access.

Relevant help links from the article:

- [Learn about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129)
- [Learn about supported languages for Google Workspace with Gemini](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/14925782)
- [Learn how to get started with Google Workspace Experiments](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447104)

## Open Drive File Viewer

When you double-click a supported file in Drive, it opens in the **overlay file viewer**.

To open a PDF or video in a dedicated space:

1. Go to [drive.google.com](http://drive.google.com/) on your computer.
2. Double-click a **PDF** or **video**.
3. Click **More actions** → **Open with** → **Open in new tab**.

**Tip:**  
To adjust the default settings for opening a PDF, go to your [Drive settings](https://drive.google.com/drive/u/0/settings).

## Get Automatic Summaries

To open Gemini in the Drive file viewer:

- Click **Ask Gemini** at the top right of the navigation bar.

When Gemini opens in the file viewer, the side panel **automatically summarizes the file**.

The summary provides a quick overview of the main points in the open PDF or video.

---

## Ask Questions and Get Answers

You can ask Gemini questions about the PDF or video you are viewing.

Steps:

1. Enter a question in the side panel’s textbox.
   - You can also select a **starter tile** to use a template prompt.
2. Review Gemini’s answer in the side panel.
3. Give feedback by clicking **thumbs-up** or **thumbs-down**.

---

## Sources for Gemini’s Answers

Gemini uses relevant sources to generate answers. These may include:

- The open PDF
- The open video
- Other files in your Google Drive
- Information from the web
- A combination of these sources

To help users verify accuracy, Gemini provides links to the sources used.

Important source behavior:

- Sources are expanded by default.
- Clicking a source chip opens that source file in a new tab.

Important consequences:

- Exiting permanently removes access to **all Workspace Experiments features**.
- You cannot rejoin Workspace Experiments after exiting.

# Use Gemini in Drive for research & analysis

**Important**: To use Gemini in Drive, you must have an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan. [Learn more about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129?hl=en&co=DASHER._Family%3DBusiness-Enterprise).

## Overview
- Group related files, folders, or emails (e.g., financial reports, pitch decks) as sources.
- Ask Gemini questions to connect the dots across your data.
- Capabilities:
  - Analyze insights across multiple folders, shared drives, Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, PDFs, Gmail, Google Chat, and Google Calendar.
  - Identify patterns, compare file versions, verify compliance against internal policies.
  - Get answers with direct citations to original documents.
  - Save grouped sources as a **Project** – a focused knowledge base.
  - Share a project with others.
  - Create new Google documents and images from Gemini outputs.

## Examples of Prompts
> "Synthesize the client feedback from Q1 meeting notes and write a list of specific requirements I should bring up on today's call based on our latest product deck."

> "Identify the top 3 risks mentioned across the Q2 operational updates," or "Compare the Q3 budget forecast sheet against our Q4 projected spend presentations."

> "Read the notes from Tuesday's marketing meeting sync and summarize all open action items that pertain to the social team."

## How to Start an “Ask Gemini” Session

### From a specific file or folder
1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. Navigate to the folder you want to analyze.
3. In the toolbar at the top, click **Ask Gemini** (sparkle icon).  
   → Opens a full-screen workspace with the selected files listed as your sources.

### From scratch
1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. At the top right, click **Ask Gemini** (sparkle icon).  
   → Full-screen workspace opens.
   - **Default settings**: Search across other Workspace apps (Drive, Gmail, Chat, Calendar) is turned **on**. Web search is **off**.

## Resuming Conversations
Gemini automatically saves your conversation history.

1. Click **Ask Gemini** at the top right.
2. In the left panel, click **History**.
3. Your history view depends on how you opened Gemini:
   - **From a Folder or Project**: previous conversations related to those sources appear under **Suggested**.
   - **From anywhere else in Drive**: conversations appear under **All**, sorted by most recent.
4. Continue where you left off.

## Drive Projects – Save & Share Sources
- Projects group all chats about specific sources, making it easy to find conversations.
- You can have multiple unique conversations using the same foundational documents.
- Share a project with your team so everyone can use the same set of documents.
- [Learn how to share your Gemini research with Drive projects](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/16686465).

## Managing Sources and Search Settings
You can control which sources Gemini uses when answering.

### Active Sources
- **Add sources**: In the left panel, under “Your sources” or “Project sources,” click **Add**.
  - **Tip**: You can add specific Gmail emails as sources.
- **Remove sources**: Hover over a source and click **Remove** (X icon). This removes the source from the analysis.
- **Temporarily exclude a source**: Uncheck the box next to the source. Gemini won’t use it, but it stays in the list.

### Suggested Sources
Gemini may suggest additional files based on your current sources or project name.  
**Important**: Suggested files are **not used** until you explicitly add them.

- To view suggestions: click **Suggested** in the left panel to expand the section.
- To add a suggestion: click **Add** next to the file to move it into active sources.

### Broadening Your Search
To let Gemini pull context from your entire Google Workspace:

1. In the left panel, toggle on **Let Gemini search for sources**.
2. Optionally, fine‑tune by checking/unchecking:
   - **Drive** – wider Drive content beyond pinned sources.
   - **Chat, Calendar, Gmail** – recent communications and meeting schedules.
   - **Web** – supplemental public internet data.
   - The primary toggle remains on; you only uncheck specific locations to limit.

[Learn more about search settings](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/16813283).

## Verifying Answers with Citations
- Hover over a citation number (e.g., `[1]`) at the end of a sentence to see which file provided the information.
- Click the file chip in the citation to open the original document in a new tab.
