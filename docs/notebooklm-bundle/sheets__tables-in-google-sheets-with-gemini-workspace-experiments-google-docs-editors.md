## Overview

This help article explains how **Gemini in Google Sheets** can automatically suggest and generate names for tables when using **Google Workspace Experiments** features.

When you accept a **“Convert to table”** suggestion in Google Sheets, Gemini automatically:

- Formats the selected data as a table
- Creates a suggested table name, such as:

> “Project_Status”

You can then **accept, edit, or delete** the suggested table name.

---

## Important Notice: Workspace Experiments Access

> **Important:** This article refers to Google Workspace Experiments, which is a trusted tester program for users to try new AI features.

To check whether you have access:

1. Open a spreadsheet in Google Sheets.
2. Look on the right side for **Summarize this table**.

Google links to additional guidance on where Workspace Experiments features are available.

---

## Generate Table Names in Google Sheets

To use Gemini-generated table names:

1. On your computer, open a spreadsheet in [Google Sheets](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets).
2. Click on a table range.
3. If available, click **Convert to table**.
4. At the top left of the table, find the table name Gemini created.
5. Optional: To edit or delete the suggested name:
   - Click the table name, or
   - Open the **Table menu**
   - Select **Rename table**

### Tip

> You can also change your table's format, and use table references and views.

Google provides a related guide: [Learn how to use tables in Google Sheets](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/14239833).

## Give Feedback About Suggested Table Names

Google notes that Gemini features are still learning and may not always support requests accurately.

If a suggested table name is:

- Inaccurate
- Unsafe
- Otherwise problematic

You can submit feedback to help improve AI-assisted Workspace features and Google’s broader AI systems.

### Privacy Warning for Feedback

> Because feedback may be human readable, do not submit data that contains personal, confidential, or sensitive information.

### Steps to Submit Feedback

1. At the bottom of the suggestion, click:
   - **Good suggestion**, or
   - **Bad suggestion**
2. In the pop-up window, optionally uncheck sharing for:
   - Prompts
   - Outputs
3. If you select **Bad suggestion**, you can choose additional feedback and specify the issue.
4. Click **Submit**.

### General Feature Feedback

To provide broader feedback about the feature:

1. At the top of Sheets, click **Help**.
2. Select **Help Sheets improve**.

### Legal Issues

To report a legal issue, Google directs users to create a request through Google’s legal support form.

## Workspace Experiments Feature Suggestions: Important Limitations

Google provides several important disclaimers about Workspace Experiments feature suggestions:

- Suggestions **do not represent Google’s views** and should not be attributed to Google.
- Do **not** rely on Workspace Experiments features for:
  - Medical advice
  - Legal advice
  - Financial advice
  - Other professional advice
- Features may suggest **inaccurate or inappropriate information**.
- User feedback helps make Workspace Experiments more helpful and safe.
- Do **not** include personal, confidential, or sensitive information in prompts.
- Google uses Workspace Experiments data and metrics to:
  - Provide products and services
  - Improve products and services
  - Develop machine learning technologies across Google

### Human Review Notice

> Your Workspace Experiments Data may also be read, rated, annotated, and reviewed by human reviewers.

Google further states that when it uses Google-selected input to generate output, it will aggregate and/or pseudonymize that content and output before human review, unless the content is specifically provided as feedback to Google.

## How Workspace Experiments Data in Google Sheets Is Collected

When using suggested table names in Google Sheets, Google uses and stores:

- The generated table name
- Your feedback on the table name

For more details, Google points users to the [Google Workspace Experiments Privacy Notice and Terms for Personal Accounts](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447401).
