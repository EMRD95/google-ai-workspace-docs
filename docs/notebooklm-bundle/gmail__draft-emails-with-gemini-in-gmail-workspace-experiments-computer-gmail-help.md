# Draft emails with Gemini in Gmail (Workspace Experiments) – Help Summary

> **Important:** This feature is part of **Google Workspace Experiments**, a trusted tester program for trying new AI capabilities. To check access, open a new message in Gmail and look for the “Help me write” icon at the bottom of the compose window.  
> [Learn where Workspace Experiments features are available](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13607340).

With **“Help me write”** you can:

- **Generate a new email** from scratch (e.g., birthday invitation, business introduction)
- **Refine existing draft text** – adjust tone, clarity, or turn a rough outline into a formal email

---

## How to Draft an Email

1. Open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/) on your computer.
2. Click **Compose** (top left), or reply to an email / open a draft.
3. In the compose window, enter your message or leave it blank.
4. In the prompt bar at the bottom, type your instructions.
   - If the prompt bar isn’t visible, click the **Help me write** icon ![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/ydRjKg11Kk4r2VOow0WHbjFZn-3feYiMEhWRz86QLOTBt-dcJbW-pL6ARo3C2d6yqXc=h36).
5. Click **Create** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/ueLgY4D6D1QIVr34WsLU2RvYQXnb4GOLGiQy).

## Refining Your Draft

Inside the prompt bar, you can improve the draft by:

- **Entering a new instruction**  
  Examples:  
  - *“Make it more professional.”*  
  - *“Mention the Friday deadline.”*

- **Using one‑click options:**  
  - **Formalize** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/hOzlU2nuGFIEICtzJNPlqfS4BS3oZgLsU4pp)  
  - **Friendly** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/EYhnfD3loaHL2d7TrAHgj9fNTsZojIXsXpL1)  
  - **Shorten** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/lwYUMWRkDG7EDCuq1yElOxcbqXLI0E4Ew36E)

> **Tip:** Switch between different versions of the draft using the **Undo** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/rzcifgL57J7GAvdegxOzByopChjbJ72SXZqs) and **Redo** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/50xDcPiV3SWeZtBe0Lll96mRu6DczOYeM4jA) buttons.

## How to Write Better Prompts (Table)

To get the most relevant, personalised, and helpful drafts, be as specific and detailed as possible.

| What to include in your prompt | Example |
|-------------------------------|---------|
| Mention the recipient or group | *“Write an apology to **my child's teacher** for their absence.”* |
| Clarify the subject and required action | *“**Decline the meeting** on Thursday.”* |
| Use tone words like “formal,” “casual,” etc. | *“Write a **formal** invitation to a business partner.”* |
| Reference a specific email or file for factual accuracy | *“Write a summary of **the Q4 earnings email** I received last week.”* |

## How “Help me write” Personalises Drafts

> **Important:** Personalised tone and style matching works only for drafts in **English**.

When you use “Help me write”, it **automatically pulls details from your recent emails and Drive files** (e.g., flight times, soccer schedules, hotel booking codes) to draft a response that includes relevant facts and matches your tone – without you having to explicitly request it.  
That information is used **only to improve your drafts** when you activate the feature.

- Click **Sources** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/64kv2TsNFyuB9nKep1VDyPew3gIK495exCmJ) to see which emails and documents Gemini used for personalization.

## Giving Feedback on a Draft

Google Workspace with Gemini learns from your feedback.

1. Below the generated text, click **Good suggestion** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/7j7OkRZfOcT69Vz6rrOiSRTPFjmKhseJIom5) or **Bad suggestion** ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/jyZQMHVBqIlMeIxVgJQZSo1iOp4iheXGMHa5).
2. Enter your feedback.
3. Click **Submit**.

> **Important:**  
> - To include screenshots, click the **Help** icon ![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/_Fo0qdrwewWdt_w7aDisG8jfn_2SHouHlv3ikSfWT2JJzRC_k7aK4YoCNQIdKov9HM0=h36) at the top right of Gmail, then **Send feedback to Google**.  
> - For legal issues, [create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

## Turning Off Workspace Experiments

To disable “Help me write” and all other Workspace Experiments features, you must **exit the Workspace Experiments programme**.  
**This action is permanent** – you will lose access to all related features and cannot rejoin.  
[Learn how to exit Workspace Experiments](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13447104#labs-opt-out&exit-labs-docs&exit-labs-gmail).
