# Collaborate with Gemini in Gmail (Workspace Experiments)

## Overview
Gemini in Gmail helps you:

- Summarize an email thread
- Suggest responses to an email thread
- Draft an email
- Find information from previous emails
- Find information from your Google Drive files
- Get information about Google Calendar events
- Create events in Google Calendar

**Feature availability**  
- Requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan ([learn more](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129))
- Available in Google Workspace Experiments (trusted tester program for personal accounts) – check access by opening a Google Slides presentation and looking for the bottom bar ([learn more](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447104))
- Supported in some languages ([language details](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13607340))

## Get Started with Gemini in Gmail

1. Open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/) on your computer.
2. Click **Ask Gemini** in the top-right corner.
3. In the side panel, choose a suggested prompt or type your own.
   - **Use a suggestion**: Select a suggestion (click **More suggestions** for additional options), replace the placeholder text in the prompt box, and press **Enter**.
   - **Write a custom prompt**: Type your request and press **Enter**.
4. (Optional) Clear history via **More options** → **Clear history**.

> **Tip:** Conversation history is lost when you refresh the browser or close/reopen Gmail. Copy any important output into your email to avoid losing it.

## Side Panel Controls (Quick Reference)

| Control | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ask Gemini** | Open/activate the side panel |
| **More options** | Clear recent history, request more suggestions |
| **Expand / Collapse** | Resize the panel |
| **Close** | Close the side panel |
| **Clear history** | Remove all generated text/images not yet inserted |
| **More suggestions** | Load additional prompt suggestions |
| **Copy** | Copy a response to clipboard |
| **Retry with Google Search** | Search the web for your question |
| **View more / View less** | Expand or minimize the response |
| **Good suggestion / Bad suggestion** | Give positive or negative feedback |
| **Gem** | Chat with a premade or custom Gem for repetitive tasks |

## Practical Use Cases & Example Prompts

### Summarize an Email Thread
- With side panel open: click **What’s this email about?** on an email thread (more than 2 replies).
- With side panel closed: click **Summarize this email** at the top of the thread.
- Or use a prompt:
  - *“Summarize this email.”*
  - *“Create a list of action items for me based on this email.”*
  - *“Explain this email to me like I’m 5 years old.”*

### Suggest Reply Options
- Open an email thread, click **Ask Gemini**, then click **Suggest a reply** at the bottom of the side panel.
- To draft a new message:
  - *“Write an announcement email for the launch of Project Clover.”*
  - *“Create a thank you note to a colleague for their work on Project Clover.”*

### Find Information from Previous Emails
Gemini can retrieve details like reservations, flights, packages, etc.
- *“When is my package arriving?”*
- *“What time is my next flight?”*

### Search for Certain Emails
Gemini runs a Gmail search for you.
- *“Show my unread emails.”*
- *“Emails from [person] sent last week.”*
- *“Show unread emails from this week.”*

### Find Information from Your Google Drive Files
Reference documents, notes, etc.
- *“What are the main points of the weekly team meeting notes from my documents?”*
- *“Summarize my document for Project Clover.”*
- Click **Sources** to check which files were used.

### Get Help with Gmail
- *“Where can I edit my email filters?”*
- *“How can I create a label?”*

### Ask Questions from the Web
> **Important:** Include phrases like “Use Google Search” or “Using web search” in your prompt.
- *“Write a paragraph describing the weather today in Mountain View, California using web search.”*
- *“Help me write a paragraph explaining if lightning can strike the same place twice using only information from the web.”*

### Get Information About Your Calendar Events
- Works only with your **primary calendar**.
- *“What’s my first meeting tomorrow?”*
- *“When’s the next time I’ll meet with Lori?”*
- **Tip:** Add `@Calendar` at the beginning of your prompt to specify Calendar.

### Create Calendar Events
- Also limited to the primary calendar.
- *“Schedule a lunch block for tomorrow at noon.”*
- *“Create a weekly run on Fridays at 8 AM.”*
- Based on an open email: *“Add this to my calendar.”*
- **Tip:** Add `@Calendar` to the prompt. If Gemini detects event details in an email, an **Add to calendar** button appears above the email.

### Create Images with Gemini
1. Open Gemini in Gmail.
2. In the side panel, enter a prompt like: *“Create an image of a dog with glasses.”*
3. You can edit the prompt or submit feedback on the generated image.
- Images are for use only within Gmail.
