# Plan your video story with AI in Google Vids (Workspace Experiments)

This help article explains how to use the **“Help me create”** feature in **Google Vids** to generate a full video draft from a text prompt. The feature is part of the invite-only **Google Workspace Experiments** early access program, powered by **Gemini**.

---

## Feature Availability & Limits

- **Access:** Invite-only via *Google Workspace Experiments*.  
  To check availability, open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/) – a “start creating” window appears if you have access.
- **Rollout:** Gradual; new features may not yet be available to all eligible users.
- **Object limits:**  
  - Up to **50 video objects** per video  
  - Up to **50 audio objects** per video  
  *(Limits are subject to change)*

> 📖 [Learn about Google Vids storage, object & version history limits](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/16286470)

## Create a Draft Video with “Help me create”

When you use **“Help me create,”** Vids generates a first draft containing:

- A suggested **outline** with scenes and auto‑generated text
- **Stock media** (videos, images, music)
- A **script**
- A **voiceover**
- **Video placeholders** to guide where you can add your own recordings

### Step‑by‑step

1. **Open a new video**  
   - Go to [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create) or type `vids.new` in your browser’s address bar.
2. Click **Start a new video**.
3. In the pop‑up, click **Plan your video story**.
4. **Enter a prompt** (or select a suggested one).
   - **Add related files**: type `@` and choose a file name from the suggestion list.
   - **Shuffle prompts**: click *Shuffle* to generate new suggestions.
5. Click **Next**.
6. **Edit the generated outline** – each scene appears as a tile:
   - Click a scene’s title to edit its prompt.
   - Delete a tile with the *Delete* icon.
   - Insert a tile below using the *Add* (+) icon.
   - To regenerate the outline entirely, type a new prompt and click **Try again**.
7. **Choose a design**:
   - Hover over a design to preview it.
   - Click a design to select it.
   - Click **Create the draft video**.
8. After creation, you can continue editing and customising.

## Prompt Writing Tips

| Dos | Details |
|-----|---------|
| **Use natural language** | Write like you talk; use full sentences. |
| **Be specific** | Provide context. Attach relevant documents (`@`) and specify audience, purpose, and goals. |
| **Make it a conversation** | If results aren’t perfect, iterate – refine your prompt and use follow‑up prompts. |

> 💡 [Get tips & real‑life use cases for gen AI at work](https://support.google.com/a/users/answer/14506784)

### Sample Prompts

> “**Explain our Q4 product roadmap for executives using @‑file.** This video informs company leadership on upcoming plans and strategy to achieve end of year goals.”

> “**Recap the process for an upcoming office move using @‑file.** This will be shared with all employees impacted by the move to prepare them for moving day.”

> “**Pitch our new product line of environmentally friendly seaweed packaging described in @‑file.** This will be shared with food and beverage manufacturers who are potential customers.”

> “**Remind employees to complete their annual self‑assessments as explained in @‑file.** This process is important so this video will be shared with everyone in the company.”

> “**Pitch video shared to internal customer service teams to use our latest product update using @‑file.** This video should convince potential customers of our product to try new features.”

## Edit or Customise Your AI‑Generated Video

### Scripts & Voiceovers

- Open the **Voiceover side panel** to view/edit the generated script.
- Each scene has its own voiceover and script.
- To change a scene’s narration:
  1. Delete the existing voiceover audio track for that scene.
  2. Add a new voiceover manually or with AI.  
     When generating a voiceover, it uses the content from the Voiceover side panel.

### Media Placeholders

Two types of placeholders appear:

- **Circular placeholders** – contain stock voiceovers; replaceable with your own recordings.
- **Larger placeholders** (with “Add your media” tag) – replaceable with your own media.

**How to replace media:**
Click the placeholder, then use the drop‑down menu to:
- **Upload**
- **Generate an image**
- **Generate a video**
- **My media**
- **Stock media**
- **Recording studio** (to record directly)

To keep the original media, click **Keep video**.
