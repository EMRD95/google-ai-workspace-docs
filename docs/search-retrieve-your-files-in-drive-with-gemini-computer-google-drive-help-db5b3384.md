# Search & Retrieve Files in Google Drive with Gemini — Summary

**Source:** Google Drive Help — “Search & retrieve your files in Drive with Gemini”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/drive/answer/16685111  
**Platform covered:** Computer

---

## Key Requirement

> **Important**: To use Gemini in Drive, you must have an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan.

Gemini in Drive is available only with eligible plans. Google links to additional information about Gemini features and plan eligibility.

---

## What Gemini in Drive Does

Gemini lets you search Google Drive using **natural language** instead of relying on exact file names, keywords, or manual review of search results.

You can ask Gemini to:

- Find specific files or folders
- Locate information across Google Workspace
- Search files shared through other Workspace apps
- Generate summarized answers from file contents via **AI Overviews**
- Continue research in the full-screen **Ask Gemini** workspace

Google notes that users should review its Gemini disclaimers and data usage information to understand how data is used and what to expect from Gemini suggestions.

---

## Search with Natural Language

You can type everyday-language prompts into the Drive search bar to locate files across your Workspace.

### Steps

1. On your computer, go to `drive.google.com`.
2. Enter a natural-language prompt in the search bar.
3. Click inline citations to verify the source files.

### Example Prompts

> `"Show me the slide decks about the project timeline that Deepti shared with me in Chat."`

> `"Show me the PDF Josh emailed me last week about the marketing strategy."`

Gemini can find files not only in Drive but also from shared Workspace contexts such as Chat or Gmail, depending on enabled search scope.

---

## Get Answers Directly in Search with AI Overviews

When you ask a question about your files, Gemini may show an **AI Overview** at the top of Drive search results.

This overview summarizes an answer using information from your files.

### Steps

1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. Enter a question in the search bar.
3. Review the AI Overview at the top of search results.
4. Click inline citations to verify the source files.

### Example Questions

> `"What is the Q3 marketing budget?"`

> `"Summarize the ABC project timeline for this year."`

---

## Continue in the Full-Screen Ask Gemini Workspace

After receiving an AI Overview, you can move into the full-screen **Ask Gemini** workspace for follow-up work.

### What You Can Do There

- Ask follow-up questions
- Compare findings
- Turn research into a saved project

### Steps

1. From the search overview, click **Ask Gemini**.
2. The conversation opens in the full-screen workspace.
3. Your current search context and source files carry over automatically.

> **Tip**: You can provide feedback on the overview by clicking thumbs up or thumbs down.

---

## Control AI Overview Search Scope

You can choose which Google Workspace sources Gemini uses for:

- AI Overviews
- Natural-language file finding

### Steps

1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. In the top search bar, click **AI Overview search**.
3. Check or uncheck sources to include or exclude.

### Available Sources

- Drive files
- Gmail attachments & links
- Chat uploads & links
- Calendar attachments & links

> **Tip:** These settings only apply to Gemini AI Overviews and natural language file finding. They do not affect your standard Drive search results.
