# Use Mind Maps in NotebookLM — Summary

**Source:** [NotebookLM Help](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16212283?hl=en)  
**Topic:** How to generate, use, save/share, and provide feedback on Mind Maps in NotebookLM.

---

## Key Excerpts

> **Important:** The NotebookLM mobile app does not support this feature at this time.

> Mind Maps in NotebookLM visually summarize your uploaded sources, showing main topics and related ideas as a branching diagram.

> **Important:** NotebookLM can be inaccurate; please double check its responses.

---

## What Mind Maps Do

Mind Maps in NotebookLM create a **visual branching diagram** from your uploaded sources. They are designed to help users quickly understand source material by showing:

- Main topics
- Related ideas
- Subtopics
- Connections between concepts

---

## When to Use Mind Maps

Use Mind Maps to:

- **Get a quick overview**  
  Quickly understand the big picture and key themes in your sources.

- **Explore and learn new things easily**  
  Use the map as an entry point into unfamiliar topics before reading details.

- **Connect the dots**  
  Visually identify relationships, links, and associations that may not be obvious.

- **Organize your thoughts**  
  Structure information to improve understanding and retention.

---

## How to Generate a Mind Map

1. Open an existing notebook in [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google.com/).
   - Or create a new notebook and upload sources.
2. In the chat, select the **Mind Map** chip.
3. In the **Studio** panel, find the newly generated Mind Map note.
   - You can revisit the Mind Map at any time.
4. To regenerate a Mind Map:
   - Select **More** / the three-dot menu.
   - Choose **Delete note**.
   - This restarts the generation process.

---

## How to Interact with a Mind Map

Available interactions include:

- **Zoom in or out and scroll**  
  Navigate around the Mind Map and focus on specific areas.

- **Expand or collapse branches**  
  Expand branches to see detailed subtopics or collapse them for a higher-level overview.

- **Select nodes to ask questions**  
  Click a node to ask NotebookLM chat questions about that specific topic.

- **Use top-right controls**  
  Options in the top-right corner let you:
  - Expand or collapse the view
  - Download the Mind Map
  - Exit the Mind Map view

---

## How to Save and Share a Mind Map

There are two sharing options:

### Option 1: Share the Entire Notebook

Generate a Mind Map, then share the notebook with another user.  
They can load the same Mind Map from the **Studio** panel.

### Option 2: Download the Mind Map

Select **Download** inside the Mind Map window, then share the downloaded file.

## Mind Map Usage Limits

Usage limits apply to Mind Map creation and are subject to change:

| Plan | Daily Mind Map Limit |
|---|---:|
| **NotebookLM Standard** | 10/day |
| **NotebookLM in Plus** | 20/day |
| **NotebookLM in Pro** | 100/day |
| **NotebookLM in Ultra — 20 TB Plan** | 500/day |
| **NotebookLM in Ultra — 30 TB Plan** | 1,000/day |

---

## Feedback Options

Users can provide feedback on generated Mind Maps:

- Select **Good content** if the Mind Map was helpful.
- Select **Bad content** if the Mind Map was:
  - Unhelpful
  - Inaccurate
  - Biased
  - Otherwise problematic

When marking content as bad, users are encouraged to describe their experience and provide additional feedback.

---

## Important Limitations and Notes

- Mind Maps are **not currently supported in the NotebookLM mobile app**.
- NotebookLM-generated content may be inaccurate, so users should **double-check responses and outputs**.
- Mind Map usage limits vary by NotebookLM plan and may change over time.
