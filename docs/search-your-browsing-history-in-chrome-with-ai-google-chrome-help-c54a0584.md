# Search Your Browsing History in Chrome with AI — Summary

**Source:** Google Chrome Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/15305774

## Overview

Chrome’s **History search, powered by AI** lets you search your browsing history using everyday language, even if you don’t remember the exact keyword, page title, or website address.

It can return AI-generated “best match” results based on sites you previously visited.

---

## Requirements

To use this feature:

- You must be **located in the US**
- Chrome must be set to **English**

Google links to instructions for changing Chrome languages or translating pages.

---

## Key Excerpts

> You can use everyday language to search your browsing history and find sites you visited. This works even if you don't know an exact keyword or website address.

> When you turn on "History search, powered by AI," in addition to the page title and URL, the page contents of the website you browse at that time are stored locally.

> Only the websites you visit after you turn on this feature can be used by AI-powered history and may appear in your AI best matches.

> As you browse websites, their page contents are saved and encrypted to your device and might affect Chrome’s performance.

> Website content can change over time. Even if the website content has changed since your last visit, AI-powered history search results use the content present at the time when it was browsed.

---

## Manage “History Search, Powered by AI”

### Important Notes

When the feature is enabled:

- Chrome stores more than standard history:
  - **Page title**
  - **URL**
  - **Page contents from websites you browse**
- Page contents are:
  - Stored **locally**
  - Saved and **encrypted to your device**
- Only pages visited **after enabling** the feature can be used for AI-powered history search.
- AI best matches may include those later-visited pages.
- The feature may be unavailable or disabled if you use:
  - A **managed profile**
  - A **managed browser**
- Organizations may disable the feature.
- Saving and encrypting page contents may affect **Chrome performance**.

### Turn the Feature On or Off

1. On your computer, open **Chrome**.
2. At the top right, select **More** → **Settings** → **AI Innovations**.
3. Select **History search, powered by AI**.
4. Turn **History search, powered by AI** on or off.

**Tip:** You can turn off “History search, powered by AI” at any time in Chrome Settings.

## Find AI Best Match Answers from Browsing History

### Important Behavior

AI-powered history search uses the version of page content that existed **when you visited the page**, not necessarily the current version of the website.

This means results may reflect older content if the website has changed since your visit.

---

## How to Search History with AI

1. On your computer, open **Chrome**.
2. At the top right, select **More** → **History** → **History** page.
3. Enter your search.
4. Check the results.

### Search Tips for AI-Powered Results

To increase the chance of receiving AI-powered history results:

- Make your search text or question **precise and detailed**
- Use **2 or more words**
- Describe what you’re searching for in everyday language

### Time Filters

You can filter history results by:

- **Yesterday**
- **Last 7 days**
- **Last 30 days**

---

## Best Matches from Browsing History

AI-powered results appear under:

> “Best matches from your history”

Details:

- This section displays AI-powered best matches.
- It can show **up to 3 best matches**.

---

## Limitations and Result Behavior

- Short, simple searches are matched directly to:
  - Page title
  - URL
- Short/simple searches will **not** produce AI-powered results.
- AI-powered results only use content from sites visited after the feature was enabled.
- Results are based on page contents stored at the time of browsing.

---

## Feedback on AI Best Matches

You can rate AI best match results:

- Select **Thumbs up** if the result was helpful.
- Select **Thumbs down** if the result did not meet your needs.

If you select **Thumbs down**, you can provide additional feedback explaining why the result was not useful.
