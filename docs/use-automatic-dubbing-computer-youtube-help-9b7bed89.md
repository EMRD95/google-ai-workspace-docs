# YouTube Help: Use Automatic Dubbing — Summary

**Source:** <https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15569972>  
**Platform:** Computer / YouTube Studio

---

## Overview

YouTube’s **automatic dubbing** feature generates translated audio tracks for videos to make them more accessible globally.

> “Automatic dubbing generates translated audio tracks in different languages to make your videos more accessible to viewers around the world.”

Videos with automatic dubs are labeled as **“auto-dubbed”** in the video description.

Viewers can:
- Set preferred languages for **audio, titles, and descriptions**
- Switch between the **original audio** and **dubbed audio tracks** on individual videos

For eligible creators, dubs are generated automatically when a new video is uploaded. YouTube may also generate dubs for older, previously published videos over time.

---

## Key Behavior & Publication

- New uploads may receive automatic dubs.
- Dubs are published based on your **channel publication settings**.
- Creators may choose to:
  - Publish dubs automatically
  - Manually review dubs before publication
  - Review all dubs
  - Review only experimental-language dubs
- Dubs may be regenerated over time to improve quality.

---

## Quality, Limitations & Eligibility

Automatic dubbing is improving, but quality can vary by language and source audio.

> “We cannot yet successfully and accurately dub all videos, but we’re continually working on improvements.”

Automatic dubs may contain errors due to:
- Mispronunciations
- Accents or dialects
- Background noise
- Translation challenges with:
  - Proper nouns
  - Idioms
  - Jargon
- Speech recognition issues
- Difficulty matching dubbed voice to the original voice

Some languages support **expressive speech**, which:

> “replicates the original audio's pitch and intonation to produce more natural-sounding dubs.”

---

## Reasons a Video May Be Ineligible

A video may not qualify for automatic dubbing for these reasons:

- **Video Length:** The video exceeds **120 minutes**
- **Audio Content:**
  - No speech
  - Only music
  - Very little spoken content
  - Original audio is in an unsupported language
- **Source Language Detection:** YouTube cannot automatically detect the source language
- **Speech Pacing:** Speech is too fast, which would create an unlistenable sped-up dub
- **Content ID Claims:** The video contains copyrighted content

---

## Best Practices

To improve dub accuracy:

- Set the **original video language** correctly when uploading.
- Review dubs before publishing if quality control is important.
- Ask someone who speaks the target language to review dubs if needed.

Important limitation:

> “You cannot edit automatic dubs.”

Creators can only preview, review, publish, unpublish, or delete dubs.

---

## Access to Automatic Dubbing

Automatic dubbing is **enabled by default** for eligible creators.

If it is not enabled, creators may use early access by enabling it in:

**YouTube Studio → Settings → Channel → Advanced settings**

Then click or check **Enable / Allow automatic dubbing**, depending on availability.

## Turn Off Automatic Dubbing for Your Channel

To disable automatic dubbing:

1. Sign in to [YouTube Studio](https://studio.youtube.com/).
2. Go to **Settings → Channel → Advanced settings**.
3. Uncheck **Allow automatic dubbing**.
4. Click **Save**.

---

## Turn On Automatic Dubbing for Your Channel

If you previously disabled automatic dubbing:

1. Sign in to [YouTube Studio](https://studio.youtube.com/).
2. Go to **Settings → Channel → Advanced settings**.
3. Check **Allow automatic dubbing**.
4. Optional: Check **Manually review dubs before publishing**.
5. Choose one:
   - **Review dubs in all languages**
   - **Review dubs in experimental languages only**
6. Click **Save**.
