# Summary: How AI-powered features use your data in Google Maps

**Source:** Google Maps Help — “How AI-powered features use your data in Google Maps”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/maps/answer/16833938

---

## Key Excerpts

> **Important**: We’re currently updating settings for Google Search services, which include Search, Maps, Shopping, Flights, Hotels, Translate, and News. These are rolling out gradually over the next few months. If the settings in this article do not match what you see, history and personalized recommendations for Search services are still controlled by [Web & App Activity](https://myactivity.google.com/personalization/settings/waa).

## Overview

Google Maps’ AI-powered features may use **location data** and **account activity** to provide more relevant, helpful, and personalized responses. Some related settings are currently being updated across Google Search services, including **Search, Maps, Shopping, Flights, Hotels, Translate, and News**.

If the new settings are not visible yet, Google says history and personalization for Search services are still controlled through **Web & App Activity**.

---

## AI-powered features and location data

Google Maps AI-powered features may use or collect location data in specific cases:

- **AI-powered answers**
  - Store the answers you receive.
  - Those stored answers may include your location.

- **Lens in Maps**
  - May store your location.

- **Gemini in Navigation**
  - May store your location.

### Exception

When you click on a place and ask a question about it:

- **Maps does not use or collect your location** for that interaction.

---

## How Maps personalizes AI-powered responses

If **Personalized Recommendations** are enabled, Google Maps may personalize AI-powered responses using account information, including:

- Places you searched for.
- Places you got directions to, **if Maps history is being saved**.
- Saved lists or labels added to places.
- Reviews, ratings, and photos you posted for places.
- Activity from your **Search history**, used to tailor responses to your tastes and preferences.

Relevant controls/resources:

- [Personalized Recommendations](https://www.google.com/search-personalization/)
- [Maps history](https://support.google.com/maps/answer/3137804)
- [Search history](https://myactivity.google.com/search-services/history/search)
- [Web & App Activity](https://myactivity.google.com/personalization/settings/waa)

## Use of data to improve AI features

Google states that:

- Questions typed into Google Maps are **not used to train AI models**.
- However, Google **does analyze those questions** to improve products and services.

## Verifying AI-powered answers

Google warns that AI-powered answers may sometimes be wrong and recommends users double-check important information.

### Recommended verification steps

- Check important information in more than one place.
- Click links to AI sources.
- Try other Google Search results.
- Ask multiple versions of the same question.
- Request different opinions or more details.
- Send feedback if something seems incorrect.

Related guide:

- [Evaluate info you find with Google](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/12003459)

## Giving feedback on AI-powered answers

Users can submit feedback if an AI-powered answer is:

- Unsafe
- Not helpful
- Inaccurate
- Otherwise problematic

### Feedback steps

1. Below the answer:
   - If helpful, tap **Thumbs-up**.
   - If it needs improvement, tap **Thumbs-down**.
2. If you tap **Thumbs-down**, select a reason.
3. Enter any additional feedback.
4. Tap **Submit**.

Feedback is used to improve the AI-powered experience.
