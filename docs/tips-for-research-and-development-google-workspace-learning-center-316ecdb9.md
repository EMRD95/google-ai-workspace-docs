# Tips for Research & Development — Google Workspace Learning Center

**Source:** Google Workspace Learning Center  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/a/users/answer/9283136  
**Purpose:** Practical Google Workspace workflows for research and development teams to store files, track product work, collaborate, collect feedback, communicate, manage schedules, and use generative AI.

---

## Overview

This guide explains how R&D and product teams can use Google Workspace apps to streamline product development across distributed teams. Core recommendations include:

- Use **Google Drive** or **shared drives** as a secure central file repository.
- Use **Google Sheets** to track product development tasks and ownership.
- Use **Google Sites** to consolidate product information in one internal website.
- Use **Google Slides**, **Gemini in Slides**, and **Google Vids** to present and visualize new ideas.
- Use **Google Forms** and **Sheets** to gather and analyze customer feedback.
- Use **Google Docs** for real-time collaborative documentation.
- Use **Google Meet** for remote team meetings and supplier/vendor collaboration.
- Use **Google Groups** to communicate with teams through one email address.
- Use **Google Calendar** to coordinate meetings, deadlines, and shared team schedules.
- Use **Google Workspace with Gemini**, where available, for AI-assisted writing, visualization, organization, and connection.

## 1. Store Files in One Location

**Use:** Google Drive or shared drives  
**Goal:** Securely store, access, and collaborate on product-related files.

### What to store centrally

- Product designs
- Quality guidelines
- Key Performance Indicators, or **KPIs**
- Mock-ups
- Confidential product files
- Feedback and approval materials

### Benefits

- Everyone accesses the latest files from one secure location.
- Drive shows which files have changed after feedback or approvals.
- Internal teams and external clients can securely access files remotely on any device.
- Shared drive files remain with the team, even if an individual contributor leaves.

### Add files to Drive

If using the latest Chrome browser or Mozilla Firefox:

1. Drag files or folders from your computer into a Drive folder.

Or:

1. Click **New**.
2. Select **File upload** or **Folder upload**.
3. Choose the file or folder to upload.

### Add files to a shared drive

**Requirement:** At least **Contributor** access.

1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. On the left, click **Shared drives**.
3. Double-click a shared drive.
4. At the top left, click **New**.
5. Choose the type of file or folder to create or upload.

**Important:** Files added to a shared drive are owned by the team, not an individual user.

## 2. Track Product Development

**Use:** Google Sheets  
**Goal:** Maintain a shared, real-time task list for product development.

### Recommended use cases

- Track sourcing activities for new products.
- Create checklists for reviewing products and processes.
- Assign tasks to team members.
- Monitor development across the product lifecycle.

### Benefits

- Global teams can update the same spreadsheet in real time.
- Reduces reliance on email updates.
- Makes it easier to assign, distribute, and oversee tasks.

### Create a checklist in Sheets

1. In [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com/), click **New**.
2. Select **Google Sheets**.
3. Choose **Blank spreadsheet**.
4. Click **Untitled spreadsheet** and enter a title for the project plan.
5. Add columns, such as:
   - Tasks
   - Task owners
   - Due dates
   - Status
   - Comments
6. Add content to track project tasks.
