# Google Workspace with Gemini FAQ for Business — Summary

**Source:** Google Workspace Help

**Last updated:** **2026-06-11 UTC**

## Overview

This FAQ explains what the **Gemini app** includes, who can use it, how Google Workspace with Gemini handles data protection and security, compliance considerations such as HIPAA, availability limitations, usage limits, and feature restrictions.

---

## Key Excerpts

> **"Gemini Apps" are also sometimes referred to as the "Gemini app" or "Gemini."**

> **Users with a Google Workspace with Gemini license get enterprise-grade data protections when they use Gemini app. Submissions aren't used to train models and are never reviewed by humans.**

> **Your interactions with Google Workspace with Gemini stay within your organization. Google Workspace with Gemini stores any prompts or generated content alongside your Workspace content and does not share them outside your organization.**

> **Your existing Google Workspace protections are automatically applied.**

> **Your content is not used for any other customers. None of your content is used for model training outside of your domain without permission.**

> **Users must be 18 years of age or older to use the add-on. Admins are responsible for assigning the add-on to users 18 years of age or older.**

> **No. Google Workspace with Gemini is unable to act on email and files that are encrypted with customer-controlled keys.**

> **To protect the service and ensure Google Workspace with Gemini is available to other customers, Google may reduce your access to AI features if we reasonably determine that you've exceeded usage limitations.**

## What Is the Gemini App?

The **Gemini app** includes several Gemini experiences:

- **Gemini web app:** [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com/)
- **Gemini mobile app** on Android and iOS
- **Gemini in Chrome**
- **Gemini on Mac**

The FAQ notes that the terms **“Gemini Apps,” “Gemini app,” and “Gemini”** may be used interchangeably.

For users with a **work or school Google Account**, Gemini Apps may be subject to different data handling terms than consumer accounts.

## Who Can Use the Gemini App?

The Gemini app is available as a **core service** with **most editions of Google Workspace**.

## Data Protection and Privacy

Google states that commercial Google Workspace customers using Google Workspace with Gemini receive the same robust security and data protection standards as other Workspace services, with additional protections for:

- Businesses
- Education customers
- Public-sector customers

### Key Data Protection Commitments

Google Workspace with Gemini provides the following protections:

- **Enterprise-grade data protections** for licensed users using the Gemini app.
- **Submissions are not used to train models**.
- **Submissions are never reviewed by humans**.
- Prompts and generated content **stay within the organization**.
- Gemini stores prompts and generated content **alongside Workspace content**.
- Content is **not shared outside the organization**.
- Existing Workspace protections apply automatically, including:
  - Data-region policies
  - Data Loss Prevention policies
  - Existing organizational controls
  - Existing data handling practices
- Customer content is **not used for model training outside the domain without permission**.

More information is available in the **Generative AI in Google Workspace Privacy Hub**.

## Protection Against Generative AI Security Threats

Google describes protections against emerging gen AI threats such as:

- Hidden suspicious URLs
- Malicious prompts embedded in email or documents
- Prompt injection attempts
- Content designed to make AI tools behave in unintended or harmful ways

When users ask Gemini to summarize content such as emails or documents:

1. Gemini analyzes the content for threats before taking action.
2. If Gemini identifies a threat:
   - It notifies the user.
   - It excludes that content from its response.

This protection is positioned as similar to defenses against known threats like malware and phishing.
