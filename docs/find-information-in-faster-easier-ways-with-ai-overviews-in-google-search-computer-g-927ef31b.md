## What AI Overviews Are

**AI Overviews** are AI-generated summaries that may appear in Google Search results when Google determines generative AI can be especially helpful.

They are designed to:

- Provide a quick snapshot of key information.
- Pull together information from a range of sources.
- Include links so users can explore further.
- Help users understand topics faster and with less manual searching.

Google is gradually expanding AI Overviews to more users, languages, and regions.

## Can AI Overviews Be Turned Off?

No. Google states that AI Overviews are a **core Google Search feature**, similar to knowledge panels.

However, users can choose the **Web filter** after performing a search.

The **Web filter** shows only text-based links and removes features such as:

- AI Overviews
- Knowledge panels
- Other rich Search features

## How AI Overviews Work

AI Overviews use **generative AI**, which learns patterns and structures from training data and produces new responses.

Google warns that AI Overviews may:

- Be inaccurate
- Be offensive
- Contain mistakes
- Need independent verification

### How to Double-Check AI Overview Responses

Google recommends:

1. **Check important information in more than one place**
   - Click supporting web links.
   - Review other Google Search results.
   - Evaluate sources carefully.

2. **Ask multiple versions of your question**
   - Rephrase the query.
   - Ask for different opinions.
   - Ask for more detail.

3. **Send feedback to Google**
   - Feedback helps improve AI Overviews for everyone.

## Continue from AI Overview into AI Mode

Users can transition from an AI Overview into a deeper conversation in **AI Mode**.

This feature is available on **mobile devices** in all regions and languages where both AI Overviews and AI Mode are supported.

### How It Works

1. Select **Show more** at the bottom of the AI Overview.
2. Type a follow-up question in the **Ask anything** bar.
3. Continue the conversation in **AI Mode**.

The context from the original search carries over, so users do not need to start again.
