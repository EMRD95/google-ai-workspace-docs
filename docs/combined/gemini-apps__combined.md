# Create & Manage AI Mini-Apps / Custom Workflows as **Gems from Google Labs**

**Source:** [Gemini Apps Help](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/16802014?hl=en)  
**Product area:** Gemini web app, Gems, Google Labs / Opal

---

## Overview

**Gems from Google Labs** are custom AI mini-apps or workflows you can create, edit, remix, and share in the Gemini web app. They are powered by **Opal**, a Google Labs experiment, and let users build app-like AI workflows using natural language.

> You can create, edit, and share custom workflows bundled into AI mini-apps. These AI mini-apps are called Gems from Google Labs in the Gemini web app. They’re powered by Opal, a Google Labs experiment.

Users can:

- Create AI mini-apps from a text description.
- Edit app workflows by describing desired changes.
- Remix premade AI mini-apps from Google Labs.
- Share AI mini-apps with links, specific people, or public access.
- Use multiple AI models for workflows, including text, image, video, and music models.

> **Important:** This is an experimental feature and part of ongoing work to enhance Gemini experiences. This experiment will help us gather your feedback and refine the experience further.

---

## Requirements & Availability

To use Gems from Google Labs, you must:

- Be **18 or over**.
- Sign in to the Gemini web app with a **personal Google Account**.
- Use the same personal Google Account when signing in to **Opal**.

### Current limitations

> **Important:**
>
> - For now, this feature is available in the Gemini web app and in English only.
> - For now, you can create and edit Gems from Google Labs on a computer only.
> - For now, this feature isn’t available in the Gemini mobile app, Gemini in Google Messages, or Gemini in Chrome.

Additional limitation:

- Not available to users with **work or school Google Accounts**.
- Classic Gems **cannot currently be converted** into Gems from Google Labs.

---

## Create an AI Mini-App

You create AI mini-apps from the **Gem manager** in Gemini.

### Steps

1. On your computer, go to [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com/).
2. On the left, click **Gems**.
3. Next to **“My Gems from Labs,”** click **New Gem**.
   - First-time users must review disclosures and follow on-screen instructions to sign in to **Opal**.
   - Use the same personal Google Account as Gemini.
4. In the text box at the bottom, describe the AI mini-app or workflow you want to create.
   - You can also click an example prompt for inspiration.
5. Click **Submit**.
   - Complex apps may take a few minutes to create and run.
   - Keep the chat open until the app is ready.
   - You can open another browser window or tab to start another chat.
6. When ready, Gemini shows:
   - The app workflow as steps.
   - A preview of the app.
7. Review the steps and click **Start app** to try it.
8. To add or change steps, describe your edits in the text box below the workflow.

### Tips

- You can remix premade AI mini-apps and customize them.
- Classic Gems cannot currently be converted into Gems from Google Labs.

# Use Apps Connected to Gemini with a Work or School Google Account — Summary

## Overview

Gemini Apps can connect with other services, called **apps**, to provide more useful responses for work or school Google Accounts.

With connected apps, Gemini can help you:

- Find information from **Gmail**
- Summarize documents from **Google Drive**
- Find and summarize information from **Google Chat** in English
- Add and show tasks from **Google Tasks**
- Create and show notes and lists in **Google Keep**
- Create, find, and edit events in **Google Calendar**
- Attach a **GitHub repository** to better understand a codebase and debug issues

> **Important:** If you’re using Gemini Apps with a personal account, see Google’s separate guidance on using Connected Apps in Gemini.

---

## Requirements

To connect apps to Gemini with a work or school Google Account, all of the following must be true:

- You have a **qualifying edition of Google Workspace**.
- Your **Keep Activity** setting is turned on.
  - Only your organization’s Google Workspace administrator can manage this setting.
- Your Google Workspace administrator has enabled the ability to connect apps to Gemini.
  - Workspace administrators can manage access to apps in Gemini through the admin settings.

---

## How to Use Apps in Gemini

> **Important:** Gemini Apps can hallucinate responses or provide outdated information, such as referencing an older email when a newer one exists. To verify information, click and review the sources listed after Gemini’s response.

### Steps

1. Go to [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com/).
   - If connecting to a Google Workspace app, make sure you’re signed in to the same Google Account you use with Google Workspace.

2. In the text box, enter:

   ```text
   @
   ```

   Then select the app you want to use.

   - Tip: Enter `@`, select **Google Drive**, and reference Workspace content using keywords from the content.

3. Ask Gemini to get information from the selected app or service.

4. Click **Submit**.

If the app has been enabled by your Workspace administrator, Gemini Apps will automatically use it.

### You need to explicitly mention the app

For a new conversation, you must explicitly ask Gemini to use the app or service.

Example workflow:

1. Enter:

   ```text
   @
   ```

2. Select **Google Drive**.
3. Use keywords from the content you want Gemini to reference in your prompt.

You do **not** need to repeat this for follow-up questions in the same conversation.

---

### The app may not be enabled by your organization

For work or school accounts, app access must be enabled by your Google Workspace administrator.

If your prompt includes multiple actions requiring separate apps or services, and one or more of those services are not enabled, **none of the related actions will be completed**.

Example:

- You ask Gemini to:
  - Create a calendar event
  - Create a reminder for that event
- But the **Tasks app** is not enabled

Result:

- The calendar event will **not** be added.
- The reminder will **not** be created.

Workspace administrators can manage access to apps in Gemini through admin controls.

# Use Deep Research in Gemini Apps — Summary

**Source:** Google Gemini Apps Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/15719111?hl=en  
**Platform covered:** Computer

---

## Overview

Deep Research in Gemini Apps lets users conduct **in-depth, real-time research** on almost any topic. Gemini uses **Google Search by default**, but users can choose or add other sources, including:

- Google Search
- Personal Gmail
- Google Drive
- Uploaded files/images
- NotebookLM notebooks

> “You can conduct in-depth and real-time research on almost any subject with Deep Research in Gemini Apps.”

Deep Research generates a research plan first, then creates a report after the user starts the research.

---

## Requirements

To use Deep Research, you must:

- **Be 18 or over**
- **Be signed in to the Gemini app**

---

## Models & Usage Limits

### Available Models

- **All users** can use **Thinking** for Deep Research reports.
- **Google AI Pro** and **Google AI Ultra** users can generate reports using **Pro** for higher quality.

> “Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra users can generate reports using Pro for even higher quality.”

### Limits

Gemini Apps limits:

- The number of **daily research requests**
- The number of research requests that can run **at the same time**

If you are close to your limit, Gemini notifies you how many research requests remain for the day.

**Google AI Pro** and **Google AI Ultra** users receive higher limits.

---

## How to Start a Deep Research Report on Computer

1. Go to [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com/).
2. In the text box, click **Add Files** → **Deep Research**.
3. Optional: To upload files or an image with your prompt, click **Add Files** → **Files**.
4. Optional: To choose research sources, click **Sources** and select sources such as Gmail or Drive.
   - Gmail, Drive, and other Google services are available only if the **Google Workspace app is connected to Gemini Apps**.
   - **Google Search is included by default**.
   - To limit research to selected sources only, **deselect Google Search**.
5. Enter a question or prompt describing what you want researched.
6. Click **Submit**.
   - Gemini creates a research plan.
   - To revise it first, click **Edit plan**.
7. Click **Start research**.
8. When the report is ready, click **Open**.

---

## Report Generation Time & Notifications

Deep Research reports usually take:

- **About 5–10 minutes**
- Longer for more complex reports

> “It usually takes about 5-10 minutes to generate the report since Gemini analyzes many sources.”

You can leave the chat while the report is generating. Gemini will notify you when it is ready:

- **Web app:** Notification appears next to the completed report’s chat thread.
- **Mobile app:** Device notification may appear, depending on notification and lock-screen settings.

---

## Visuals & Animations in Reports

**Availability:** Google AI Ultra plan only.

> **Important:** For now, this feature isn’t available if you include any Google Workspace services, like Gmail and Drive, as a source.

With a **Google AI Ultra** plan, Deep Research reports may include visuals and animations to make complex information easier to understand.

Possible visuals include:

- Charts
- Diagrams
- Interactive simulators

## Context Window

| Access Level | Context Size |
|---|---|
| Standard / Pro levels | **32 thousand** |
| Higher tiers | **1 million** |

### What a Context Window Means

A context window is Gemini’s “reading capacity” when processing prompts, files, and conversations.

> “The context window is like the reading capacity. It determines how much content can be understood together at one time.”

### Why a Larger Context Window Matters

A larger context window allows Gemini to process more content at once.

Example:

> “With a 1M token context window, Gemini can understand up to 1,500 pages of text or 30,000 lines of code.”

If the context window is exceeded, responses may fail to account for all uploaded or provided content, especially in large files with details spread throughout.

> “You can create and save Gems to customize responses in Gemini Apps to your specific needs.”

Gems can be created and managed in the Gemini web app and used across supported Gemini surfaces.

To create and use Gems:

- You must be signed in to Gemini Apps.
- With a **personal Google Account**, you must be:
  - **13 or older**, or
  - the applicable minimum age in your country.
- You must follow Google’s Terms of Service and Prohibited Use Policy.

**Important notice from Google:**

> **Important:** When you create or chat with a Gem, remember that you agreed to Google's Terms of Service, including the Prohibited Use Policy. Be sure not to violate others' rights. To add files from Google Drive:

- **Keep Activity** must be turned on.
- Google Workspace must be connected to Gemini Apps.
- If Google Workspace isn’t connected, Gemini will prompt you to connect it.

> If you add a file from your Drive, Gemini will use the most recent version of the file. If you make changes to the file, these changes will be reflected in your Gem.

By default, Gem responses may cite uploaded knowledge files.

To turn citations off:

> If you don’t want the Gem’s responses to include citations to your knowledge files, select **Disable knowledge citations**. This also disables citations for files uploaded in your Gem chats.

Additional tips:

- You may need to scroll down to find the **Knowledge** section.
