## What Generative AI Is

Generative AI is a **machine learning model** designed to create new content by recognizing and applying patterns learned during training.

### Important Clarifications

- Generative AI is **not human**.
- It **does not think for itself**.
- It **does not feel emotions**.
- It is effective at **finding and reproducing patterns**.

### How AI Use Has Evolved

Previously, AI was mainly used to:

- Understand information
- Recommend information

Now, generative AI can also help create new content, including:

- **Images**
- **Music**
- **Code**
- Other creative or informational outputs

---

## How Machine Learning Models Are Trained

Machine learning models learn through **observation and pattern matching**, a process called **training**.

### Example: Learning What a Sneaker Is

To understand what a sneaker is, a model may be trained on **millions of photos of sneakers**. Over time, it recognizes common features such as:

- Footwear worn by humans
- Laces
- Soles
- Logos

### How Training Enables Generation

A trained model can:

1. Take an input like “Generate an image of sneakers with a goat charm.”
2. Connect what it has learned about **sneakers**, **goats**, and **charms**.
3. Generate a new image, even if it has never seen that exact image before.

## How Large Language Models Power Generative AI

Generative AI and **Large Language Models**, or **LLMs**, are part of the same broader technology area.

### Difference Between Generative AI and LLMs

- **Generative AI** can be trained on many types of data.
- **LLMs** use **words** as their main source of training data.

### How LLM-Based Experiences Work

Experiences powered by LLMs, such as **Gemini** and **AI Overviews**, predict likely next words based on:

- The user’s prompt
- The text generated so far
- Patterns learned during training

This flexibility allows LLMs to produce creative responses.

### Example

If prompted to complete:

> “Harry [blank],”

an LLM might predict:

- “Styles”
- “Potter”

depending on context and learned patterns.

## How to Use Generative AI

Google emphasizes that generative AI should be used as a **starting point or assistant**, not as a full replacement for human creativity, judgment, or responsibility.

### 3 Suggested Uses

#### 1. Brainstorm Creative Ideas

Example:

- Get help writing a prequel to your favorite movie.

#### 2. Ask Unusual or Complex Questions

Example:

- “Which came first, the chicken or the egg?”

#### 3. Get an Extra Boost of Help

Examples:

- Suggest a title for a story you wrote.
- Help identify the species of an animal or insect in an image.

### Responsible Use

Users should use generative AI responsibly and review Google’s:

- [Generative AI Prohibited Use Policy](https://policies.google.com/terms/generative-ai/use-policy)

## AI Can and Will Make Mistakes

Generative AI is **experimental** and still developing. Google warns that it can produce incorrect, misleading, or misunderstood responses.

---

## Common Types of Generative AI Errors

### 1. Hallucinations: Making Things Up

Generative AI may invent answers. This is called a **hallucination**.

> **“When generative AI invents an answer, it's called a hallucination.”**

### Why Hallucinations Happen

Unlike Google Search, which retrieves information from the web, LLMs do **not** gather information directly. Instead, they predict likely next words based on:

- User input
- Training patterns
- Previously generated text
