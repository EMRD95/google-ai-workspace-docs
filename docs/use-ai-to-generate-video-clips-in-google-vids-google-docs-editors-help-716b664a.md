# AI Video Clip Generation in Google Vids – Comprehensive Summary

## Feature Availability & Requirements

- **Eligible subscription**: Requires an eligible Google Workspace subscription with Gemini features.  
  [Learn about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13952129)
- **Supported languages**: [See Gemini language support](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/14925782)

## Generated Video Specifications

- **Frame rate**: 24 fps
- **Resolution**: 720p
- **Aspect ratio**:
  - **Landscape (Widescreen)**: 16:9
  - **Portrait (Vertical)**: 9:16
- **Monthly generation limit**: Up to 50 videos per month; resets at 12 AM PT on the 1st day of each month.  
  For Google AI Pro / AI Ultra with family sharing, the limit is shared.  
  [Learn more about generation limits](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/15609411)

## 1. Generate a Video Clip from Scratch (Text Prompt)

**Important**: Generated clips are only available in landscape or portrait orientation.

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc) on your computer.
2. Select **Veo 3.1** from the start menu – or, in an existing video, click the Veo icon on the right.
3. In the side panel dropdown, choose **Create from Scratch**.
4. (Optional) Choose **Landscape** or **Portrait** aspect ratio.
5. Enter a **text prompt** with details: subject, location, action, camera, lighting, dialogue, sounds, tone.  
   **Example prompts**:
   - “A cat walking down a fashion week catwalk wearing designer clothes with electronic music in the background.”
   - “A dog driving a race car in Italy at sunset with loud engine roars.”
6. (Optional) Set a clip aspect ratio different from your canvas using the option at the bottom of the prompt window.
7. Click **Generate**.
8. To refine, edit the text prompt and regenerate.
9. Hover over the generated clip and click **Insert** to add it to your Vid.
10. The clip appears on its own timeline track at the bottom – you can control it there.

> **Tip**: Always insert the clip into your Vid to keep it accessible for later editing within the session.

## 2. Animate an Image into an 8‑Second Video Clip

**Important**: Only **Landscape (16:9)** or **Portrait (9:16)** images are accepted. If the uploaded image has a different ratio, Vids automatically adds **black bars**.

### Steps

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc).
2. Select **Veo 3.1** (as above).
3. In the side panel dropdown, choose **Animate an image**.
4. (Optional) Select **Landscape** or **Portrait**.
5. Click **Add image** and upload a **.jpg** or **.png** image.
   - **Best practice**: Use a high‑quality, sharp, clear source image – its composition and style set the visual direction.
6. Enter a motion‑focused description (do **not** re‑describe the scene). Describe how elements move or how the camera moves.  
   **Example** (from an elephant image): “The elephant moves around naturally.”
7. Click **Create**. The clip appears in the “Generate video” panel when ready.
8. To adjust, click **Edit prompt** and refine.
9. Hover and click **Insert** to add the clip to your Vid; it gets its own timeline track.

## 3. Generate a Video with Reference Images (Ingredients)

**Important**: This feature only supports **Landscape (16:9)** aspect ratio.

- Upload **up to 3 reference images** to define subjects, styles, and locations.
- Re‑use the same references across multiple clips to maintain **consistent characters, design, and objects**.

**Example**: Provide images of a woman, a yellow mug, and an office; prompt:  
“Create a video of a woman drinking coffee from this mug, in this office.”

### Steps

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc) and select **Veo 3.1**.
2. From the side panel dropdown, choose **Create from scratch**.
3. Click **Ingredients** (image icon).
4. Select up to 3 images.
5. (Optional) Click **Avatar** to use an avatar (including its voice) as an ingredient.  
   **Note**: Avatar ingredients are limited to English and certain account types (see below).
6. Write a prompt that explains how to incorporate the ingredients – reference the objects, styles, or people.
7. Click **Generate**.

#### Avatar Availability (for Ingredients)
Avatars can only be used as ingredients in **English** and with these accounts:
- Business Starter, Business Standard, Business Plus
- Enterprise Starter, Enterprise Standard, Enterprise Plus
- Essentials Starter, Enterprise Essentials, Enterprise Essentials Plus
- Nonprofits
- Education Plus and Education Teaching and Learning add‑on
- Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra (**USA only**)
- Google AI Pro for Education add‑on
