# Connect the Google Workspace App to Gemini Apps — Summary

**Source:** Google Gemini Apps Help — “Connect the Google Workspace app to Gemini Apps”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/15229592

---

## Overview

Gemini Apps can connect to your Google Workspace data to help you work across Google services. Once connected, Gemini can retrieve, summarize, organize, and act on information from supported Workspace apps.

With the **Google Workspace app**, Gemini Apps can help you:

- Summarize, answer questions, and find information from services like **Gmail, Docs, and Drive**
- Add, edit, and retrieve **Google Tasks** and reminders
- Create and retrieve **Google Keep** notes and lists
- Create and manage **Google Calendar** events

> **Tip:** You can also get personalized Gemini experiences based on your content in Gmail when you connect the Google Workspace app.

---

## Requirements

To connect Google Workspace to Gemini Apps, you must:

- **Be signed in to the Gemini app**
  - Use the **same account** you use with Google Workspace.
- **Have Keep Activity turned on**
  - Gemini can’t connect to Google Workspace if this setting is off.

### Important Notes

- Only **some Google Workspace services** are currently available in **Live chats**.
- Gemini may provide incorrect or outdated information.

> **Important:** Gemini Apps can hallucinate responses or provide outdated information, like from an older email when there’s a more recent one. To check the information, select and review the sources listed after the response.

---

## How to Use the Google Workspace App in Gemini Apps

1. Sign in to Gemini with the same account you use for Google Workspace.
2. Ask Gemini to get information from Google Workspace.
3. If Google Workspace is not yet connected, Gemini will offer the option to connect it.
4. Follow the on-screen instructions.

You can disconnect the Google Workspace app later from **Connected Apps settings** at:  
https://gemini.google.com/apps

### Prompting Tip

Do **not** paste the URL of a Google document or email into your prompt. Instead:

- Use keywords from the content
- Specify the relevant service or content type
- Use `@` to select a Google Workspace service

> **Tip:** Don’t paste the URL of a document or email in your prompt. To reference content in Google Workspace, use keywords from the content and specify the service or content type. To specify a Google Workspace service, enter `@` and select the service.

## Example Prompts

### Find Information

- “What dates did Erik propose in the email about the Grand Canyon hiking trip?”
- “Find the most recent lease agreement from my Drive and check how much the security deposit was”

### Summarize a Document

- “Find my resume titled June 2023 from my drive and summarize it to a short paragraph personal statement”

### Simplify and Organize Information

- “Find the doc from Ashley about the neighborhood clean-up project and summarize the proposal for me in 5 bullets”

### Capture Tasks in Google Tasks

- “Help me plan a camping trip and create a packing list for me.”
- Follow-up example: “Create a reminder to bring those things in Tasks.”

### Save Ideas and Notes to Google Keep

- “Recommend some places to visit in Kyoto and save them to Keep.”
- “Find me a recipe for apple pie and save the ingredients to Keep.”

### Check Schedule and Manage Calendar Events

- “What’s on my calendar today?”
- “Check the email that Alice sent about our trip to Tahoe this weekend, and add it to my calendar.”

## Why You Might Not Be Able to Connect Google Workspace

### Work or School Account Restrictions

For work and school accounts, a Google Workspace administrator must enable app connections first.

> **For work and school accounts, the Google Workspace administrator must first enable the ability to connect with apps.**

### Gmail Smart Features May Be Off

Gemini Apps require **Smart features in other Google products** to be enabled in Gmail settings.

If you turn this setting on:

1. Reload the Gemini app.
2. Try connecting Google Workspace again.

### You Must Mention Workspace Explicitly in New Chats

For every new conversation, explicitly ask Gemini to get or add something from your personal Google Workspace.

You can mention:

- **Gmail** or email
- **Docs** or documents
- **Drive** or PDFs
- **Calendar** or events
- **Tasks** or to-dos
- **Keep** or notes

You do **not** need to repeat this in follow-up questions within the same chat.

Example:

- **First prompt:** “Summarize my recent emails from Harper elementary school”
- **Next prompt:** “When is pajama day?”
