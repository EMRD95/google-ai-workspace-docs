# Search your Chrome history with Gemini in Chrome

You can find pages you've visited before in Chrome by asking Gemini in Chrome. You don’t need to keep tabs open to save them for later, or remember the exact site and when you visited it. Just describe what you're searching for using everyday language, like "what’s that chicken parmesan recipe I found last week?" Gemini in Chrome will search your Chrome history to help you rediscover that page and resume what you were doing.

## What you need

This feature is only available if you:

- Opted in to Gemini in Chrome. [Learn how to get Gemini in Chrome](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/16283624).
- Updated to the latest version of Chrome. [Learn how to update Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95414).
- Signed in to Chrome with a personal Google Account. [Learn how to sign in to Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/185277).

  - This feature isn’t available in Incognito mode. It’s also not available if you sign in to Chrome with a work or school Google Account.
- Synced your Chrome history. [Learn how to sync your history in Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/185277).

**Important:** For now, this feature is not available in Live chats with Gemini in Chrome.

## Find pages you visited in Chrome

1. On your computer, open Chrome.
2. At the top of your browser, click Ask Gemini ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/DOo8pSHet4pMKNJy8JqVa95JUbHs0U63uHqg).
3. Ask Gemini in Chrome to find a page you previously visited.
   - Use everyday language to describe the page you’re trying to find.

[Learn how to check or delete your Chrome browsing history](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95589).

### Examples

- Show me the article I was reading this morning about the stock market.
- What was that place with vegan pizza I was looking at?
- What was that hotel I was looking at in Palm Springs last week?
- What were the steps for that DIY guide to fixing a leaky faucet?
- Show me those ladies trousers I was looking at.

## Gemini in Chrome can’t find the page

If you asked Gemini in Chrome to find a page you previously visited but it couldn’t find it:

1. Make sure you’re signed in to the same Google Account you used when you visited the page. This should also be the same Google Account that you use with Gemini.
2. If you use Chrome on multiple devices, check if your Chrome history is synced. [Learn how to sync your history in Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/165139).
