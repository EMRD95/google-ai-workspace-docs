# Get Started with Gemini in Google Drive — Summary

**Source:** Google Drive Help — “Get started with Gemini in Google Drive”

## Key Requirement

> **Important**: To use Gemini in Drive, you must have an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan.

Users need an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan to access Gemini features in Google Drive.

---

## What Gemini in Google Drive Can Do

Gemini in Drive is designed to improve productivity by helping users interact with and learn from their files in new ways.

You can use Gemini in Drive to:

- **Perform complex research and discuss topics synthesized from multiple files**
- **Share research sources with others using Drive projects**
- **Quickly search and retrieve files using AI Overviews**
- **Create content, images, and Audio Overviews**
- **Summarize files and folders**
- **Use Gems with Gemini in Drive**
- **Organize files**

Google also recommends reviewing its information about **disclaimers and data usage** to understand how data is used and what to expect from Gemini suggestions.

## How to Navigate Gemini in Google Drive on a Computer

When using Drive on the web, Gemini opens in a **full-screen immersive workspace**.

### Steps to Open Gemini in Drive

1. On your computer, go to [drive.google.com](http://drive.google.com/).
2. At the top right, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. Use the full-screen interface to conduct your research.

### Main Interface Areas

The Gemini workspace includes:

1. **Prompt box**  
   - Located at the bottom.
   - Used to type your request.

2. **Menu**  
   - Located at the top left.
   - Provides access to:
     - Conversation history
     - Gems
     - Saved Projects

3. **Sources Panel**  
   - Located on the left side.
   - Lets you:
     - Manage explicit file sources
     - Toggle broad search settings for other Google Workspace apps

## Giving Feedback on Gemini Output

Google notes that:

> Google Workspace with Gemini is constantly learning and may not be able to support your request.

If Gemini generates an inaccurate or unsafe suggestion, users can submit feedback.

### Feedback Steps

1. On your computer, at the bottom right of the generated text, click:
   - **Good suggestion**, or
   - **Bad suggestion**
2. If you click **Bad suggestion**, you can:
   - Select the issue you found
   - Enter additional feedback
3. Click **Submit**

### General Feature Feedback

To provide general feedback about the feature:

- Go to **Help**
- Select **Send feedback to Google**

### Legal Issues

To report a legal issue, users should create a legal request through Google’s legal support page.
