# Create Background Images with Gemini in Google Meet — Summary

## Key Excerpts & Important Notes

> **Important:**
>
> - This feature requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan.
> - Sometimes, you may not be able to generate a background because Smart feature control is disabled.

> On Google Meet, you can use the "Generate a background" prompt to create background images using artificial intelligence. For example, you can ask Google Meet to create an illustration of a magical forest.

> Your self-view shows a mirrored or inverted background but other meeting participants can find it in a correct orientation.

## Feature Overview

Google Meet allows eligible users to generate custom meeting backgrounds using Gemini AI through the **“Generate a background”** prompt.

You can describe the type of background you want, and Meet will generate suggested images you can apply as your meeting background.

### Example prompts

- `"Luxurious living room interior"`
- `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`
- `"A cozy coffee shop with flowers"`

### Availability

- Available on **desktop**
- Available on **Android**
- The page is specifically for **Computer**, but notes Android support as well
- Also available through **Google Workspace Experiments**, Google’s early access testing program

### Requirements

To use the feature, you need:

- An eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan
- Smart features enabled, if required by your account settings

If background generation is unavailable, it may be because **Smart feature control is disabled**.

## How to Create an AI Background Before a Meeting

1. On a computer, go to [meet.google.com](https://meet.google.com/).
2. Select a meeting.
3. At the bottom-right of your self-view, click:
   - **Apply visual effects**
   - Then **Generate a background**
4. In the **“Generate a background”** setup, enter a prompt.

   Example prompts:

   - `"Luxurious living room interior"`
   - `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`

5. Click **Create** to generate a suggested background image.
6. Optional: Edit the prompt after generating:
   1. At the top of the right-side panel, click the prompt.
   2. Edit the prompt.
   3. Click **Create a new sample**.
   4. Click **Apply**.
7. To use a generated image, click the image.
8. To exit the setup, click **Close**.

## How to Create an AI Background During a Meeting

1. On a computer, go to [meet.google.com](https://meet.google.com/).
2. Join a meeting.
3. At the bottom of the meeting window, click:
   - **More options**
   - Then **Apply visual effects**
   - Then **Generate a background**
4. In the **“Generate a background”** setup, enter a prompt.

   Example prompts:

   - `"Luxurious living room interior"`
   - `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`

5. Click **Create** to generate suggested background images.
6. Optional: Edit the prompt after generating:
   1. At the top of the right-side panel, click the prompt.
   2. Edit the prompt.
   3. Click **Create a new sample**.
   4. Click **Apply**.
7. To use a generated image as your meeting background, click it.
8. To leave the background generation setup, click **Close**.

## Prompt-Writing Guidance

For better results, Google recommends including:

- A **style** or **mood**
- A **setting**
- Objects or details in the environment

### Good prompt example

> “A cozy coffee shop with flowers.”

This prompt works well because it includes:

- Mood: cozy
- Setting: coffee shop
- Objects: flowers

## Giving Feedback on Generated Backgrounds

Google notes that Gemini may not always support a request or may produce inaccurate or unsafe suggestions.

If a generated image is inaccurate, inappropriate, or unsafe, you can submit feedback.

### How to submit feedback

1. Hover over the generated image.
2. At the bottom of the image, click either:
   - **Good suggestion**
   - **Bad suggestion**
3. If you select **Bad suggestion**, you can:
   - Choose the issue you found
   - Enter additional feedback
4. Click **Submit**.

### Legal issues

To report a legal issue, Google directs users to create a legal request through Google’s legal support process.
