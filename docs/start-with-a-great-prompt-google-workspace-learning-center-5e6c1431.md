# Start with a great prompt — Google Workspace Learning Center Summary

**Source:** Google Workspace Learning Center  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/a/users/answer/14590328  
**Topic:** Getting started with Gemini prompts in Google Workspace apps

---

## Core Message

Prompts are instructions you give to **Gemini** to generate content. The quality of Gemini’s output depends heavily on the quality of the prompt. Strong prompts use **natural language**, provide **clear instructions**, and include enough **context or specific details** for Gemini to produce a useful response.

> “Prompts tell Gemini what to do. And the better the prompt, the better the result.”

Gemini prompts can be used across Google Workspace apps, including:

- **Google Docs**
- **Google Sheets**
- **Google Slides**
- **Gmail**
- **Google Meet**
- **Google Vids**
- **gemini.google.com**

## Important Usage Warnings Before Using Gemini

Google provides several cautions for using Gemini in Google Workspace:

- Gemini feature suggestions **do not represent Google’s views** and should not be attributed to Google.
- Do **not** rely on Gemini features for:
  - Medical advice
  - Legal advice
  - Financial advice
  - Other professional advice
- Gemini may suggest **inaccurate or inappropriate information**.
- User feedback helps improve Gemini’s helpfulness and safety.
- Enterprise users can submit feedback about generative AI features, but:
  - Feedback should not contain **personal, sensitive, or confidential information**
  - Feedback may be **read by humans**

## What Is a Prompt?

A prompt is the instruction or request you give Gemini.

> “A prompt tells Gemini what you want it to generate.”

Good prompts should:

- Use **natural language**
- Sound like instructions to a **friend or colleague**
- Include relevant **context**
- Provide **specific details**
- Clearly state the desired result

> “Good prompts use natural language, as if you were giving instructions to a friend or colleague. And the best prompts provide the context, or the specific details, Gemini needs to provide a great response.”

# How to Write Prompts in Google Workspace Apps

---

## Write a Prompt in Google Docs

Use Gemini in Docs through **Help me write**.

### Steps

1. On your computer, open a [Google Docs file](https://docs.google.com/document/).
2. Click where you want to write in the document.
3. On the left, click **Help me write**.
4. Enter a prompt with your instructions.
5. Click **Create**.
6. Review Gemini’s response.
7. Optional: Click **Refine** and choose how Gemini should change the content.
8. When finished, click **Insert**.

### Example Prompt

```text
Imagine you are the lead on a project. Write an update to your executive leadership team on [Project X]. Include the current status for each business function and next steps. Use a formal, brief tone.
```

## Write a Prompt in Google Sheets

Use Gemini in Sheets through the **Ask Gemini** sidebar.

### Steps

1. On your computer, open a new spreadsheet or tab in [Google Sheets](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/u/0/).
2. The **Ask Gemini** sidebar opens automatically.
3. Enter a prompt.
4. Click **Create**.
5. Optional: Click **Refine** and choose how Gemini should change the content.
6. When finished, in the preview, click **Insert**.

### Example Prompt

```text
Imagine you are preparing for a customer meeting with a new client. Create a framework for researching the client's company in order to create a sales prospect.
```

### Related Help

- [Learn more at the Docs Editors Help Center](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/14356410)

## Write a Prompt in Google Slides

Use Gemini in Slides to create images.

### Steps

1. On your computer, open a presentation in [Google Slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation/).
2. Click the slide where you want to add the image.
3. Go to **Insert → Image → Create image with Gemini AI**.
4. In the right-side panel, enter a prompt.
5. Optional: Click **Add a style** to customize the image.
6. Click **Create** to see several suggested images.
7. Optional:
   - **Edit your prompt:** Click the prompt at the top of the right panel, revise it, then click **Create**.
   - **See more suggested images:** Click **View more**.
8. When finished, click **Insert**.

### Prompting Tip for Better Images

For better image-generation results, include details such as:

- Subject
- Setting
- Distance to subject
- Materials
- Background

### Example Prompt

```text
A close up of a boat made out of wood on a lake surrounded by trees at sunrise.
```
