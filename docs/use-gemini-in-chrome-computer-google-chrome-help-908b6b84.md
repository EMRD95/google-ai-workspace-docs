# Use Gemini in Chrome — Google Chrome Help Summary

**Source:** <https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/16283624>  
**Platform covered:** Computer

## Overview

Gemini in Chrome provides AI assistance directly in the browser. It can help users get key takeaways, clarify concepts, find answers, compare information across pages, and more.

> “To provide the most relevant responses, Gemini in Chrome uses content from your current browser tab.”

On computers, users can also share **up to 10 open tabs** with Gemini.

---

## Availability & Requirements

> **Important**: Gemini in Chrome is not available to all users at this time. We’re gradually releasing Gemini in Chrome, so it might not be available to you just yet.

To use Gemini in Chrome on a computer, you must:

- Be **18 or over**
- Be in a **supported region**
- Use a **Chromebook Plus, Mac, or Windows computer**
- Use the **latest version of Chrome**
- Be **signed in to Chrome**
  - Not available in **Incognito mode**
- Set your device to a **supported language**

For work or school Google Accounts:

- Access must be enabled by your administrator.
- Gemini Apps usage depends on organizational settings.

## How to Chat with Gemini in Chrome

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. At the top of the browser, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. If using it for the first time, follow the opt-in instructions.
4. Enter your question or request in the text box.

### Tab context behavior

By default:

- Gemini uses content from your **current tab** to respond.
- Tabs opened from a chat or shared tab are also shared with Gemini.
- In Gemini Live, the current tab is shared by default.

You can add tabs directly in a prompt by typing:

```text
@
```

Then select the tab.

You can share **up to 10 open tabs**.

**Tip:** After opting in, Gemini in Chrome is added to the **Mac menu bar** or **Windows system tray**, and keyboard shortcuts become available.

## Managing Gemini Chats

Inside Gemini in Chrome, you can:

### Go Live

Click **Go Live** at the bottom to talk while browsing.

### Share or stop sharing tabs

Use **Update tabs** in the text box to add or remove shared tabs.

- Shared tabs show a **check mark**.
- Tabs being used by Gemini show a **glowing underline**.

### Pop out or dock Gemini

Use:

- **Pop-out chat**
- **Dock to original tab**

### Start a new chat

Click **Start new chat**.

If it is not visible:

1. Click **More options**
2. Select **Start new chat**

When starting a new chat, your current tab is shared by default unless you disable:

> **Share current tab by default**

### Switch Gemini models

Click the model name in the text box, then choose another model.

### Find recent chats

Click **More options** at the top of Gemini in Chrome.

## Managing Tabs Shared with Gemini

When Gemini opens, your current tab’s page content is shared by default.

### Stop sharing a tab

1. In Gemini in Chrome, above the text box, click **Show shared tabs**.
2. Next to the tab, click **Remove tab**.

When a tab is no longer shared:

- Its glowing underline disappears.

### Share additional tabs

You can share up to **10 recent open tabs** across browser windows.

Steps:

1. Click **Ask Gemini** at the top of Chrome.
2. In the text box, click **Add tabs**.
3. Select the tabs to add.

Effects:

- Shared tabs show a **check mark**.
- Gemini uses shared page content to respond.
- Tabs Gemini is using show a **glowing underline**.

**Tip:** You can also search for and add tabs by entering:

```text
@
```

## Workspace Webpages

When sharing a Google Workspace page, such as:

```text
docs.google.com
```

Gemini may directly access your Workspace account to respond about the shared Workspace webpage.

This allows Gemini to refer to an entire Google Doc rather than only what is visible in the browser tab.

To use more personal Workspace content, connect the Workspace app to Gemini Apps.

## Gemini in Chrome Settings

Settings path:

1. Open Chrome.
2. Click **More** → **Settings**.
3. Go to **AI innovations** → **Gemini in Chrome**.
4. If the menu is hidden, click **Menu** first.

---

## Hide or Show Gemini Icon

Under **Preferences**, you can turn these settings on or off:

- **Show Gemini at the top of the browser**
- **Show Gemini in Mac menu bar and turn on keyboard shortcut** on Mac
- **Show Gemini in system tray and turn on keyboard shortcut** on Windows

**Tip:** You can also right-click the top of Chrome and select:

- **Unpin Gemini**
- **Pin Gemini**
