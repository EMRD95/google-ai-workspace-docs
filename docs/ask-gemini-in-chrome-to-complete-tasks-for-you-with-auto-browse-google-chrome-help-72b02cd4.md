# Ask Gemini in Chrome to Complete Tasks with Auto Browse — Summary

Source: Google Chrome Help — “Ask Gemini in Chrome to complete tasks for you with auto browse”

## Overview

**Auto browse** lets you ask **Gemini in Chrome** to complete multi-step tasks on the web for you. Gemini may use your preferences, instructions, previously shared details, page context, and personal information to provide more useful results.

Example tasks include:

- Comparing products, searching for deals, and adding items to your cart
- Finding and booking travel accommodations based on criteria
- Making restaurant reservations and scheduling appointments
- Performing administrative web tasks and other online chores

> **Important:** Gemini in Chrome can use a broad range of sites on the web to complete tasks for you. This is true even if you haven't connected the related apps to Gemini Apps.

---

## Requirements

To use auto browse on a computer, you must:

- Be **18 or older**
- Be located in the **United States**
- Be eligible for and opted in to **Gemini in Chrome**
- Use the **latest version of Chrome**
- Set your device language to **English**
- Have **Google AI Pro** or **Google AI Ultra** on a personal account
- Sign in to Chrome with a **personal Google Account**
  - Not available in **Incognito mode**
  - Not available with a **school Google Account**
- Set Chrome **Safe Browsing** to either:
  - **Enhanced protection**, or
  - **Standard protection**

### Availability notes

> **Important:**
>
> - We’re gradually releasing auto browse in Gemini in Chrome, so it might not be available to you just yet.
> - For now, this feature is not available in Live chats or Gemini in Chrome on iPhone devices.

## How to Ask Gemini in Chrome to Complete a Task

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. Click **Ask Gemini** at the top of the browser.
3. In the text box, describe the task you want Gemini to complete.
4. Click **Submit**.
5. Review Gemini’s proposed plan.
   - Confirm it understood:
     - Your prompt
     - The page context
     - Your personal info
   - Gemini chooses which sites to use and **may share your personal info with those sites**.
6. If the plan looks correct, click **Start Task**.
7. If needed, you may be asked to:
   - Review and confirm sensitive steps
   - Take over steps you must complete personally
   - Give Gemini permission to sign in to a site for you

While Gemini is performing a task:

- An **auto browse icon** appears on the tab Gemini is using.
- If Gemini needs your attention, a notification appears at the top of the browser.

---

## Task Controls

### Go to the task tab

- Next to the task in chat, click **Switch tabs**
- If a task needs attention, click **Check your task** at the top of the browser

### Take over a task

You can take over at any time.

Steps:

1. Go to the tab Gemini is using.
2. Click **Take over task**.
3. Complete the steps yourself.
4. To return control to Gemini, click **Resume** or **Give back task** in the Gemini chat.

### Resume a task

- Click **Resume** or **Give back task**

### Stop a task

- Click **Stop** in Gemini in Chrome, or
- Close the task tab

## Signing In to Websites

With your permission, Gemini in Chrome can use **Google Password Manager** to sign in to websites for you.

Key privacy point:

> Google Password Manager does not share your passwords with Gemini in Chrome.

You can remove sign-in permission at any time.

### Remove Gemini’s permission to sign in to a site

1. Open **Chrome**.
2. Go to **More** → **Passwords and autofill** → **Google Password Manager**.
3. Click **Settings** on the left.
4. Under **“Gemini can sign in for you,”** review allowed sites.
5. Click **Delete** next to a site to remove permission.

---

## Turn Auto Browse On or Off

To change whether Gemini in Chrome can browse for you:

1. Open **Chrome**.
2. Go to **More** → **Settings**.
3. In Settings, click **AI innovations** → **Gemini in Chrome**.
   - If the menu is hidden, click **Menu** first.
4. Under **Permissions**, turn **Let Gemini browse for you** on or off.

## Usage Limits

Gemini in Chrome has limits on:

- The number of multi-step task requests per day
- The number of tasks Gemini can perform at the same time

Daily limits:

| Plan | Multi-step task requests |
|---|---:|
| **Google AI Pro** | Up to **20 requests per day** |
| **Google AI Ultra** | Up to **200 requests per day** |

When you reach a limit, Gemini in Chrome notifies you.
