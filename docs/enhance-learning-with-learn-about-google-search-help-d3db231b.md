# Enhance Learning with Learn About — Google Search Help Summary

**Source:** Google Search Help — “Enhance learning with Learn About”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/15662709

---

## Overview

**Learn About** is a generative AI learning companion from Google designed to support interactive learning. It combines **Google Search**, **Gemini**, and teaching principles to help users explore topics, study, and engage with educational content.

> “Learn About is a generative AI learning companion that brings Google Search, Gemini, and teaching principles to provide interactive learning experiences.”

Users can get started by typing a prompt, selecting a topic, or uploading images/materials.

---

## What Learn About Can Do

Learn About can help users:

- Explore topics through:
  - Detailed explanations
  - Images
  - Videos
  - Learning cards such as:
    - “common misconceptions”
    - “stop and think”
    - “test your knowledge”
- Get clear help for studies and assignments.
- Use quizzes and interactive guides to improve learning.
- Use **text, images, or uploaded materials** to get assistance.
- Discover new topics aligned with personal goals and interests.

> “With Learn About, you can expand your knowledge by creating an engaging learning journey that fits your needs and preferences.”

## Availability

Learn About is currently available:

- To users **signed in to a Google Account**
- In the **United States**
- For users **13 or older**
- **Only in English**

Access it here:  
[https://learning.google.com/experiments/learn-about](https://learning.google.com/experiments/learn-about)

## How Learn About Works

Learn About uses **generative AI**, which learns patterns and structures from training data and uses them to create new content.

Google notes that generative AI is still evolving and may make mistakes.

> “While exciting, this technology is rapidly evolving and improving, and may provide inaccurate or offensive information that doesn’t represent Google’s views. Learn About can and will make mistakes.”

Users are encouraged to:

- Think critically about responses.
- Verify important information with multiple sources.
- Learn how to evaluate information found through Google.

Related help pages:

- [Learn more about generative AI and its limitations](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/13954172)
- [Learn more about how to evaluate info you find with Google](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/12003459?hl=en)

### Important Caution

> **Tip:** Do not rely on responses from Learn About for medical, legal, financial, or other professional advice.

Google also links to a video:

- [Watch this before using generative AI — YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=unPKJJjQP0A)

## How to Get Started

1. Go to [https://learning.google.com/experiments/learn-about](https://learning.google.com/experiments/learn-about).
2. Enter a question or prompt in the text box at the top.
   - You can also upload an image.
3. Click **Submit** or press **Return** / **Enter** on your keyboard.

**Tip:** To start a new chat, click **New chat** at the top left.

---

## Highlight Words or Phrases for More Information

Users can highlight any word or phrase in Learn About content to get additional help.

Steps:

1. Highlight a word, words, or phrase.
2. Choose an option from the dropdown menu:

Available options include:

- **"What is this?":** Get a definition or explanation.
- **"See a picture":** Open an image related to the topic or word.
- **"Search the web":** Find more information.
- **"Ask about this…":** Ask a more detailed question about something shown in the conversation.

This feature is designed to help users understand unfamiliar terms or explore related ideas more deeply.

## Giving Feedback on Learn About Responses

Google encourages users to provide feedback to improve Learn About’s helpfulness, accuracy, and safety.

> “Your feedback helps make Learn About more helpful, accurate, and safe.”

### How to Rate a Response

Below each Learn About response, users can select:

- **Good response** — if the response was helpful, accurate, and/or complete.
- **Bad response** — if the response was:
  - Unhelpful
  - Inaccurate
  - Unsafe
  - Offensive
  - Biased
  - Broken
  - Otherwise problematic

### Add More Details

Users can optionally provide additional written feedback in a text box.

Example:

- If marking a response as bad because it is incorrect, selecting **“Not Factually Correct”** helps Google understand the issue.

### Submit Feedback

Click **Submit** to send the feedback.

Users can also request content removal under Google policies or applicable laws:  
[Request removal of content](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420)
