# Overview

**Gemini Live in Chrome** lets you have a voice conversation with Gemini while browsing web pages on a computer. You can use it to:

- Ask questions about a topic.
- Brainstorm ideas.
- Get help finding information on a page.
- Interrupt Gemini mid-response to add details or change the topic.
- Share page content from browser tabs so Gemini can respond with page context.
- Use voice commands to navigate web pages, where available.

# How to Go Live with Gemini in Chrome

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. At the top of the browser, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. In the text box, click **Go Live**.
4. The first time you use it, follow the on-screen setup instructions for **Gemini Live**.
5. Start talking.

You can interrupt Gemini while it’s responding if you want to:

- Add more context.
- Change the topic.
- Ask a follow-up question.

Google also links to more information about **how Google works with Gemini Live data**.

# Managing a Live Chat

While using Gemini in Chrome, you can control the Live chat in several ways.

## Turn Closed Captions On or Off

At the top of Gemini Live, click:

- **Turn on live captions**, or
- **Turn off live captions**

## Continue the Chat with Text

At the bottom, click:

- **Switch to text mode**

This lets you continue the conversation by typing instead of speaking.

## Start a New Chat

At the top, click:

- **More options** → **Start New Chat**

## Change Gemini’s Voice

At the top, click:

- **More options** → **Select Voice**

# Managing Gemini in Chrome Permissions

To use Gemini Live properly, Gemini in Chrome may need permissions for:

- Page content in Chrome.
- Your microphone.
- Other optional capabilities.

## How to Manage Permissions

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. At the top, click **More** → **Settings**.
3. In the Settings menu, click:
   - **AI innovations** → **Gemini in Chrome**
   - If the menu is hidden, first click **Menu**.
4. Under **Permissions**, turn the following permissions on or off:
   - **Precise location**
   - **Microphone**
   - **Share current tab by default**
   - **Let Gemini browse for you**

# Managing Tabs Shared with Gemini Live

Gemini Live can use shared tab content to provide context-aware responses.

## Share or Pause Sharing Your Current Tab

If you share your current tab, Gemini can use the page content to answer questions.

### Important Behavior

- If you navigate to a different page in the same shared tab, **Gemini uses the new page**.
- You can pause sharing at any time.
- When Gemini is using a page:
  - The page’s edges glow.
  - The tab shows a glowing underline.
- When sharing stops:
  - The glowing page edges disappear.
  - The glowing tab underline disappears.

## Share Your Current Tab

In Gemini Live, click:

- **Share current tab**

This shares the page content from the current tab with Gemini.

## Pause Sharing Your Current Tab

In Gemini Live, click:

- **Current tab**, or
- **Pause sharing current tab**

---

# Sharing Other Open Tabs

Gemini Live can use more than just your current tab.

## Key Limit

You can share:

- Your current tab, plus
- **Up to 10 recent open tabs** across browser windows.

## Manage Shared Tabs

1. In Gemini Live, click **Add tabs**.
   - If you already shared tabs, click the **Manage tabs** button, which shows the number of currently shared tabs.
2. Next to the tab you want to share, click **Add tab**.
3. To remove a tab, click **Remove tab**.

## Remove All Shared Tabs

On the **Manage tabs** button, click:

- **Stop sharing tabs**
