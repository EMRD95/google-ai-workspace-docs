# Generate Questions with Gemini in Google Forms (Workspace Experiments) — Summary

**Source:** Google Docs Editors Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/docs/answer/16362810?hl=en

## Key Excerpts

> **Important:** This article is referring to Google Workspace Experiments, which is an invite only program for users to try new AI features. The Workspace Experiments program may not be available in your country.

> To check if you have access to Workspace Experiments program features, open a new form on Google Forms and look for “Help me create a form” at the top.

> This feature is currently available on desktop and in English.

> You can use Gemini to quickly add relevant new questions to your form based on your current content.

> **Important:** You can use forms and quizzes in English only with a single section.

> Because feedback may be human readable, do not submit data that contains personal, confidential, or sensitive information.

---

## Overview

Google Forms users in the **Google Workspace Experiments** invite-only program can use **Gemini** to generate suggested questions for a form based on the form’s existing content.

### Availability

- Available only through **Google Workspace Experiments**
- Invite-only program
- May not be available in all countries
- Currently available:
  - On **desktop**
  - In **English**
- Forms/quizzes must be:
  - In **English**
  - Limited to a **single section**

### How to Check Access

Open a new form in **Google Forms** and look for:

> “Help me create a form”

at the top of the form.

---

## Add Questions to Your Form with Gemini

To generate suggested questions:

1. On your computer, open a form in [Google Forms](https://google.com/forms).
2. Click **Suggest questions**.
   - You must already have **at least 2 questions** in the form for Gemini to generate suggestions.
3. Select the generated questions you want to include.
   - Optional: Click **Retry** to generate a new set of questions.
4. When the suggestions look good, click **Insert suggestions**.

---

## Give Feedback on Suggested Questions

Google notes that Gemini-powered Workspace features are still learning and may not always support a request correctly.

You can provide feedback if a suggestion is:

- Inaccurate
- Unsafe
- Inappropriate
- Otherwise problematic

### Feedback Steps

1. Below the generated text, click:
   - **Good suggestion**
   - or **Bad suggestion**
2. If you choose **Bad suggestion**, select the issue and optionally provide more details.
3. Click **Submit**.

### General Feature Feedback

To provide general feedback on the feature:

- At the bottom of the page, click **Help & feedback**
- Then click **Help forms improve**

### Legal Issues

To report a legal issue, [create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

## Important Safety, Accuracy, and Privacy Notes

Google provides several warnings about Workspace Experiments suggestions:

- Suggestions **do not represent Google’s views** and should not be attributed to Google.
- Do **not** rely on Workspace Experiments features for:
  - Medical advice
  - Legal advice
  - Financial advice
  - Other professional advice
- Suggestions may be:
  - Inaccurate
  - Inappropriate
- User feedback helps improve safety and usefulness.
- Do **not** include personal, confidential, or sensitive information in prompts or feedback.

## Workspace Experiments Data Use

Google uses Workspace Experiments data and metrics to:

- Provide products and services
- Improve products and services
- Develop products, services, and machine learning technologies across Google

### Human Review

Workspace Experiments data may be:

- Read
- Rated
- Annotated
- Reviewed by human reviewers

Google states that when it uses Google-selected input to generate output, it will **aggregate and/or pseudonymize** that content and the resulting output before human review, unless the content is specifically submitted as feedback.

## Data Collected in Google Forms

When you generate a question in Forms, Google uses and stores:

- The generated question
- Form content referenced to generate questions
- Your feedback on the generated question

For details, review the [Google Workspace Experiments Privacy Notice and Terms for Personal Accounts](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447401).
