# Learn how to schedule events in Gmail

In Gmail, you can schedule Google Calendar events and negotiate times to meet directly from a message. There are different options available in Gmail based on your account type.

| **Feature** | **Description** | **Availability** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Suggest times to meet with Gemini in Gmail](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/16865189) | Use Gemini in Gmail to coordinate and set up meetings with one or more people. If you have an appointment schedule, you can also insert a booking page.<br> This feature supports 1:1 and group meetings. | - Workspace Business Standard and Plus<br>- Workspace Enterprise Starter, Standard, and Plus<br>- Workspace Frontline Plus<br>- Google AI Pro for Education<br>- Google AI Pro and Ultra<br>- Gemini Business and Enterprise - Legacy |
| [Create an event from a Gmail message](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13469141) | Use Gmail to convert an existing email thread into a Calendar invite. When you create the event, it automatically copies the email subject and body into the event details, and adds all email recipients as invitees. | All Gmail users |
| [Insert an event or booking page in a Gmail message](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/16865501) | When you draft a reply or a new email, you can create an event and insert a summary of it in your email draft. If you have an appointment schedule, you can also insert a booking page. | - Workspace Business Starter<br>- Personal Gmail accounts |
| [Offer times to meet in a Gmail message](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/16864784) | You can offer a list of times to meet from your calendar to your recipient. The event isn’t created until the recipient selects a time. Recipients get times in their own time zone on your booking page.<br>This feature supports 1:1 meetings only. | - Workspace Business Starter<br>- Personal Gmail accounts |
