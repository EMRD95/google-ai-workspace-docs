# Search with Google Lens in Chrome — Summary

**Source:** Google Chrome Help — “Search with Google Lens in Chrome”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/15086890  
**Platform covered:** Computer

## Overview

Google Lens in Chrome lets you search or ask questions about content on a webpage. You can use it to identify objects, find products, search images, highlight text, select areas of a page, or ask questions about the current page.

> “You can search anything you find with Google Lens in Chrome. For example, you can select an image of a shirt on a page to know where to buy it or take a photo of an animal to identify it.”

---

## Requirement

**Important:** You must have **Google set as your default search engine**.

> **Important:** [Set Google as your default search engine](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95426).

---

## Search a Page with Google Lens

### Steps

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. Go to a website.
3. At the top right, select **More** → **Search this tab with Google Lens**.
   - Alternatively, right-click on a page and select **Search this tab with Google Lens**.
4. Use Google Lens to interact with the page.

---

## What You Can Do with Google Lens

### Ask Questions About the Current Page

You can ask questions about the page you’re viewing.

> **Ask questions about the content of the page you’re on:** At the top right of the screen, in the search box or on the side panel, enter your question. The answers to your question are provided by Google Lens based on the content of the page.

### Select Anything to Search

You can search by clicking, highlighting text, or dragging over part of the page.

> **Select anything to search:** On a webpage, click, highlight text, or select and drag anywhere on the page.

### Ask Follow-Up Questions

To continue the conversation or refine your search:

> To ask a follow-up question, enter it in the "Ask a follow up" bar near the bottom of the side panel. This leads you to AI Mode.

Google provides a related help article for AI Mode:  
[Learn how to get AI-powered responses with AI mode in Google Search](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/16011537)

### AI Overviews

Search results may include AI-generated summaries.

> You may find AI Overviews in your Google Search results.

Related article:  
[Learn about AI Overviews](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/14901683)

---

## Closing the Google Lens Side Panel

You can close the side panel in any of these ways:

- Select anywhere in the live page area.
- Press **Esc**.
- In the side panel, select **Close**.

---

## Search Results Behavior

- Search results appear in the **side panel**.
- To open a result separately, select the search result to open it in a **new tab**.

> Search results display in the side panel. To open a result in a new tab, select the search result.

---

## Pin Google Lens to the Toolbar

You can pin **Search this tab with Google Lens** to the Chrome toolbar for quicker access.

> In the toolbar, you can pin "Search this tab with Google Lens."

Related article:  
[Learn how to pin and unpin a specific side panel](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/13156494#pinthesearchbar)

# Additional Ways to Search with Google Lens

## From the Address Bar

### Steps

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. Go to a website.
3. Select the **address bar** → **Ask Google about this page**.

### Add Tabs or Files to a Question

To include more context:

> To add tabs or files to your question, select **AI Mode**.

### Address Bar Tips

- **“Ask Google about this page”** may appear by default on certain pages.
- To check AI Mode history:
  - From the address bar, select **AI Mode** → **View your AI Mode history**.

---

## From an Image

### Steps

1. Right-click the image.
2. Select **Search with Google Lens**.

You can also right-click outside an image and choose **Search with Google Lens** from the menu.

# How Page Data Is Processed

Google explains how data is handled when you use Lens in Chrome.

## When Searching Webpage Content

- A **screenshot of the page** and **page data** are sent to Google.
- Google does **not save data beyond temporary storage** needed to process the specific query.
- The page or image is **not used for training** as a result of the Lens query.

> When you use Lens to search webpage content in Chrome, a screenshot of the page and page data will be sent to Google. Google doesn't save any data beyond temporary storage for processing the specific query. Neither the page nor image is used for training as a result of this.

## When Selecting Part of a Page

- The selected page content is sent to **Google Search**.
- You can manage or delete Search activity in **My Google Activity**.

> When you select part of the page, the selection is sent to Google Search. To manage or delete your Search activity, go to [My Google Activity](https://myactivity.google.com/myactivity).
