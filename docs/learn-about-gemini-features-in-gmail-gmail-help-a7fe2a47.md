# Learn about Gemini Features in Gmail — Summary

**Source:** [Gmail Help: Learn about Gemini features in Gmail](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/16831098?hl=en)

## Core Message

Gemini features in Gmail are designed to make Gmail a **“personal, proactive inbox assistant.”** Availability depends on:

- **Account type**
- **Paid plan eligibility**
- **Country/region**
- **Language**
- **Platform**
- **Local regulations and Google’s AI principles**

Google says availability will expand gradually to more countries and territories.

---

## Key Excerpts

> “Gemini features turn Gmail into your personal, proactive inbox assistant.”

> “We’ll gradually expand to more countries and territories in a way that's consistent with local regulations and our AI principles.”

### Important Availability Notes

> “Access to Gemini features in Gmail varies based on account type. Users with eligible Google AI or Google Workspace plans can access some features globally. However, availability is currently limited to the US for users with personal Gmail accounts without a paid plan.”

> “If a feature is available in multiple languages for Google Workspace or Google AI plans, a personal account in the US can use the feature in those supported languages.”

## Footnotes and Special Conditions

### Mobile Availability for AI Overview in Gmail Search

\* **Google AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra only.**

This means iOS and Android support for **AI Overview in Gmail search** applies only to Google AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra users.

### Gemini Side Panel Availability Change

\*\* Google notes that it is changing how AI appears in Gmail:

> “We're working to make it easier to use AI directly in Gmail. As such, Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra subscribers in the US may no longer see Ask Gemini in Gmail or be able to open the Gemini in Gmail side panel. New in-line AI experiences in Gmail, such as AI Overviews and ‘Help me write,’ are available directly in the product.”

**Implication:** Some Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers in the US may lose access to the **Ask Gemini** side panel in Gmail, while newer inline AI features remain available directly inside Gmail.
