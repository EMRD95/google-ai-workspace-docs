# Ask Questions in Google Maps — Summary

**Source:** [Google Maps Help: Ask questions in Google Maps](https://support.google.com/maps/answer/16842041)  
**Platform covered:** **Computer**  
**Feature:** Ask Maps / Gemini in Google Maps

---

## Overview

Google Maps includes an AI-powered feature, **Ask Maps**, powered by **Gemini**, that lets users ask questions and get recommendations or details about destinations directly inside Google Maps.

> “You can get recommendations and answers from Gemini in Google Maps. This AI-powered tool helps you find the best places to go and provides specific details about your destination.”

---

## Important Requirement: WebGL

Ask Maps requires **WebGL** to work.

> **Important:** WebGL is required for Ask Maps to function. Some browsers block the WebGL technology used to make 3D images.

Useful links from the article:

- [Learn more about which browsers might cause problems](https://www.khronos.org/webgl/wiki/BlacklistsAndWhitelists)
- [Check if your web browser can use WebGL](https://get.webgl.org/)

---

## How to Ask Questions in Google Maps

1. On your computer, open [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps).
2. Click **Menu** → **Ask Maps**.
3. In the prompt box, enter a question.
4. Tap **Send**.
5. You can continue asking follow-up questions in the same conversation.

---

## Examples of Questions You Can Ask

The article provides examples of practical, multi-step, and location-based questions:

> “Any fun things to do this weekend within a 15-minute drive?”

> ”I need to pick up my friend from the airport. What's the fastest way to get there? When should I leave if I need to be there by 6:00 PM? Where can I get flowers on the way?”

> ”My phone is about to run out of battery. Where can I buy a charger? Are there any charging stations available at no charge without having to order a coffee?”

These examples show that Ask Maps can help with:

- Local recommendations
- Route planning
- Timing and departure planning
- Finding stops along a route
- Locating specific amenities or services
- Follow-up questions

## Find Past Conversations in Chat History

To access previous Ask Maps conversations:

1. On your computer, open [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps).
2. Click **Menu** → **Ask Maps**.
3. Tap **History** to view past conversations.
4. Tap a past conversation to load it.

---

## Delete Conversations from Chat History

To delete a conversation:

1. On your computer, open [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps).
2. Click **Menu** → **Ask Maps**.
3. Tap **History**.
4. Next to the conversation you want to delete, tap **Delete**.

> **Important:** You can only delete the full conversation, not just a part of it.

---

## Report a Problem with an Ask Maps Response

To report an issue with a response:

1. Under the response, tap **Thumbs-down**.
2. Select a reason for the feedback.
3. In the text box, describe the issue in detail.
4. Tap **Submit**.

For positive feedback or feature suggestions:

> **Tip:** To leave positive feedback or suggest features, tap Thumbs-up and fill out the form.

## Overview

Google Maps’ AI-powered features may use **location data** and **account activity** to provide more relevant, helpful, and personalized responses. Some related settings are currently being updated across Google Search services, including **Search, Maps, Shopping, Flights, Hotels, Translate, and News**.

If the new settings are not visible yet, Google says history and personalization for Search services are still controlled through **Web & App Activity**.

---

## AI-powered features and location data

Google Maps AI-powered features may use or collect location data in specific cases:

- **AI-powered answers**
  - Store the answers you receive.
  - Those stored answers may include your location.

- **Lens in Maps**
  - May store your location.

- **Gemini in Navigation**
  - May store your location.

### Exception

When you click on a place and ask a question about it:

- **Maps does not use or collect your location** for that interaction.

---

## How Maps personalizes AI-powered responses

If **Personalized Recommendations** are enabled, Google Maps may personalize AI-powered responses using account information, including:

- Places you searched for.
- Places you got directions to, **if Maps history is being saved**.
- Saved lists or labels added to places.
- Reviews, ratings, and photos you posted for places.
- Activity from your **Search history**, used to tailor responses to your tastes and preferences.

Relevant controls/resources:

- [Personalized Recommendations](https://www.google.com/search-personalization/)
- [Maps history](https://support.google.com/maps/answer/3137804)
- [Search history](https://myactivity.google.com/search-services/history/search)
- [Web & App Activity](https://myactivity.google.com/personalization/settings/waa)

# Use Gemini While You Navigate — Android (Google Maps Help)

**Source:** Google Maps Help — “Use Gemini while you navigate”  
**Platform covered:** Android phones/tablets

## Overview

Gemini can be used inside Google Maps during navigation to help you get information and complete tasks while **driving, walking, or riding a two-wheeler**. You can ask questions or give commands without leaving navigation, helping you stay focused on the road.

## Important Notes

- **Availability:** Gemini is currently available in many countries and languages.
  - Related help: *Gemini mobile app availability*
- **Language and voice settings:** Google Maps uses the **Gemini language and voice preferences** for the signed-in Google account.
  - Related help: *Change Gemini’s language*
- **Location-based answers:** Gemini in Google Maps provides answers based on your **precise location preferences**.
  - Related help: *Manage precise location preferences*

---

## How to Talk to Gemini in Google Maps on Android

1. Open the **Google Maps app** on your Android phone or tablet.
   - If you haven’t used Gemini before, first get started with the **Gemini mobile app**.

2. While in **navigation mode**, activate Gemini by saying:

   > “Hey Google”

   Or tap the **Gemini** icon in Maps.

3. Ask Gemini for help with anything supported in Maps or connected apps.

### If “Hey Google” Doesn’t Work

Check that **“Hey Google” and Voice Match**, powered by Gemini, are turned on.

---

## What You Can Ask Gemini to Do in Maps

## 1. Get Help with Google Maps

### Basic Navigation Controls

Use Gemini to control navigation with commands such as:

> "Navigate home"  
> "Stop navigating"  
> "Add stop"  
> "Mute guidance"  
> "Avoid tolls"  
> "Show alternative routes"

### Basic Route Answers

Ask for information about your current route, including:

> "What's my next turn?"  
> "When will I arrive?"  
> “What’s the weather like at my destination?”  
> "What's my next stop?"

### Report Route Issues

Submit reports about conditions on your route, such as:

> "There’s traffic on the road."  
> "There’s construction up ahead."  
> "There's a car breakdown that’s causing congestion."

---

## 2. Search and Get Information About Places

Gemini can help you search for places while navigating.

### Search Capabilities

You can:

- Search along your route
- Find places near you
- Find places near your destination
- Refine search results
- Add stops to your route

Example:

> “Find me a cafe near my destination that serves tiramisu.”

### Place Information

Gemini can answer questions about destinations, search results, specific places, and landmarks.

Examples:

> “What's the most popular dish on the menu?”  
> "When does {Place Name} close?"

---

## 3. Call or Text Someone

You can ask Gemini to place calls or send messages:

> "Call Mom."  
> "Send a text to Larry”  
> "Send an SMS to Sergey.”

---

## 4. Play Music

Gemini can control music playback with commands like:

> "Play some jazz"  
> “Stop playing music.”

Related help: *Learn how to listen to music*

---

## 5. Use Other Apps with Gemini

**Important:** To use other apps with Gemini in Google Maps, the apps must be connected to the **same account signed into Google Maps**.

Related help: *Use and manage connected apps in Gemini*

Examples of supported requests:

> "Summarize my latest emails."  
> "When’s my next meeting?"  
> "Play music with Spotify."
