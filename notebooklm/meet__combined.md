## What Ask Gemini in Google Meet Does

Ask Gemini brings Gemini capabilities directly into active Google Meet meetings. It helps users privately catch up, summarize, and extract useful meeting information.

### You can use Ask Gemini to:

- Summarize ongoing discussions
- Get a recap of what someone said
- List:
  - Key takeaways
  - Decisions
  - Action items
- Catch up on missed discussion if you joined late, **only if “take notes for me” was enabled**

> “Ask Gemini in Meet brings the power of Gemini into your organization’s meetings. It can help track action items and summarize discussions.”

---

## How Ask Gemini Works

Ask Gemini generates responses using:

- Live meeting captions
- Google Workspace resources the user has permission to view, such as:
  - Docs
  - Sheets
  - Slides
  - Gmail
  - Calendar
- Google Search and public websites

### Important Limitations

- Ask Gemini interactions and responses are **private to each user**.
- Caption data is **not stored after the meeting ends**.
- Ask Gemini **does not create a meeting recording**.
- It cannot answer questions about discussion that happened before the user joined unless **“take notes for me”** had already been turned on.
- Ask Gemini is **not available in breakout rooms**.
- Gemini can make mistakes, including about people, so users should review its output.

> “No caption data will be stored after the meeting ends. Ask Gemini in Meet does not create a meeting recording.”

> “Gemini in Workspace can make mistakes, including about people, so users should review its output.”

## Getting Started with Ask Gemini

Ask Gemini starts automatically after **two participants join** a meeting. The meeting host can also turn it on.

### Use Ask Gemini During a Meeting

1. In the meeting, go to the **bottom left**.
2. Click the **Ask Gemini** text box.
3. Ask questions about the meeting by:
   - Typing a question
   - Selecting suggested questions
   - Clicking the Ask Gemini icon for more suggestions

### If Ask Gemini Doesn’t Turn On

- Hover over the **Ask Gemini turned off** icon in the bottom left.
- Click **Start Now**.
- If it still does not turn on, check troubleshooting causes.

# Calendar & Meet settings for "take notes for me"

## Important requirements & limitations
- **Requires** an eligible Google Workspace subscription with Gemini features.
- **Supported languages**: English, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Spanish.  
  - ⚠️ Only one language per meeting – multiple languages are not supported.
- **Gemini Alpha** users may have additional options (e.g., auto‑enable for meetings with 3+ guests).

## What the feature does
- Automatically captures meeting notes in a **Google Doc** and shares them with your team.
- Provides a **“Summary so far”** for participants who join late.
- Sends the organizer an **email with a link to the recap** after the meeting ends (the link also appears in the Calendar event).

## Turning automatic note taking on/off
Settings apply to **all future meetings you host** (configurable at [meet.google.com](https://meet.google.com/)).

1. Click **Settings** (top right) > **Meeting records** (left column).
2. Under **“Automatic note taking for all future meetings”**, choose:
   - **Off** – Never auto‑add.
   - **For all scheduled meetings I host** – Always on.
   - **For all scheduled meetings I host with 3+ guests** *(Gemini Alpha only)* – Enables when a third guest (including you) is added.
3. Click **Save**.

### While in the pre‑meeting greenroom:
- The page shows whether the feature will be enabled or is already on (if someone is in the call).
- Click the notification to open **“Meeting tools”** and start/stop notes and transcription (not available for external guests or if host controls restrict you).
- **Note:** Once someone joins the Meet call, you cannot change these settings.

## Adjust language & sharing settings per meeting
These settings are configured inside **Google Calendar** when editing an event.

### To select the language:
1. Open the event in Calendar and click **Edit event**.
2. Add Google Meet video conferencing.
3. Click **Video call options** > **Meeting records**.
4. Check **“take notes with Gemini”**.
5. Choose the language from the dropdown. *(Some languages are only available to Alpha users.)*

### To change sharing defaults:
1. Follow the same path to **Meeting records**.
2. Below the checkboxes, find **“take notes for me sharing default settings”**.
3. Select one of the following:
   - **All invited guests, including those outside of my organization** – Full sharing.
   - **Invited guests in my organization** – Internal only.
   - **Hosts and co‑hosts only** – Restricted to hosts/co‑hosts.

## Feature Overview

Google Meet allows eligible users to generate custom meeting backgrounds using Gemini AI through the **“Generate a background”** prompt.

You can describe the type of background you want, and Meet will generate suggested images you can apply as your meeting background.

### Example prompts

- `"Luxurious living room interior"`
- `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`
- `"A cozy coffee shop with flowers"`

### Availability

- Available on **desktop**
- Available on **Android**
- The page is specifically for **Computer**, but notes Android support as well
- Also available through **Google Workspace Experiments**, Google’s early access testing program

### Requirements

To use the feature, you need:

- An eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan
- Smart features enabled, if required by your account settings

If background generation is unavailable, it may be because **Smart feature control is disabled**.

---

## How to Create an AI Background Before a Meeting

1. On a computer, go to [meet.google.com](https://meet.google.com/).
2. Select a meeting.
3. At the bottom-right of your self-view, click:
   - **Apply visual effects**
   - Then **Generate a background**
4. In the **“Generate a background”** setup, enter a prompt.

   Example prompts:

   - `"Luxurious living room interior"`
   - `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`

5. Click **Create** to generate a suggested background image.
6. Optional: Edit the prompt after generating:
   1. At the top of the right-side panel, click the prompt.
   2. Edit the prompt.
   3. Click **Create a new sample**.
   4. Click **Apply**.
7. To use a generated image, click the image.
8. To exit the setup, click **Close**.

---

## How to Create an AI Background During a Meeting

1. On a computer, go to [meet.google.com](https://meet.google.com/).
2. Join a meeting.
3. At the bottom of the meeting window, click:
   - **More options**
   - Then **Apply visual effects**
   - Then **Generate a background**
4. In the **“Generate a background”** setup, enter a prompt.

   Example prompts:

   - `"Luxurious living room interior"`
   - `"A magical, sunny forest glade"`

5. Click **Create** to generate suggested background images.
6. Optional: Edit the prompt after generating:
   1. At the top of the right-side panel, click the prompt.
   2. Edit the prompt.
   3. Click **Create a new sample**.
   4. Click **Apply**.
7. To use a generated image as your meeting background, click it.
8. To leave the background generation setup, click **Close**.

# Cloud-Based Effects Processing

Google Meet can process some video effects in the cloud to help devices run real-time video more efficiently.

## Benefits

Cloud-based processing can help:

- Save battery
- Save processor power
- Optimize video meeting quality

## How It Works

Meet automatically adjusts between device-based and cloud-based effects processing.

It prioritizes cloud-based processing for:

- Devices with **4-core CPUs or lower**
- Devices with **fewer than 8 logical cores**

## Availability

> **Important:** This feature is currently available for Windows, ChromeOS, and Linux machines that comply with device-based processing requirements.

Cloud-accelerated visual effects are available in eligible regions:

- Central Asia
- Europe
- Northern Africa
- North America
- Southeastern Asia

Support for additional regions is being added.

## How to Turn On Translated Captions
> *"If you need subtitles in your meetings, learn how to use captions in Google Meet."*

1. On your computer, go to [Google Meet](https://meet.google.com/).
2. In the meeting, click **More options** (`⋮`) → **Settings** (`⚙`) → **Captions**.
3. Select **Language of the meeting**.
4. Turn on **Translated captions** (`🌐`).
5. Choose the language you want captions translated **into**.

**Tip:** If recording a meeting, select **Record captions** to embed the captions in the recording.

---

## Customize Captions Appearance
1. Join a meeting → **More options** → **Settings** → **Captions**.
2. Under **“Customize your captions,”** adjust:
   - Font
   - Font size
   - Font color
   - Background color
3. Click **Close** (settings are saved automatically).

---

## View Past Translated Captions
While captions are turned on:
- Scroll **up or down** to review captions from earlier in the conversation.
- Translates only the portions of the conversation **where you were in the meeting and had captions enabled**.
