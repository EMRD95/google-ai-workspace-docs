# Ask Gemini in Chrome to Complete Tasks with Auto Browse — Summary

Source: Google Chrome Help — “Ask Gemini in Chrome to complete tasks for you with auto browse”

## Overview

**Auto browse** lets you ask **Gemini in Chrome** to complete multi-step tasks on the web for you. Gemini may use your preferences, instructions, previously shared details, page context, and personal information to provide more useful results.

Example tasks include:

- Comparing products, searching for deals, and adding items to your cart
- Finding and booking travel accommodations based on criteria
- Making restaurant reservations and scheduling appointments
- Performing administrative web tasks and other online chores

> **Important:** Gemini in Chrome can use a broad range of sites on the web to complete tasks for you. This is true even if you haven't connected the related apps to Gemini Apps.

---

## Requirements

To use auto browse on a computer, you must:

- Be **18 or older**
- Be located in the **United States**
- Be eligible for and opted in to **Gemini in Chrome**
- Use the **latest version of Chrome**
- Set your device language to **English**
- Have **Google AI Pro** or **Google AI Ultra** on a personal account
- Sign in to Chrome with a **personal Google Account**
  - Not available in **Incognito mode**
  - Not available with a **school Google Account**
- Set Chrome **Safe Browsing** to either:
  - **Enhanced protection**, or
  - **Standard protection**

### Availability notes

> **Important:**
>
> - We’re gradually releasing auto browse in Gemini in Chrome, so it might not be available to you just yet.
> - For now, this feature is not available in Live chats or Gemini in Chrome on iPhone devices.

---

## How to Ask Gemini in Chrome to Complete a Task

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. Click **Ask Gemini** at the top of the browser.
3. In the text box, describe the task you want Gemini to complete.
4. Click **Submit**.
5. Review Gemini’s proposed plan.
   - Confirm it understood:
     - Your prompt
     - The page context
     - Your personal info
   - Gemini chooses which sites to use and **may share your personal info with those sites**.
6. If the plan looks correct, click **Start Task**.
7. If needed, you may be asked to:
   - Review and confirm sensitive steps
   - Take over steps you must complete personally
   - Give Gemini permission to sign in to a site for you

While Gemini is performing a task:

- An **auto browse icon** appears on the tab Gemini is using.
- If Gemini needs your attention, a notification appears at the top of the browser.

---

## Task Controls

### Go to the task tab

- Next to the task in chat, click **Switch tabs**
- If a task needs attention, click **Check your task** at the top of the browser

### Take over a task

You can take over at any time.

Steps:

1. Go to the tab Gemini is using.
2. Click **Take over task**.
3. Complete the steps yourself.
4. To return control to Gemini, click **Resume** or **Give back task** in the Gemini chat.

### Resume a task

- Click **Resume** or **Give back task**

### Stop a task

- Click **Stop** in Gemini in Chrome, or
- Close the task tab

Gemini in Chrome can help you:

- Find and book travel accommodations based on specific criteria
- Manage dining reservations and food delivery
- Search for tickets for events and group entertainment
- Find and compare products for online shopping
- Draft communications and organize calendar schedules
- Research job markets and professional networking opportunities
- Assist with online finance chores, such as fetching receipts

## Signing In to Websites

With your permission, Gemini in Chrome can use **Google Password Manager** to sign in to websites for you.

Key privacy point:

> Google Password Manager does not share your passwords with Gemini in Chrome.

You can remove sign-in permission at any time.

### Remove Gemini’s permission to sign in to a site

1. Open **Chrome**.
2. Go to **More** → **Passwords and autofill** → **Google Password Manager**.
3. Click **Settings** on the left.
4. Under **“Gemini can sign in for you,”** review allowed sites.
5. Click **Delete** next to a site to remove permission.

## Turn Auto Browse On or Off

To change whether Gemini in Chrome can browse for you:

1. Open **Chrome**.

3. In Settings, click **AI innovations** → **Gemini in Chrome**.
   - If the menu is hidden, click **Menu** first.
4. Under **Permissions**, turn **Let Gemini browse for you** on or off.

## Overview

**Help me write** is an experimental Chrome feature that provides AI-powered writing suggestions in open text fields on the web. It is intended for **short-form writing**, such as:

- Reviews
- Surveys
- Replies
- Drafting or refining text in web forms

It can generate new text from a prompt or rewrite selected text.

---

## Key Requirements

To use **Help me write**, you must:

- Be **located in the US**
- Be **at least 18 years old**
- Have **English** set as your Chrome browser language
- Be **signed in** to your Google Account in Chrome
- Have **Make searches and browsing better** turned on

Availability:

- Language: **English only**
- Platforms: **Windows, Mac, and Linux**
- Region: **US only**

---

## How to Get Writing Suggestions

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. Go to a website.
3. Right-click an open text field.
   - To rewrite text, **highlight the text first**, then right-click it.
4. Select **Help me write**.
5. In the **“Help me write”** box, enter your prompt.
   - A prompt can be:
     - A phrase
     - A question
     - An instruction
     - A sentence
   - If you highlighted text, it will automatically appear in the box.
6. Choose a writing goal:
   - **Polish:** Helps rewrite text and add noticeable value without changing the user’s tone.
     - This is the default option.
   - **Elaborate:** Creates a more detailed or creative draft with additional sentence structures and details.
   - **Formalize:** Rewrites with a more professional tone.
7. Select **Create**.
8. Select **Insert** or **Replace** to add the suggestion to the text field.

---

## Editing and Refining Suggestions

After generating a suggestion, you can:

- Edit your original input:
  - Select **Edit**, then **Update**
- Edit directly in the generated response:
  - Click anywhere in the response box
- Modify the response using options such as:
  - **Casual**
  - **Formal**
  - **Elaborate**
  - **Shorten**
  - **Rephrase**
  - **Retry**
- Use **Undo** or **Redo** for changes to generated text

You can also rate suggestions:

- Select **Thumbs Up**
- Select **Thumbs Down**
  - If you choose Thumbs Down, you can explain why the suggestion didn’t meet your needs.

# Manage on-device Generative AI models in Chrome

To provide an enhanced browser experience, Chrome uses on-device AI models to help power web and browser features. Chrome may download on-device Generative AI models in the background, so features that rely on these on-device models stay ready for use. If you delete on-device AI models, only features that rely on them will be unavailable.

Some AI features in Chrome do not rely on on-device Generative models, and those features may still run even if the on-device Generative AI models are removed.

## Understand on-device Generative AI models

On-device Generative AI models enable Chrome to perform AI-powered tasks directly on your device.

These models might be used on features, such as:

- Help with writing or rephrasing text
- Warn you about scams
- Summarize web pages
- Organize your tabs

For these models to work on your device, Chrome downloads and stores them on your device.

## Manage Generative AI models on your device

To manage on-device Generative AI models:

# Search Your Browsing History in Chrome with AI — Summary

**Source:** Google Chrome Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/15305774

## Overview

Chrome’s **History search, powered by AI** lets you search your browsing history using everyday language, even if you don’t remember the exact keyword, page title, or website address.

It can return AI-generated “best match” results based on sites you previously visited.

---

## Requirements

To use this feature:

- You must be **located in the US**
- Chrome must be set to **English**

Google links to instructions for changing Chrome languages or translating pages.

---

## Key Excerpts

> You can use everyday language to search your browsing history and find sites you visited. This works even if you don't know an exact keyword or website address.

> When you turn on "History search, powered by AI," in addition to the page title and URL, the page contents of the website you browse at that time are stored locally.

> Only the websites you visit after you turn on this feature can be used by AI-powered history and may appear in your AI best matches.

> As you browse websites, their page contents are saved and encrypted to your device and might affect Chrome’s performance.

> Website content can change over time. Even if the website content has changed since your last visit, AI-powered history search results use the content present at the time when it was browsed.

---

## Manage “History Search, Powered by AI”

### Important Notes

When the feature is enabled:

- Chrome stores more than standard history:
  - **Page title**
  - **URL**
  - **Page contents from websites you browse**
- Page contents are:
  - Stored **locally**
  - Saved and **encrypted to your device**
- Only pages visited **after enabling** the feature can be used for AI-powered history search.
- AI best matches may include those later-visited pages.
- The feature may be unavailable or disabled if you use:
  - A **managed profile**
  - A **managed browser**
- Organizations may disable the feature.
- Saving and encrypting page contents may affect **Chrome performance**.

### Turn the Feature On or Off

1. On your computer, open **Chrome**.
2. At the top right, select **More** → **Settings** → **AI Innovations**.
3. Select **History search, powered by AI**.
4. Turn **History search, powered by AI** on or off.

**Tip:** You can turn off “History search, powered by AI” at any time in Chrome Settings.

---

## Find AI Best Match Answers from Browsing History

### Important Behavior

AI-powered history search uses the version of page content that existed **when you visited the page**, not necessarily the current version of the website.

This means results may reflect older content if the website has changed since your visit.

- **Yesterday**

## Best Matches from Browsing History

> “Best matches from your history”

Details:

- This section displays AI-powered best matches.

---

## Limitations and Result Behavior

- Short, simple searches are matched directly to:
  - Page title
  - URL
- Short/simple searches will **not** produce AI-powered results.
- AI-powered results only use content from sites visited after the feature was enabled.
- Results are based on page contents stored at the time of browsing.

# Use Gemini in Chrome — Google Chrome Help Summary

**Source:** <https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/16283624>  
**Platform covered:** Computer

## Overview

Gemini in Chrome provides AI assistance directly in the browser. It can help users get key takeaways, clarify concepts, find answers, compare information across pages, and more.

> “To provide the most relevant responses, Gemini in Chrome uses content from your current browser tab.”

On computers, users can also share **up to 10 open tabs** with Gemini.

---

## Availability & Requirements

> **Important**: Gemini in Chrome is not available to all users at this time. We’re gradually releasing Gemini in Chrome, so it might not be available to you just yet.

To use Gemini in Chrome on a computer, you must:

- Be **18 or over**
- Be in a **supported region**
- Use a **Chromebook Plus, Mac, or Windows computer**
- Use the **latest version of Chrome**
- Be **signed in to Chrome**
  - Not available in **Incognito mode**
- Set your device to a **supported language**

For work or school Google Accounts:

- Access must be enabled by your administrator.
- Gemini Apps usage depends on organizational settings.

---

## How to Chat with Gemini in Chrome

1. Open **Chrome** on your computer.
2. At the top of the browser, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. If using it for the first time, follow the opt-in instructions.
4. Enter your question or request in the text box.

### Tab context behavior

By default:

- Gemini uses content from your **current tab** to respond.
- Tabs opened from a chat or shared tab are also shared with Gemini.
- In Gemini Live, the current tab is shared by default.

You can add tabs directly in a prompt by typing:

```text
@
```

Then select the tab.

You can share **up to 10 open tabs**.

**Tip:** After opting in, Gemini in Chrome is added to the **Mac menu bar** or **Windows system tray**, and keyboard shortcuts become available.

Gemini in Chrome can help you:

- Summarize key takeaways from an article
- Explain complex topics differently
- Test your knowledge of a subject
- Modify recipes for dietary needs
- Compare or consolidate information across pages
- Make recommendations based on your preferences
- Complete multi-step actions on your behalf
- Draft Gmail messages
- Look up past photos and albums

# Use the Gemini web app to get answers in Chrome

## What you need

To use the Gemini web app, you need to:

- Have a personal Google Account that you manage on your own. You still can’t access the Gemini web app with a Google Account managed by Family Link. [Read the Gemini Apps Privacy Notice](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/13594961#privacy_notice).
