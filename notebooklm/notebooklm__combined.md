# NotebookLM Help Summary: Add or Discover New Sources for Your Notebook

Source: [Google NotebookLM Help — Add or discover new sources for your notebook](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16215270?hl=en)

---

## Key Excerpts & Important Facts

> **Important:** At this time, the NotebookLM mobile app may have limitations to this feature.

> A source is a copy or auto-synced version of the source document you import or upload to the app.

> When you use NotebookLM, the model uses the sources you upload to answer your questions or complete your requests.

> Each source can contain up to **500,000 words** or up to **200MB** for uploaded files.

> You can include up to **50 sources** for Free users.

> To chat with a specific set of sources in your Notebook, select them individually in the “Source” panel.

> When uploading multiple web URLs, separate links by a space or a new line.

> If the source content is too short, NotebookLM references the entire document without citing individual text from your source.

---

# 1. What Sources Are in NotebookLM

A **source** is either:

- A copy of a document you upload/import, or
- An auto-synced version of a connected source, such as a Google Drive file.

NotebookLM uses your selected sources to answer questions, summarize material, and complete requests within a notebook.

---

# 2. Supported Source Types & Limits

## Supported Source Types

NotebookLM supports importing or uploading the following source types:

### Files & Text

- Copy-pasted text
- Microsoft Word: `.docx`
- Text files: `.txt`
- Markdown: `.md`
- PDFs: `.pdf`
- CSV files: `.csv`
- PowerPoint files: `.pptx`
- ePub files

### Google Drive / Workspace Files

Supported Google Drive files include:

- Google Docs
- Google Slides — up to **100 slides**
- Google Sheets — limited to **100k tokens**
- Supported files from Google Drive generally

### Audio Files

Supported audio sources include MP3 and WAV, among others.

### Images

Supported image file types:

- `avif`
- `bmp`
- `gif`
- `heic`
- `heif`
- `ico`
- `jp2`
- `jpe`
- `jpeg`
- `jpg`
- `png`
- `tif`
- `tiff`
- `webp`

**Note:** Certain types of images may not work as well.

### Web & Online Sources

- Web URLs
- YouTube URLs of public videos
- Gemini Chats

### Gemini Chats

You can chat with your notebooks in Gemini and add them as context to NotebookLM notebooks.

  - **500,000 words**, or
  - **200MB** for uploaded files

# 3. Tips for Working with Sources

- Avoid uploading documents you do not have rights to.
- You can create a source by copying and pasting text.
- You can add or edit the title when creating a pasted-text source.
- To chat with only certain sources, select them in the **Source** panel.
- When uploading multiple web URLs, separate each link with:
  - A space, or
  - A new line.

# 4. Summarizing Sources

NotebookLM provides two main ways to summarize sources.

## Option 1: Ask in Chat

You can ask NotebookLM to summarize:

- A full source
- Specific sections
- Specific topics
- Information across selected sources

For better results, ask focused questions.

### Better Prompt Example

Instead of:

> "Summarize this source"

Use:

> "What are the key findings about dog training in the 'Dog Training 101' document?"

When multiple sources are selected, include source names in your prompt to help NotebookLM narrow its response.

---

## Option 2: Use the Source Guide

NotebookLM auto-generates a summary of the entire source in the **Source Guide**.

To access it:

1. Open the source viewer on the left side.
2. Select **“To open a source.”**

On a computer:

# Create a Notebook in NotebookLM — Summary

**Source:** [NotebookLM Help: Create a notebook in NotebookLM](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16206563?hl=en)

## Key Excerpts

> **Important:** At this time, the NotebookLM mobile app may have limitations to this feature.

> In NotebookLM, you can create individual notebooks. A notebook is a collection of sources for a specific project.

> **Tip:** Each notebook is independent. NotebookLM can’t access information across multiple notebooks at the same time.

> **Tip:** You can add or remove sources in your notebook by selecting **+ Add** or unselecting individual sources in the “Sources” panel.

> **Tip**: When you have 5+ sources, NotebookLM can auto-label & categorize sources, allowing you to quickly and easily organize your sources by topic.

> **Tip**: Any modifications made within exported Docs or Sheets files do not synchronize with your original NotebookLM content. Sharing permissions do not carry over from NotebookLM to exported files.

> Don't rely on NotebookLM for medical, legal, financial, or other professional advice. Any content regarding those topics is provided for informational purposes only and isn’t a substitute for advice from a qualified professional.

---

## What a Notebook Is

A **NotebookLM notebook** is an independent workspace containing sources for a specific project.

- Each notebook has its own sources, chat, and generated outputs.
- NotebookLM **cannot access information across multiple notebooks at the same time**.
- The mobile app may not fully support all notebook creation features.

---

## Create Your First Notebook

1. Open [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google.com/).
2. Select **Create new notebook**.
3. In the pop-up window, choose **Upload a source**.
4. Select or discover the sources you want to add.
5. Start collaborating with NotebookLM using the **Chat** and **Studio** panels.

---

## Main Notebook Panels

### Chat Panel

The **Chat** panel displays a generated summary of your sources.

You can use it to:

- Ask questions about your sources
- Give instructions
- Choose from suggested options
- Interact with NotebookLM based on the uploaded materials

### Studio Panel

The **Studio** panel is where NotebookLM generates outputs based on your sources.

- **Mind maps**
- **Reports**
- **Data Tables**
- **Flashcards**

- **Infographics**

NotebookLM may sometimes automatically create starter outputs such as reports, flashcards, infographics, slide decks, audio overviews, or video overviews when sources are first added.

- These auto-generated items are created once.
- They **do not count toward usage limits**.

  - FAQ
  - Study guide
  - Briefing document
  - AI-suggested report type

Open the **three dots menu** next to a generated report to select:

- **Export to Docs**
- **Export to Sheets**, if the report contains data tables

- Each data table populates its own tab in a Google Sheet.

### Exporting Data Tables

Open the **three dots menu** next to a generated Data Table and select:

- **Export to Sheets**

Export behavior:

- The data table populates the first tab of a new Google Sheet.
- Citations populate the second tab.

- A **Show prompt** option is also available next to the title in the viewer.

- [Learn more about using NotebookLM with a work or school Google account](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16337734).

## Questions Not Answered
NotebookLM may not answer because of:

- **Safety flags**: The source content contains language that triggers a safety flag. This occurs with sensitive topics like violence, sexuality, or obscenity, even in historical contexts.
- **Unclear phrases**: When your notebook has many sources, refine your question to help NotebookLM locate the most relevant pieces of information.
  - **Tip:** Make your question more precise and understandable.
- **Information not in sources**: NotebookLM only answers based on the information provided in your uploaded sources. If the answer isn't in the source material, it won't respond.

## Reporting Offensive Responses
1. Select thumbs down (⊖).
2. Choose **Offensive/unsafe**.
3. Click **Submit**.

We’ll review the report and take appropriate actions to improve the safety of our model.

## Notebook Sharing Icons
- **Lock icon**: Notebook is private (shared with no one).
- **Shared icon**: Notebook shared individually with users.
- **Globe icon**: Notebook is publicly shared.

## Reporting Infringed Rights in a Shared Notebook
If you find content that violates the law:
- Open the settings menu in the notebook and select **Report Notebook**, or visit this [direct link](https://support.google.com/legal/troubleshooter/1114905).
- We’ll review and consider blocking, limiting, or removing access.
- **Tip:** You can also report through [report content on Google](https://support.google.com/legal/troubleshooter/1114905).

> Your data will never be shared by NotebookLM.

[Learn how NotebookLM protects your data](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16179536).

- **Notebooks**: up to 100
- **Sources per notebook**: up to 50, each up to 500,000 words
- **Daily limits**:
  - 50 chat queries
  - 3 audio generations

## Source File Limits
- Per source: 500,000 words or up to 200 MB for local uploads. There is no page limit.

## Source Import Failures
Sources can’t be imported if:
- It exceeds the word limit (500,000) or file size limit (200 MB).
- The original PDF file is copy-protected.

## How Data Is Used in Prompts
- **Notes**: Used only when you specifically select a note.
- **Sources**: Always used, either the entire set or the subset you select.
- **Conversation history**: Used to generate responses.

## Citations
If your source content is too short, NotebookLM may reference the entire document without citing individual text from your source.

Currently, there is no way to recover deleted notes.

## Duplicating a Notebook
This feature isn’t supported yet.

# Generate Audio Overview in NotebookLM — Summary

Source: [NotebookLM Help](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16212820?hl=en)

## Key Excerpts & Important Notes

> **Important:** At this time, the NotebookLM mobile app may have limitations to this feature.

> Audio Overviews are deep-dive discussions between AI hosts that provide in-depth summaries of the key topics in your uploaded sources. These overviews are designed to be an objective reflection of your source content, rather than subjective opinions from the AI hosts.

> **Important:**
>
> - Audio Overviews, including voices, are AI-generated and may contain inaccuracies or audio glitches.
> - NotebookLM may take a couple of minutes to generate the Audio Overview.

> **Tip:** You can also listen to Audio Overviews while continuing to work within NotebookLM. You can explore relevant quotes, get citations, and query sources while listening to an Audio Overview in the background.

---

## What Audio Overviews Are

**Audio Overviews** are AI-generated audio discussions in NotebookLM based on uploaded notebook sources. They summarize and explain key topics from the sources, typically through AI-hosted conversations.

They are intended to be:

- **Source-grounded**
- **Objective reflections** of the uploaded material
- Useful for listening while continuing to work in NotebookLM

However, they may include:

- Inaccuracies
- Audio glitches
- AI-generated voice artifacts

---

## Generate an Audio Overview

### Requirements

To generate or delete an Audio Overview, you need **edit access** to the notebook.

### Steps

1. Open an existing notebook or create a new notebook.
2. Upload sources.
3. In the **Studio** panel, select **Audio Overview**.
4. NotebookLM generates the audio in the background.
   - You can continue generating other artifacts.
   - You can navigate to other screens while it processes.
5. Wait a couple of minutes for generation to complete.

---

## Customize an Audio Overview

Within the generation panel, you can set preferences before generating the audio.

### Format Options

- **Deep Dive — default**
  - An in-depth conversation.
  - Two hosts unpack and connect topics from your sources in a lively discussion.

- **The Brief**
  - A quick overview.
  - A single speaker delivers key takeaways in under two minutes.

- **The Critique**
  - Critical feedback.
  - Two hosts provide a constructive evaluation of material such as an essay or design document.

- **The Debate**
  - Multiple perspectives.
  - Two hosts engage in a formal back-and-forth debate on the topic.

### Other Customization Options

- Choose a **language**.
- Tailor the **length**:
  - Shorter
  - Default
  - Longer  
  - **English only**
- Add a **custom prompt** to:
  - Focus on specific topics
  - Adjust the expertise level
  - Provide other instructions

## Why NotebookLM May Be Unable to Answer Questions

NotebookLM may fail to answer or may produce limited answers for several reasons.

### 1. Safety flags

Source content may trigger a safety filter.

This can happen with sensitive topics such as:

- Violence
- Sexuality
- Obscenity

This may occur even when the material is presented in a historical or academic context.

### 2. Unclear phrasing

When a notebook contains many sources, NotebookLM first retrieves the most relevant information based on the user’s question, then generates an answer from that information.

If the question is vague, the system may not retrieve the right source material.

**Actionable guidance:**

- Rephrase the question
- Make the question clearer
- Ask more specific questions

### 3. Information is not in the sources

NotebookLM is designed to answer based on the information provided in uploaded or available sources.

If the answer is not present in the sources, NotebookLM may not be able to answer.

### Key excerpt

> NotebookLM is designed to answer questions based on the information provided in your uploaded sources.

# Use Mind Maps in NotebookLM — Summary

**Source:** [NotebookLM Help](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16212283?hl=en)  
**Topic:** How to generate, use, save/share, and provide feedback on Mind Maps in NotebookLM.

---

## Key Excerpts

> **Important:** The NotebookLM mobile app does not support this feature at this time.

> Mind Maps in NotebookLM visually summarize your uploaded sources, showing main topics and related ideas as a branching diagram.

> **Important:** NotebookLM can be inaccurate; please double check its responses.

---

## What Mind Maps Do

Mind Maps in NotebookLM create a **visual branching diagram** from your uploaded sources. They are designed to help users quickly understand source material by showing:

- Main topics
- Related ideas
- Subtopics
- Connections between concepts

---

## When to Use Mind Maps

Use Mind Maps to:

- **Get a quick overview**  
  Quickly understand the big picture and key themes in your sources.

- **Explore and learn new things easily**  
  Use the map as an entry point into unfamiliar topics before reading details.

- **Connect the dots**  
  Visually identify relationships, links, and associations that may not be obvious.

- **Organize your thoughts**  
  Structure information to improve understanding and retention.

---

## How to Generate a Mind Map

1. Open an existing notebook in [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google.com/).
   - Or create a new notebook and upload sources.
2. In the chat, select the **Mind Map** chip.
3. In the **Studio** panel, find the newly generated Mind Map note.
   - You can revisit the Mind Map at any time.
4. To regenerate a Mind Map:
   - Select **More** / the three-dot menu.
   - Choose **Delete note**.
   - This restarts the generation process.

---

## How to Interact with a Mind Map

Available interactions include:

- **Zoom in or out and scroll**  
  Navigate around the Mind Map and focus on specific areas.

- **Expand or collapse branches**  
  Expand branches to see detailed subtopics or collapse them for a higher-level overview.

- **Select nodes to ask questions**  
  Click a node to ask NotebookLM chat questions about that specific topic.

- **Use top-right controls**  
  Options in the top-right corner let you:
  - Expand or collapse the view
  - Download the Mind Map
  - Exit the Mind Map view

---

## How to Save and Share a Mind Map

There are two sharing options:

### Option 1: Share the Entire Notebook

Generate a Mind Map, then share the notebook with another user.  
They can load the same Mind Map from the **Studio** panel.

### Option 2: Download the Mind Map

Select **Download** inside the Mind Map window, then share the downloaded file.

---

## Mind Map Usage Limits

Usage limits apply to Mind Map creation and are subject to change:

| Plan | Daily Mind Map Limit |
|---|---:|
| **NotebookLM Standard** | 10/day |
| **NotebookLM in Plus** | 20/day |
| **NotebookLM in Pro** | 100/day |
| **NotebookLM in Ultra — 20 TB Plan** | 500/day |
| **NotebookLM in Ultra — 30 TB Plan** | 1,000/day |

---

## Feedback Options

Users can provide feedback on generated Mind Maps:

- Select **Good content** if the Mind Map was helpful.
- Select **Bad content** if the Mind Map was:
  - Unhelpful
  - Inaccurate
  - Biased
  - Otherwise problematic

When marking content as bad, users are encouraged to describe their experience and provide additional feedback.

---

## Important Limitations and Notes

- Mind Maps are **not currently supported in the NotebookLM mobile app**.
- NotebookLM-generated content may be inaccurate, so users should **double-check responses and outputs**.
- Mind Map usage limits vary by NotebookLM plan and may change over time.
