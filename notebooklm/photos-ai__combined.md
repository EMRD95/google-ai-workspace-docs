# Edit Your Photos in Google Photos — Computer Summary

**Source:** Google Photos Help — “Edit your photos”  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/photos/answer/6128850

---

## Overview

Google Photos on desktop lets you edit photos by applying suggested edits, cropping/rotating, using advanced tools, adjusting image settings, adding filters, changing timestamps, undoing edits, editing shared-album media, and handling RAW photos.

> **Tip:** If you have [backup](https://support.google.com/photos/answer/6193313) turned on, your edits will sync to your Google Photos Account. You can check AI info for edited photos.

Some editing features are not available on the web. If a feature is missing, use the Google Photos app on Android or iOS. Chromebook users may need to use the Photos Android app on ChromeOS.

---

## Key Requirements & Important Notes

### RAM Requirement

Several features require sufficient device memory:

> **Important:** Your device must have at least 4 GB RAM.

This applies to:
- Suggested edits
- Editing tools
- Photo adjustment options

### Web Limitations

> Some editing features aren't available on the web. If you can’t find an editing feature on the web, try using the Google Photos app on Android or iOS.

### Comparing Edits

While editing, you can compare your current edits with the original:

> **Tip:** While you edit, click and hold the photo to compare your edits to the original.

### Edits Are Reversible

> **Tip:** Your edits will be applied to the photo, but you can undo them at any time.

---

## Access Google Photos

Most actions begin here:

1. Go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/)
2. Open the photo you want to edit
3. Click **Edit** at the top

Google Help repeatedly provides this shortcut:

[GO TO MY PHOTOS](https://photos.google.com/)

# Editing Options

---

## Use Suggested Edits

Suggested edits are automatically based on the type of photo and can be applied with one click.

### Steps

1. On your computer, go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/).
2. Open the photo you want to edit.
3. At the top, click **Edit**.
4. Click a suggested edit name.
   - Example: **Enhance**
5. To undo the suggestion, click the same suggestion name again.
6. Click **Save** when finished.

---

## Crop or Rotate a Photo

Use **Crop & rotate** to resize framing or rotate the photo.

### Steps

1. Go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/).
2. Open the photo.
3. Click **Edit**.
4. Click **Crop & rotate**.
   - To crop: drag the edges.
   - To rotate: click **Rotate** at the top.
5. Click **Save**.

---

## Use Editing Tools

Tools provide more targeted edits, depending on the photo.

### Steps

1. Go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/).
2. Open the photo.
3. Click **Edit** → **Tools**.
4. Select the tool you want.
5. Make changes.
6. Click **Save**.
7. To undo a tool effect, select the tool and click **Revert**.

### Available Tools

> **Tip:** Tool options depend on your photo. Some tools may not be available for all photos.

Tools include:

- **Portrait light** — Adjusts the position and brightness of light on portraits of a person.
- **Blur** — Adjusts background blur.
- **Sky** — Lets you select palettes and adjust the color and contrast in the sky.
- **Color pop** — Desaturates the background while keeping the foreground in color.

---

## Adjust Photos

The **Adjust** panel lets you fine-tune specific photo properties with a dial.

### Steps

1. Go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/).
2. Open the photo.
3. Click **Edit** → **Adjust**.
4. Select the type of effect to apply.
5. Move the dial to change the effect.
6. Click **Save**.
7. To undo an effect, deselect the option.

### Adjustment Options

- **Brightness** — Adjusts overall lightness or darkness.
- **Contrast** — Adjusts the difference in tones.
- **White point** — Adjusts the brightest value.
- **Black point** — Adjusts the darkest value.
- **Highlights** — Adjusts detail in the brightest areas.
- **Shadows** — Adjusts detail in the darkest areas.
- **Saturation** — Adjusts color intensity.
- **Warmth** — Adjusts color temperature.
- **Tint** — Adjusts hue.
- **Skin tone** — Adjusts saturation of skin-colored tones.
- **Blue tone** — Adjusts saturation of blue tones, such as sky or water.
- **Pop** — Adjusts local contrast of edges.
- **Vignette** — Reduces brightness around the edges.
- **HDR effect** — Enhances brightness and contrast across the image for a more balanced photo.

---

## Add a Filter

Filters apply preset styles and can be adjusted in strength.

### Steps

1. Go to [photos.google.com](https://photos.google.com/).
2. Open the photo.
3. Click **Edit** → **Filters**.
4. Select a filter.
5. Move the dial to change filter strength.
6. Click **Save**.
7. To remove a filter, click **None**.

# Gemini Features in Photos Privacy Hub — Summary

**Source:** Google Photos Help  
**Last updated:** **February 18, 2026** — Updated the “What happens when I connect Google Photos to other Google services” section.

---

## Key Privacy Commitments

Google states the following core privacy protections for Gemini features in Google Photos:

> - Your personal data in Google Photos is never used for ads.  
> - Responses aren't reviewed by humans, unless you give feedback, or to address abuse or harm (this is rare).  
> - We don't train any generative AI models outside of Google Photos with your personal data in Google Photos.

**Important caveat:** These protections apply to Gemini features **within Google Photos**. If you share content with or connect Photos to another Google or third-party service, that other service’s own policies and controls may apply.

---

## What Are Gemini Features in Photos?

Gemini features in Photos are Google Photos features that use Google’s Gemini models to make Photos “smarter and more helpful.”

They include:

- **Ask Photos**
- **AI-powered title suggestions**

These features are covered by both:

- Google’s general **Privacy Policy**
- This specific **Gemini features in Photos privacy notice**

---

## What Data Is Collected & Used

Gemini features in Photos use information you have provided to Google, including:

- Photos and videos stored in your Google Photos library
- Face group labels and names
- Information in your Google Account
- Relationship information you provide for face groups
- Facts saved to your **Remember List**

Google may also process photos and videos to:

- Improve edits
- Make inferences
- Generate insights related to your life

Examples of inferences include:

- Estimated age of top face groups
- Locations associated with top face groups
- Insights related to people, places, and events in your life

Google Photos may use these inferences and insights to provide:

- Customized memories
- Edits
- Creations
- Ask Photos responses

---

## Face Groups, Labels, Names & Relationships

Adding names and relationships to face groups can improve Google Photos features, especially Ask Photos.

Benefits include:

- More helpful Ask Photos responses
- More accurate answers
- Better personalization

Users can manage, view, and delete:

- Face group labels
- Face group names
- Relationship information

---

## Remember List

The **Remember List** lets you save facts for Google Photos to remember and use later.

How it works:

- You can state facts directly in an Ask Photos conversation.
- Ask Photos will confirm it will remember the fact.
- You will have the option to undo the action.
- Saved facts can be viewed from the **“Gemini features in Photos”** setting.
- Each saved fact is shown in the Remember List.
- You can review and delete any saved fact.

---

## Advertising

Google states clearly:

> Your personal data in Photos is never used for ads.

This also applies to Ask Photos conversations.

---

## Generative AI Model Training

Google states:

> We don't train any generative AI models outside of Google Photos with your personal data in Google Photos.

However, this protection has limits when Google Photos is connected to other Google services.

When connecting Photos to other services, Google says it does **not** train generative AI models outside Google Photos directly on:

- Imagery from your Google Photos library
- Audio from your Google Photos library

But Google may train on derived data such as:

- Summaries
- Inferences
- Generated media based on the contents of your Photos library

---

## Human Review

### Ask Photos Responses

Human reviewers generally **do not review Ask Photos responses**, except:

- When you give feedback
- In rare cases to address abuse or harm

### Ask Photos Queries

Ask Photos queries may be reviewed by humans to improve Photos, but Google says privacy protections are applied first.

Before human review, Google takes steps such as:

- Disconnecting queries from Google Accounts
- Using specialized processes to remove sensitive content

Reviewed queries do **not** include:

- Ask Photos responses
- Your photos or videos
- Account information used by Ask Photos to generate responses

Users can turn off query review in settings.

---

## Opting Out of Query Donation

You can opt out of human review of your Ask Photos queries at any time.

This is called opting out of **query donation**.

---

When you submit feedback from Ask Photos, Google attaches additional information to help diagnose issues and improve the product.

Included information may be:

### Conversation Transcript

> Your queries, and Ask Photos' responses from the active Ask Photos conversation.

### Optional Screenshot

You may include a screenshot, but Google warns it could contain personal photos if visible.

# Learn about information stored on your photos

With Google Photos, you can find certain information about a photo's metadata, which can be based on the International Press Telecommunications Council ( [IPTC](https://iptc.org/about-iptc/)) standards. IPTC includes copyright information, the creator's name, captions or image descriptions. Sometimes AI edits are included, but can be edited or removed.

This article focuses on photos that contain only IPTC metadata. IPTC is a long-standing format used by photographers and media organizations to describe and manage images, but the information can be edited or removed.

You may also find digital watermarks like SynthID embedded into images edited with generative AI which will [surface](https://blog.google/products/search/about-this-image-google-search/) on tools like Google Search or Lens. [Learn more about SynthID](https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/identifying-ai-generated-images-with-synthid/).

**Important:**

- Not all images have AI info. The AI info section is removed for photos that haven’t been edited with AI or that don’t follow IPTC standards.
- Note that IPTC metadata can be modified.

## Key Excerpts / Important Notices

> **Important:**
>
> - These features aren't available in all regions at this time. Functionality can vary by location. Some features may have additional eligibility requirements.

## Overview

Google Photos offers generative AI creation tools on Android, mainly:

- **Remix** — Reimagines photos using AI templates.
- **Photo to video** — Turns a still photo into a short dynamic video using effects or text prompts.

These tools are found in the **Create** tab of the Google Photos app and require eligible accounts, backed-up photos, and an internet connection.

---

## Availability & Usage Limits

- Features are **not available in all regions**.
- Functionality may vary depending on location.
- Some features may have extra eligibility requirements.
- Photos must be **backed up** before editing.
- A **network connection** is required.
- Users can generate only a **limited number of photos or videos per day**.
- Buying a **Google AI plan** increases the number of available generations.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

To use these AI creation tools:

- You must be **at least 13 years old**.
- You need a **personal Google Account**.
- These features are **not available** for:
  - Google Workspace accounts
  - School accounts

Additional age restrictions:

- **Remix** is available only to users **18 years old and over**.
- The **text prompt** feature in Photo to video is initially available only to users **18 years old and over**.

# Remix Your Photos

## What Remix Does

**Remix** is a tool in the Google Photos **Create** tab that uses AI templates to turn photos into unique, shareable creations.

It allows users to:

- Browse featured or full template selections
- Apply creative styles or visual effects
- Generate personalized or template-based AI creations
- Save, share, regenerate, or send feedback on results

---

## How to Use Remix on Android

1. Open the **Google Photos** app on your Android device.
2. At the bottom, tap **Create**.
3. Open **Remix** in one of two ways:
   - Under the **“Remix your photos”** header, select a template.
   - Tap **Remix** to view available templates.
4. If using the feature for the first time, an acknowledgement screen appears.
   - Tap **Try now** to continue.
5. Choose a template.
6. Tap **Generate**.
7. After generation, you can:
   - Tap **Save** to save the result.
   - Tap **Share** to share it.
   - Tap **Regenerate** to get a different result using the same template.
   - Tap **Send feedback** to share your experience and help improve the feature.

---

## Browsing Remix Templates

You can start from featured templates or browse all options.

### Start with a Featured Template

1. Under **“Remix your photos”**, find the featured template cards.
2. Scroll through available templates.

### Browse All Templates

1. Tap **View all**.
2. Scroll through every template option.

### Filter Templates

1. Tap specific category labels.
2. Scroll through the filtered template options.
3. To try another creative direction, tap a new category at any time.

---

## Remix Template Types

### Personalized Templates

Personalized templates are marked with a **badge**.

Key details:

- They use insights from your life to create unique and relevant outputs.
- Some personalized templates do **not require uploading an image**.
- For some templates, generation starts immediately after selection.
- To use badged templates, you must meet the **eligibility requirements for Gemini features in Photos**.

### Other Templates

Other templates apply specific creative styles or visual effects to generate a photo of you.

These templates usually require a **reference photo** before generation.

#### Upload a Reference Photo

To upload from your photo library:

1. Tap **Upload your own reference photo**.
2. Tap **Add photo**.
3. Choose from suggested photos or scroll through your library.
4. To change the uploaded photo, tap **Edit**

# Use Ask Photos for photos, information & assistance

You can ask Google Photos for photos, information, and assistance with a conversational agent.

When you search with Ask Photos, it starts by showing a page of relevant results. For simple searches like “beach” or “dog,” this is usually enough. But if you ask something complex like “Photos that’d make great phone backgrounds,” or “What did I eat on my trip to Barcelona?,” Gemini models continue to work in the background to narrow down the results set and highlight the most relevant results or information based on your search.

This feature is still experimental, so it may give unexpected or inaccurate results. When you leave feedback, you can help improve Ask Photos for everyone.

Edit your pictures in Google Photos just by asking. Describe the edits you want and watch the changes appear in seconds. Google Photos uses advanced Gemini model capabilities to understand, interpret, and apply your edits, so you don’t have to be a professional to get the picture you want. [Learn how to ask Photos to edit your pictures](https://support.google.com/photos/answer/14674995#ask_edits).

## Before you start

**Important:** This feature isn't available in all regions at this time.

Currently, Ask Photos is rolling out to a select number of countries and regions, and is available in several languages, with plans to continue expanding. Functionality can vary by location.

To use Ask Photos, you must meet the [Gemini features in Photos requirements](https://support.google.com/photos/answer/15344015#eligibility_for_gemini).

**Tip:** If you don’t have access to Ask Photos, you can still [use classic Search](https://support.google.com/photos/answer/15235862).

### Terms of Service

You agree that your use of Ask Photos is subject to the [Google Terms of Service](https://policies.google.com/terms) and the [Generative AI Prohibited Use Policy](https://policies.google.com/terms/generative-ai/use-policy).

We’re committed to protecting your privacy. [Learn how Ask Photos keeps your data private](https://support.google.com/photos/answer/15344015).
