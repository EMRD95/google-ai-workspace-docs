# Collaborate with Gemini in Gmail — Markdown Summary

**Source:** Google Gmail Help — “Collaborate with Gemini in Gmail”  
**Platform covered:** Computer  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/mail/answer/14355636?hl=en

## Overview

Gemini in Gmail lets eligible users use AI directly inside Gmail through the **Ask Gemini** side panel. It can help summarize emails, draft and suggest replies, search prior emails, reference Google Drive files, interact with Google Calendar, generate images, and use Gems for repetitive tasks.

---

## Feature Availability

- Requires an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan.
- Supported languages vary by feature.
- Google notes a change for some subscribers:

> “We're working to make it easier to use AI directly in Gmail. As such, Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra subscribers in the US may no longer see Ask Gemini in Gmail or be able to open the Gemini in Gmail side panel. Powerful AI experiences in Gmail, such as AI Overviews and ‘Help me write,’ continue to be available directly in the product.”

---

## What Gemini in Gmail Can Do

With Gemini in Gmail, you can:

- Summarize an email thread.
- Suggest responses to an email thread.
- Draft an email.
- Find information from previous emails.
- Find information from your Google Drive files.
- Get information about Google Calendar events.
- Create events in Google Calendar.
- Generate images.
- Use Gems for repetitive or specialized tasks.

---

## Get Started with Gemini in Gmail

1. On your computer, open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/).
2. At the top right, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. In the side panel, either:
   - Select a suggested prompt, or
   - Write your own prompt.
4. Press **Enter**.

### Use a Suggested Prompt

1. Select a suggestion from the list.
   - To see more options, select **More suggestions**.
2. At the bottom, in the prompt box, replace the example text with your own information.
3. Press **Enter**.

### Enter Your Own Prompt

1. At the bottom, in the prompt box, enter a prompt.
2. Press **Enter**.

### Clear Gemini History

To remove your history:

1. Click **More options**.
2. Select **Clear history**.

**Important tip:**

> “To avoid losing your history, copy a suggestion into your email.”

You lose your Gemini conversation history when:

- You refresh your browser.
- You close and reopen Gmail.

## Overview
Gemini in Gmail helps you:

- Summarize an email thread
- Suggest responses to an email thread
- Draft an email
- Find information from previous emails
- Find information from your Google Drive files
- Get information about Google Calendar events
- Create events in Google Calendar

**Feature availability**  
- Requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan ([learn more](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/13952129))

## Get Started with Gemini in Gmail

1. Open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/) on your computer.
2. Click **Ask Gemini** in the top-right corner.
3. In the side panel, choose a suggested prompt or type your own.
   - **Use a suggestion**: Select a suggestion (click **More suggestions** for additional options), replace the placeholder text in the prompt box, and press **Enter**.
   - **Write a custom prompt**: Type your request and press **Enter**.
4. (Optional) Clear history via **More options** → **Clear history**.

> **Tip:** Conversation history is lost when you refresh the browser or close/reopen Gmail. Copy any important output into your email to avoid losing it.

| **Copy** | Copy a response to clipboard |

| **Good suggestion / Bad suggestion** | Give positive or negative feedback |
| **Gem** | Chat with a premade or custom Gem for repetitive tasks |

# Draft Emails with Gemini in Gmail — Summary

**Source:** [Gmail Help: Draft emails with Gemini in Gmail](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13955415?hl=en)  
**Platform covered:** Computer

---

## Overview

In Gmail, Gemini’s **“Help me write”** feature can help you draft or revise emails directly in the compose window.

You can use it to:

- **Generate a new email draft**, such as:
  - A birthday invitation
  - An introduction to a potential business contact
- **Refine existing text** for tone and clarity, such as turning a rough outline into a formal email.

---

## Requirements

You can use **“Help me write”** with:

- **An eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan**
  - Available globally in multiple supported languages.
- **A personal Google Account**
  - Available in supported languages.
  - Restricted to the **US only**.

> Learn more: [Supported languages for Google Workspace with Gemini](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/14925782#zippy=%2Cgemini-summary-cards)

---

## How to Draft an Email with “Help me write”

1. On your computer, open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/).
2. At the top left, click **Compose**.
   - Alternatively, you can **reply to an email** or **open an existing draft**.
3. In the compose window:
   - Enter your message, **or**
   - Leave the field blank.
4. At the bottom, in the prompt bar, enter your prompt.
   - If the prompt bar is not visible, click **Help me write**.
5. Click **Create**.

---

## How to Refine Your Draft

To polish or revise a draft, you can either enter a new instruction in the prompt bar or use preset refinement options.

### Example refinement prompts

> "Make it more professional."

> "Mention the Friday deadline."

### Built-in refinement options

Use these options in the prompt bar:

- **Formalize**
- **Friendly**
- **Shorten**

### Switching between drafts

**Tip:** To switch between different generated drafts, use:

- **Undo**
- **Redo**

These buttons appear at the right side of the prompt bar.

---

## How to Improve Gemini Draft Results

For the most relevant and helpful drafts, make prompts **specific and detailed**.

| What to include in your prompt | Example |
|---|---|
| Mention the person or group you are writing to. | “Write an apology to **my child's teacher** for their absence.” |
| Clarify the subject and required action. | “ **Decline the meeting** on Thursday.” |
| Use tone words like “formal,” “casual,” or other adjectives. | “Write a **formal** invitation to a business partner.” |
| To improve factual accuracy, mention a specific email or file. | “Write a summary of **the Q4 earnings email** I received last week.” |

---

## Personalization: How “Help me write” Uses Your Data

**Important:** To have “Help me write” match your tone and writing style, your drafts must be in **English**.

When you use **“Help me write,”** Gemini may proactively use relevant information from:

- Other emails
- Google Drive files

Examples of details it may reference include:

- Flight times
- Soccer schedules
- Hotel booking codes

This allows Gemini to draft a personalized response that includes relevant details and better matches your tone and style **without requiring explicit instructions**.

Google states that this information is only used to improve drafts when you use **“Help me write.”**

### View sources used by Gemini

**Tip:** To see which emails and documents Gemini used to personalize a response, click **Sources**.

---

## Giving Feedback About a Draft

Google Workspace with Gemini is still learning and may not always support your request correctly.

If a generated draft is inaccurate or appears unsafe, you can submit feedback.

### Steps to submit feedback

1. Below the generated text, click:
   - **Good suggestion**, or
   - **Bad suggestion**
2. Enter your feedback.
3. Click **Submit**.

### Feedback with screenshots

To send feedback with screenshots:

1. Click **Help** in the top right of Gmail.
2. Select **Send feedback to Google**.

Learn more: [Send feedback in Gmail](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/9212652)

### Reporting legal issues

To report a legal issue, [create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

## What AI Overview in Gmail Search Does

Gmail can generate an **AI Overview** when you search your inbox using **natural language questions** or **key phrases**, such as:

- `"When is my flight to Hawaii?"`
- `"Find the latest Cymbal Team Meeting notes."`

Based on relevant emails, Gmail can:

- Provide a **key fact or summary** directly above search results.
- **Synthesize information across multiple emails** into one answer.
- Save time by reducing the need to open and read individual messages.

AI Overviews work best with **descriptive natural language queries**. If no overview appears, try asking a more specific question or using a more descriptive phrase.

# Use Suggested Replies in Gmail (Workspace Experiments) – Summary

**Important context:** This feature is part of **Google Workspace Experiments**, a trusted tester program for new AI features. It uses Gemini to suggest personalized email replies. Access may be limited; check by replying to an email and looking for suggestions at the bottom. [See where Workspace Experiments features are available](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13607340).

---

## Requirements

- **Workspace smart features** must be turned on in Gmail settings. [Learn more](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/15604322).
- **Google Account language** must be set to English. [Change language](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/32047).
- Even if requirements are met, suggested replies are not always available.

---

## How to Use

1. Open [Gmail](https://mail.google.com/) on a computer.
2. Open an email.
3. At the bottom of the email, click the **suggested reply**.
   - You can edit the text before sending.
4. Click **Send**.

> **Note:** The feature is currently available on Computer, Android, iPhone & iPad (device switcher present).

---

## Personalization Details

> *“Suggested Replies with personalization are available in English.”*

- Gemini draws details from your **emails and Drive files** (e.g., flight times, hotel booking codes, soccer schedules) to craft a relevant response.
- The response matches your **tone and writing style**.
- Click **“Sources”** above a suggestion to see which emails/docs were used. You cannot edit the source list.

---

## Giving Feedback

Google Workspace with Gemini is still learning; feedback helps improve it. **Do not submit personal, confidential, or sensitive information** because feedback may be read by humans.

- **Quick feedback:** Click **Send feedback** (icon) at the top of a suggested reply, write your comment, and **Submit**.
- **Feedback with screenshots:** Click **Help** (question mark icon) → **Send feedback to Google**. [Learn more](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/9212652).
- **Legal issues:** [Create a request](https://support.google.com/legal/answer/3110420).

---

## Turning Off Workspace Experiments

- To disable these AI features, you must **exit Workspace Experiments entirely**.
- **This action is permanent** – you lose access to all Workspace Experiments features and cannot rejoin.
- [How to exit](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/13447104#labs-opt-out&exit-labs-docs&exit-labs-gmail).

## Important Disclaimers

> *“Workspace Experiments feature suggestions don’t represent Google’s views, and should not be attributed to Google.”*

- **Not professional advice** – don’t rely on it for medical, legal, financial, or other professional matters.
- Suggestions may be **inaccurate or inappropriate**.
- **Don’t include personal, confidential, or sensitive information** in prompts.
- Google uses Workspace Experiments data to **improve products, services, and machine learning technologies**.
- Your data may be **read, rated, annotated, or reviewed by humans**. Where Google uses Google-selected input, the content is aggregated/pseudonymized before human review, unless provided as feedback.
