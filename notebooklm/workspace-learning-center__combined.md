Use this when you want Gemini to generate new text at a specific location in a document.

## What Is a Prompt?

A prompt is the instruction or request you give Gemini.

> “A prompt tells Gemini what you want it to generate.”

# Recommended Google Workspace Workflows for R&D

---

## 1. Store Files in One Location

**Use:** Google Drive or shared drives  
**Goal:** Securely store, access, and collaborate on product-related files.

### What to store centrally

- Product designs
- Quality guidelines
- Key Performance Indicators, or **KPIs**
- Mock-ups
- Confidential product files
- Feedback and approval materials

### Benefits

- Everyone accesses the latest files from one secure location.
- Drive shows which files have changed after feedback or approvals.
- Internal teams and external clients can securely access files remotely on any device.
- Shared drive files remain with the team, even if an individual contributor leaves.

### Add files to Drive

If using the latest Chrome browser or Mozilla Firefox:

1. Drag files or folders from your computer into a Drive folder.

Or:

1. Click **New**.
2. Select **File upload** or **Folder upload**.
3. Choose the file or folder to upload.

### Add files to a shared drive

**Requirement:** At least **Contributor** access.

1. Go to [drive.google.com](https://drive.google.com/).
2. On the left, click **Shared drives**.
3. Double-click a shared drive.
4. At the top left, click **New**.
5. Choose the type of file or folder to create or upload.

# Tips to Write Prompts for Gemini  
**Source:** Google Workspace Learning Center — “Tips to write prompts for Gemini”  
**Context:** Gemini for Google Workspace productivity guide

---

## Core Purpose

To get better results from **Gemini for Google Workspace**, users should follow basic prompt-writing best practices. Good prompts should be natural, specific, contextual, and broken into manageable tasks when needed.

---

## Key Prompt-Writing Best Practices

### 1. Use Natural Language

Write prompts conversationally, as if speaking to a friend or colleague.

| Instead of... | Try this... |
|---|---|
| `"Training plan."` | `"Write a training plan for the sales team for the launch of a brand new product."` |

**Takeaway:** Avoid short, vague fragments. Ask in complete, natural sentences.

---

### 2. Be Clear and Concise

Gemini can follow precise instructions, so avoid ambiguity.

| Instead of... | Try this... |
|---|---|
| `"Marketing talking points."` | `"Give me 12 thoughtful questions to ask a Chief Marketing Officer on their strategy for 2024."` |

**Takeaway:** Specify the exact output you want, including quantity, audience, and topic.

---

### 3. Provide Context

The more context you provide, the more useful Gemini’s response can be.

| Instead of... | Try this... |
|---|---|
| `"Write about a sales job."` | `"Write a job description for a _[job title]_, including the required skills and experience, as well as a summary of _[company name]_ and the position."` |

**Takeaway:** Include relevant background such as role, company, audience, goal, or constraints.

---

### 4. Use Specific and Relevant Keywords

Gemini can understand individual words and phrases, so using relevant keywords helps it generate more accurate and informative responses.

| Instead of... | Try this... |
|---|---|
| `"Create project plan."` | `"Create a project plan for the launch of a brand new product. The timeframe should be from now until June 2024."` |

**Takeaway:** Include keywords tied to the task, deliverable, subject, and timeline.

---

### 5. Break Down Complex Tasks into Separate Prompts

If a task involves several related actions, split it into multiple prompts.

**Reason:** This helps Gemini better understand each step and provide more useful responses.

## What Is the Gemini Side Panel?

The **Gemini side panel** lets users chat with Gemini while working inside Google Workspace apps.

It can assist with:

- Summarizing long email threads and suggesting responses.
- Creating and refining text using the context of the current document.
- Summarizing spreadsheets and creating tables or formulas.
- Generating slides, creating images, and summarizing presentations.
- Answering questions about files stored in Drive.

The key benefit is that Gemini can use context from emails, documents, files, and other Workspace content **without requiring users to switch applications or tabs**.
