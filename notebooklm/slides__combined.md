## Overview

Gemini in Google Slides helps users create and improve presentations directly inside Slides. With Gemini, you can:

- Generate images.
- Generate new slides.
- Summarize a presentation.
- Write or rewrite content.
- Reference Google Drive files while writing or creating slides.

---

## Feature Availability

- Requires an eligible **Google Workspace** or **Google AI** plan.
- Supported languages vary by feature.
- Gems are not supported in all languages.

---

## Getting Started with Gemini in Slides

1. Open a presentation in [Google Slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation) on your computer.
2. At the top right, click **Ask Gemini**.
3. In the side panel:
   - Select a suggested prompt, or
   - Write your own prompt.
4. Press **Enter**.

### Using Suggested Prompts

1. Choose from the suggestions list.
   - **Tip:** Select **More suggestions** for more options.
2. Replace the example text in the prompt box with your own information.
3. Press **Enter**.

### Entering Your Own Prompt

1. Type your prompt in the prompt box at the bottom.
2. Press **Enter**.

### Conversation History

You can remove your Gemini history by selecting:

> **More options → Clear history**

**Important tip:** Insert a suggestion into your presentation if you don’t want to lose it. Conversation history is lost when:

- You refresh your browser.
- You close and reopen the presentation.
- Your computer goes offline.

## Generate Images Based on Slide Content

To create images with Gemini:

1. Open a presentation in Google Slides.
2. Click **Ask Gemini**.
3. In the side panel, click:

> **Tools → Create images**

4. Enter a prompt describing the desired image.
5. Click **Submit**.
6. After the image is generated, optionally:
   - Select **Preview** to view it.
   - Select **Generate more** for additional options.
7. In the preview window, click **Insert** to place the image in a slide.
8. To edit the generated image, enter a new prompt describing the adjustment.

**Important limitation:** Generated images are for use only within Google Slides.

# Generate & Insert an Image

## Where It Works

- Google Slides
- Google Vids

## Steps

1. On your computer, open:
   - a presentation in **Google Slides**, or
   - a video in **Google Vids**.
2. Click the slide or scene where you want to add the image.
3. Open the image generation tool:
   - In **Slides**:  
     **Insert → Help me visualize → Image**
   - In **Vids**:  
     **Insert → Generate an image**
4. In the right panel, enter a prompt.
5. Optionally customize:
   - **Style** using **Add a style**
   - **Aspect ratio**
6. Click **Create**.
7. Preview the generated image by clicking it.
8. To insert it:
   - Click the image in the right panel.
   - In the preview window, click **Insert**.

## Example Prompts

Original examples from the article:

- “A beautiful photograph of a Hawaiian beach at sunset with palm trees in the distance.”
- “A plate of freshly baked blueberry muffins on a wooden kitchen table next to a large window.”
- “Watercolor painting of hydrangeas in a water pitcher.”
- “A futuristic cityscape with skyscrapers and flying cars set at night with HD details and shadows.”

## Aspect Ratio Options

| Option | Ratio |
|---|---:|
| **Square** | 1:1 |
| **Wide** | 16:9 |
| **Tall** | 9:16 |

## Prompting Tip

For better results, avoid figurative or vague language. Include concrete details such as:

- Subject
- Setting
- Distance to subject
- Materials
- Background

Example:

> “A close up of a boat made out of wood on a lake surrounded by trees at sunrise.“

## Availability & Eligible Plans

Gemini presentation generation in Google Slides is available for:

- **Google AI Ultra and Google AI Pro accounts**
- **Gemini Alpha**
  - Access is controlled by your administrator.
  - If you do not see the feature, your admin may not have enabled Alpha features.
- **Google Workspace Experiments**
  - A trusted tester program for personal accounts to try new AI features.

## What Gemini Can Do in Google Slides

Gemini in Google Slides can generate a **fully editable presentation** with:

- Text
- Images
- A presentation structure
- A slide-by-slide plan
- Optional use of reference files
- Optional style matching from an existing deck

Gemini guides users through a short, tailored conversation to refine the presentation before generating slides.

---

## How to Generate a New Presentation

### 1. Open Google Slides

Go to [Google Slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation) on your computer.

### 2. Start a Presentation

From the start screen:

- Click **“Presentation”**

Or, in a blank presentation with no existing slides:

- Click **Ask Gemini** in the top-right corner.

### 3. Describe the Presentation

In the Gemini side panel, enter what you want to create.

Include details such as:

- Topic
- Audience
- Number of slides
- Tone or style
- Design preferences
- Goals or desired outcome

Example:

> “Create a 5-slide presentation on quantum computing for a general audience with a modern design.”

---

## Optional: Add Reference Sources

You can upload files from Google Drive for Gemini to use as references.

### Steps

1. Click **Add sources**
2. Select **Add from Drive**
3. Choose the file you want to use
4. Click **Add**

Gemini may also be allowed to search and reference relevant content from:

- The web
- Google Drive
- Chat
- Gmail

Source access depends on settings. Users can manage source settings through Google Workspace controls.

---

## Optional: Match an Existing Presentation Style

You can upload an existing deck so Gemini can match its presentation style.

### Steps

1. Click **Match presentation style**
2. Select **Add from Drive**
3. Choose the deck you want Gemini to use
4. Click **Add**

This helps Gemini align the generated presentation with an existing visual style or format.

---

## Refining the Presentation Plan

After the initial prompt and optional sources/style deck, Gemini may ask follow-up questions or clarification questions.

- These questions vary based on the topic and references provided.
- You can answer them to improve the output.
- You may also skip them.

After this step, click **Next**.

---

## Gemini’s Generated Presentation Plan

Before creating the deck, Gemini generates a plan that includes:

- **Overview**
- **Sources**
  - A list of documents used as references
- **Steps**
  - The content outline for each slide

---

## Editing the Presentation Outline

Before approving the final deck, you can modify Gemini’s outline.

### Edit a Slide Title or Content

- Click the slide outline.
- Edit the brief description or title.

### Add a New Slide

- Hover over any title.
- Click **Add step**.
- Enter the new slide title.

### Remove a Slide

- Hover over the slide.
- Click **Delete**.

### Apply Changes

- Click **Update plan** to have Gemini regenerate the outline based on your edits.

---

## Generate the Final Presentation

Once satisfied with the plan:

1. Click **Approve**
2. Keep Google Slides open while Gemini generates the presentation

Important:

- You may switch to other tabs or windows.
- Do **not** close the Google Slides tab until generation is complete.

---

## After the Presentation Is Generated

Once Gemini creates the deck, you can:

- Ask Gemini to refine the content **one slide at a time**
- Ask Gemini to generate additional slides
- Manually edit any deck element
- Ask Gemini to add or edit images

Related capabilities include:

- Generating individual slides with Gemini
- Generating and editing images in Google Slides and Vids
