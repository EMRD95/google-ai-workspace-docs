## Overview

**Learn About** is a generative AI learning companion from Google designed to support interactive learning. It combines **Google Search**, **Gemini**, and teaching principles to help users explore topics, study, and engage with educational content.

> “Learn About is a generative AI learning companion that brings Google Search, Gemini, and teaching principles to provide interactive learning experiences.”

Users can get started by typing a prompt, selecting a topic, or uploading images/materials.

---

## What Learn About Can Do

Learn About can help users:

- Explore topics through:
  - Detailed explanations
  - Images
  - Videos
  - Learning cards such as:
    - “common misconceptions”
    - “stop and think”
    - “test your knowledge”
- Get clear help for studies and assignments.
- Use quizzes and interactive guides to improve learning.
- Use **text, images, or uploaded materials** to get assistance.
- Discover new topics aligned with personal goals and interests.

> “With Learn About, you can expand your knowledge by creating an engaging learning journey that fits your needs and preferences.”

# 1. What AI Mode Is

**AI Mode** is Google Search’s advanced AI-powered search experience. It lets users:

- Ask broad or complex questions.
- Receive AI-generated answers.
- Ask follow-up questions.
- Explore cited web links and supporting sources.
- Go deeper than standard **AI Overviews**.

AI Mode is designed for questions that may otherwise require multiple searches, such as:

- Exploring a new concept.
- Comparing options.
- Researching multiple aspects of a topic.
- Asking follow-up questions in context.

Google says AI Mode relies on Search’s understanding of web information and high-quality web content to improve factuality. If Google does not have enough confidence in an AI-generated response, AI Mode may instead show a set of web links.

---

# 2. How AI Mode Works

AI Mode uses a technique Google calls **“query fan-out.”**

## Query Fan-Out

AI Mode:

1. Breaks a question into subtopics.
2. Searches for each subtopic simultaneously.
3. Searches across multiple data sources.
4. Combines the results into a single AI-powered response.
5. Provides links so users can explore further.

This allows AI Mode to gather more relevant web content than a single traditional search query.

---

# 3. Accuracy, Limitations, and Fact-Checking

Google emphasizes that AI Mode is an early-stage AI product and may make mistakes.

Possible issues include:

- Misinterpreting web content.
- Missing context.
- Producing inaccurate or incomplete responses.
- Personalization errors.

## How to Double-Check AI Mode Responses

Google recommends:

- **Check important information in more than one place.**
  - Use the AI source links.
  - Review other Google Search results.
- **Ask multiple versions of your question.**
  - Ask for different perspectives.
  - Ask for more detail.
- **Send feedback** if something appears incorrect.

## What Generative AI Is

Generative AI is a **machine learning model** designed to create new content by recognizing and applying patterns learned during training.

### Important Clarifications

- Generative AI is **not human**.
- It **does not think for itself**.
- It **does not feel emotions**.
- It is effective at **finding and reproducing patterns**.

### How AI Use Has Evolved

Previously, AI was mainly used to:

- Understand information
- Recommend information

Now, generative AI can also help create new content, including:

- **Images**
- **Music**
- **Code**
- Other creative or informational outputs

---

## How Machine Learning Models Are Trained

Machine learning models learn through **observation and pattern matching**, a process called **training**.

### Example: Learning What a Sneaker Is

To understand what a sneaker is, a model may be trained on **millions of photos of sneakers**. Over time, it recognizes common features such as:

- Footwear worn by humans
- Laces
- Soles
- Logos

### How Training Enables Generation

A trained model can:

1. Take an input like “Generate an image of sneakers with a goat charm.”
2. Connect what it has learned about **sneakers**, **goats**, and **charms**.
3. Generate a new image, even if it has never seen that exact image before.

---

## How Large Language Models Power Generative AI

Generative AI and **Large Language Models**, or **LLMs**, are part of the same broader technology area.

### Difference Between Generative AI and LLMs

- **Generative AI** can be trained on many types of data.
- **LLMs** use **words** as their main source of training data.

### How LLM-Based Experiences Work

Experiences powered by LLMs, such as **Gemini** and **AI Overviews**, predict likely next words based on:

- The user’s prompt
- The text generated so far
- Patterns learned during training

This flexibility allows LLMs to produce creative responses.

### Example

If prompted to complete:

> “Harry [blank],”

an LLM might predict:

- “Styles”
- “Potter”

depending on context and learned patterns.

Google emphasizes that generative AI should be used as a **starting point or assistant**, not as a full replacement for human creativity, judgment, or responsibility.

Example:

Examples:

Users should use generative AI responsibly and review Google’s:

## How to Use AI Mode on a Computer

### 1. Open AI Mode

Go to Google Search and select **AI Mode**, or visit:

> [google.com/ai](https://google.com/ai)

---

### 2. Enter a Request

Type your question or task in the **Ask anything** bar near the bottom of the screen.

Example prompts from the article:

> “Deep cleaning appointment for an apartment move out.”

> “Reservation for 6 on Friday with vegetarian options”

---

### 3. Select “Check for me”

Choose **“Check for me”** to let AI Mode help complete the request.

---

### 4. Answer Follow-Up Questions if Needed

AI Mode may ask clarifying questions to better understand the request.

> **Note:** If your initial request has enough detail, this step will be skipped automatically.

---

### 5. Google May Call Businesses on Your Behalf

If a business has limited information online, Google may call the business to gather the details you requested.

Important call-related details:

- Only the details you shared and confirmed are provided to businesses.
- Calls are used to get information not available online, such as:
  - Earliest appointment times
  - Current inventory
  - Pricing or availability

If you do **not** want Google to call businesses:

> type “don’t call” into the prompt box and then select **Enter**.

---

### 6. Review the Request

Before AI Mode proceeds:

- Review the request details.
- Choose whether to receive an email notification.
- If calls are made, the email will notify you when results are ready.

---

### 7. Confirm the Request

Select:

> **Looks good**

This allows AI Mode to proceed.

---

### 8. View Results

Timing depends on the type of request:

- **Web results:** usually take a few minutes
- **Call results:** can take up to **30 minutes**

You can leave AI Mode and return later.

A **blue dot** appears on the thread when results are ready in AI Mode history.

---

### 9. Email Notifications

If you opted into email notifications and calls were made, you’ll receive an email when your results are ready.

---

### 10. Stop the Request

To end all work on a request, select:

> **Stop**

---

## Important Behavior Details

> **Important:**
>
> - AI Mode is designed to provide the most accurate and up-to-date information. If a business does not have its information listed online, such as the earliest available appointment or current inventory in stock, Google will make a call to get those answers for you.
> - AI Mode can process multiple requests simultaneously. If you provide unrelated details during an active search, AI Mode will suggest starting a new thread so each task is handled independently.
> - Google only calls for the specific information you've requested, keeping interactions brief, professional, and focused. Business owners can learn more about managing [these automated calls](https://support.google.com/business/answer/16190256).
