# Create a Form with Gemini in Google Forms — Summary

**Source:** Google Docs Editors Help  
**URL:** https://support.google.com/docs/answer/16346789?hl=en

---

## Overview

Google Forms supports a Gemini-powered feature called **“Help me create a form”** that can generate new forms from a prompt. It can also create forms using files from Google Drive, including **Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, and PDFs**.

> **Important:** This feature requires an eligible Google Workspace or Google AI plan.  
> [Learn about Gemini features and plans](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13952129).

### Availability

- The feature is **rolling out gradually**, so it may not be available to all users yet.
- It is currently available on **desktop**.
- Creating quizzes with Gemini has additional availability limits:
  - Available as part of **Gemini Alpha**
  - Admin-controlled access
  - Also available through **Google Workspace Experiments**
  - **Only available in English**

---

## Use Gemini to Create a Form

### Steps

1. On your computer, open a new form in [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/u/0/).
2. In the window that opens, enter a prompt describing the form you want.
   - Alternatively, in the toolbar, click **Help me create a form**.
   - To get prompt ideas, use suggested prompt tiles or click **Shuffle**.
3. Enter a prompt.

   Example:

   > “Create a simple RSVP form for a neighborhood potluck, collecting name, allergy info, and what dish they'll bring.”

   **Tip:** More detailed prompts usually produce better results.

4. Click **Create**.
5. Optional: After Gemini generates a draft, you can:
   - **Edit your prompt**
     1. At the top of the generated draft, click the prompt.
     2. Edit your prompt.
     3. Click **Submit**.
   - **Create a new version**
     - Click **Recreate** to generate another version for the same prompt or use case.

     > **Important:** After you create a new version, you can’t go back to the previous version.

6. When satisfied with the generated draft, click **Create form**.

### Current Limitations

- Gemini does **not yet support creating forms with multiple sections**.
- Gemini does **not yet support editing an existing form** with “Help me create a form.”
  - If used, the current form will be **replaced with the newly generated form**.

## Prompt Examples for Creating Forms

Google recommends including details to generate more complex and useful forms.

Example prompts:

> “Design a post-event survey to collect attendee feedback on a business networking event.”

> “Develop a 10-question quiz to assess basic knowledge of biology for high school students.”

> “Create an event planning form for a weekend yoga retreat. Include options for accommodation preferences (shared or private room), dietary restrictions, and interest in optional add-on activities. Make sure you have one question which asks which weekend in April 2025 works best for potential attendees.”

## Create a Quiz with Gemini in Forms

Gemini can also generate quizzes in Google Forms using **“Help me create.”**

### Feature Availability

- Available as part of **Gemini Alpha**
- Controlled by your organization’s administrator
  - If unavailable, your administrator may not have [Alpha features enabled](https://support.google.com/a/answer/14170809)
- Also available through [Google Workspace Experiments](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/13447104)
- Available **only in English**

### What Gemini Can Generate for Quizzes

- Based on your prompt, Gemini may generate either a **form** or a **quiz**.
- When a quiz is generated, Gemini also:
  - Marks correct answers
  - Assigns point values
- Supported question types include:
  - Multiple choice
  - Checkbox
  - Grid
  - Short answer
  - Long answer

> **Tip:** “Help me create” can also use your documents or curriculum to generate a quiz.

### Steps to Create a Quiz

1. On your computer, open a new form in [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/u/0/).
2. Enter a prompt describing your quiz.
   - Or use the toolbar and click **Help me create a form**.

   Sample prompt:

   > “Create a 10-question quiz on biology for high-school students based on my course curriculum.“

3. Click **Create**.
4. Review the generated quiz.
5. To review correct answers and point values, check the box next to **“Show answer key.”**
6. Optional: After generating a quiz, you can:
   - **Edit your prompt**
     1. At the top of the generated draft, click the prompt.
     2. Edit your prompt.
     3. Click **Submit**.
   - **Create a new version**
     - Click **Recreate**.

     > **Tip:** After you create a new version, you can’t go back to the previous version.

7. Click **Create quiz**.
8. Optional: After the quiz is created:
   - Edit questions or change the answer key.
   - Learn more: [Create and mark quizzes with Google Forms](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/7032287)

## How to Use Gemini to Create a Form

### Step-by-Step

1. Open a new form in [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/u/0/).
2. In the opening window, **enter a prompt** that describes your form (or click the toolbar icon **Help me create a form**).
3. **Example prompt**:
   > “Create a simple RSVP form for a neighborhood potluck, collecting name, allergy info, and what dish they'll bring.”
4. Click **Create**.
5. After generation, you can:
   - **Edit the prompt** (click the prompt at the top, modify, then click Recreate).
   - **Create a new version** (click Recreate).  
     > **“Important:** After you create a new version, you can’t go back to the previous version.”
6. When satisfied, click **Create form**.

> **Tip:** Explore suggested prompt tiles or click **Shuffle** for ideas. More detail in your prompt often yields better results.

> **Important:** The feature **does not yet support** editing an existing form — it will replace the existing form entirely. Also not yet supported: quiz settings or forms with multiple sections.

## Creating a Quiz with Gemini

- When a quiz is generated, **correct answers and point values** are automatically marked. Question types can include multiple‑choice, checkbox, grid, short‑answer, and long‑answer.
- **“Help me create”** can also generate quizzes from your own documents or curriculum.

### Steps

1. Open a new form in Google Forms.
2. Enter a quiz prompt, e.g.:
   > “Create a 10-question quiz on biology for high-school students based on my course curriculum.”
3. Click **Create**.
4. Review the generated quiz. Check **“Show answer key”** to see correct answers and points.
5. To refine:
   - **Edit the prompt** (click it, edit, click Submit).
   - **Recreate** for a fresh version (previous version lost).
6. Click **Create quiz**.
7. After generation, you can:
   - Edit questions or change the answer key ([learn more](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/7032287)).
   - In the “Questions” tab, click **Suggest questions** to get more questions based on the existing quiz.
8. Click **Publish** to distribute, then manage responders if needed.

## Use Gemini to Summarize Form Responses

Gemini can summarize responses to **open text questions** in Google Forms.

### Important Limits

- Summaries are available only when there are **3 to 200 responses**.
- Summaries cannot be generated for **quiz responses**.
- Summaries are not generated for direct personal-info style questions, such as:
  - Names
  - Addresses

### Steps to View a Response Summary

1. On a computer, open a form in [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/).
2. At the top, click **Responses**.
3. In the summary block, summaries for each text question are generated automatically.

### Optional Actions

- Click **Retry** to generate a new response summary.
- Click **Copy** to copy the summary.
- If new responses arrive while the summary is open, click **Refresh with latest responses**.

### Notes

- Generated summaries are **not saved**.
- Users must copy them if they want to retain them.
- They can also regenerate summaries later.

## Generate & Refine Themes from Responses

Gemini can identify, group, and analyze common themes in Google Forms text responses using Google Sheets.

### Best-Use Range and Limits

- Works best with **8–200 responses**.
- If a form has **more than 200 responses**, the theme generation option becomes unavailable:
  - The button is grayed out.
  - An error message appears.
- Themes are generated using **AI functions in Google Sheets** and are subject to AI function limits.
- If responses are **fewer than or equal to 8**, or **over 500**, the summary may appear but the theme button is grayed out.

---

## Steps to Generate Themes

1. On a computer, open a form in [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/).
2. At the top, click **Responses**.
3. Scroll to the text question where you want to generate themes.
4. Below the question’s summary, click **See theme percentages**.
5. To modify generated themes, click **Modify themes**.

### Modify Themes

- To add a theme, click **Add**.
- To delete a theme, click **Delete category** next to the theme name.

### Get Detailed Theme Analysis

Click **See theme percentages** again to open a new Google Sheet.

The generated Google Sheet includes:

- A **pie chart** showing the distribution of themes.
- A **summary response theme count** showing how many responses are associated with each theme.

### Error Handling

If an error occurs in Google Sheets:

1. Open the Sheets side panel.
2. Click **Refresh & Generate**.

## 📝 Summarizing Responses with Gemini

### Automatic Summary Generation

- **Trigger**: Once a question receives **3 to 200 responses**, a summary block appears automatically for each text question in the **Responses** tab.
- **Where**: `Responses` → look for the summary block under each text question.

### How to Use

1. Open the form and click **Responses**.
2. Summary for each text question is generated automatically (no manual action).
3. **Optional actions**:
   - **Retry**: Click to generate a new summary.
   - **Copy**: Click to copy the summary to clipboard.
   - **Refresh with latest responses**: If new answers arrive while the summary is open, click to update.

> **Important notes**:
> - Summaries **are not saved** – you must copy or regenerate them.
> - Summaries **won’t be generated** for direct questions (e.g., names, addresses).
> - **Quiz responses** are not supported – no summaries for quiz forms.

### Step‑by‑Step

   - A **pie chart** of theme distribution.
   - A **response theme count** table mapping each theme to its number of responses.

> **Tip for extreme counts**: If responses are **≤8 or >500**, the text summary still shows, but the theme percentage button is **grayed out**.
