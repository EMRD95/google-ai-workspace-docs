## What Google Vids AI Avatars Are

Google Vids AI avatars are **digital presenters** that can speak from a script with natural-looking expressions. They are designed to help teams create professional videos quickly without requiring:

- A camera
- A studio
- A live presenter
- Traditional video shoots
- Complex production logistics

They can be used to create videos that are consistent, polished, scalable, and easier to personalize.

## How AI Avatars Work in Google Vids

The workflow is simple:

1. **Select an avatar**
   - Choose from a library of professionally designed avatars.
   - Or create a custom avatar using prompts and settings.

2. **Write a script or prompt**
   - Enter the message the avatar should deliver.
   - Prompts can also define avatar appearance or scene context.

3. **Generate the video**
   - Google Vids creates the AI avatar video in minutes.

4. **Edit within Google Vids**
   - Avatars integrate with scripts, timelines, media, captions, and existing Workspace assets.

## Recording Your Own Voiceover

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc).
2. Choose **Record** from the start menu, or click the Record icon in an existing video.
3. (Optional) Enter a script in the sidebar for your scene.
4. Under **Recording options**, select one of:
   - **Camera**
   - **Camera & screen**
   - **Screen**
   - **Voiceover**
5. Click **Start recording** at the bottom of the side panel.
   - For Camera or Screen & camera options, the script can be used in **teleprompter mode**. [Learn more about Vids recording studio](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/14956207).

---

## Generating an AI Voiceover

### Important Notes
- Generated voiceovers use AI models; voice options may change with model updates, but existing voiceovers won’t change.

### Steps (Scene-by-Scene or All Scenes)
1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos?usp=hc).
2. Click **Voiceover** on the right.
3. Choose **Current scene** or **All scenes**.
4. Write your script in the text box (plain text only, max **2,500 characters**, no emojis/symbols).
5. (Optional) Use bracketed tags for emotional cues, pacing, non-verbal sounds:
   - `[Read this like you're excited]: Your script here.`
   - `This [pause] is amazing!`
   - `[laugh] That was a great point.`
6. Click **Insert voiceover**.
7. Select a voice from the **Voiceover** options, then click **Select** → **Insert voiceover**.
8. To edit an existing voiceover: select the scene, enter new script, click **Insert voiceover** → **Update voiceover** → **Replace**.

### Supported Languages for AI Voiceovers
Arabic, Bangla, Dutch, English, French, German, Hindi, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Marathi, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Spanish, Tamil, Telugu, Thai, Turkish, Ukrainian, Vietnamese.

## Syncing Script to Voiceover

- A **"Voiceover outdated" badge** appears when the script doesn’t match the generated voiceover (on the scene or in the list).
- **To sync:**
  1. Open Vids, click Voiceover.
  2. Select a scene, update the script.
  3. Click **Update voiceover** → **Replace** (for current scene) or **Update all voiceovers** → **Replace** (for all scenes).
- **Tip:** Hover over the badge to see "Click update to sync the script and voiceover."

# Plan your video story with AI in Google Vids (Workspace Experiments)

This help article explains how to use the **“Help me create”** feature in **Google Vids** to generate a full video draft from a text prompt. The feature is part of the invite-only **Google Workspace Experiments** early access program, powered by **Gemini**.

---

## Feature Availability & Limits

  To check availability, open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/) – a “start creating” window appears if you have access.
- **Rollout:** Gradual; new features may not yet be available to all eligible users.

  - Up to **50 video objects** per video

## Create a Draft Video with “Help me create”

When you use **“Help me create,”** Vids generates a first draft containing:

- A suggested **outline** with scenes and auto‑generated text
- **Stock media** (videos, images, music)
- A **script**
- A **voiceover**
- **Video placeholders** to guide where you can add your own recordings

### Step‑by‑step

1. **Open a new video**  
   - Go to [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create) or type `vids.new` in your browser’s address bar.
2. Click **Start a new video**.
3. In the pop‑up, click **Plan your video story**.
4. **Enter a prompt** (or select a suggested one).
   - **Add related files**: type `@` and choose a file name from the suggestion list.
   - **Shuffle prompts**: click *Shuffle* to generate new suggestions.
5. Click **Next**.
6. **Edit the generated outline** – each scene appears as a tile:
   - Click a scene’s title to edit its prompt.
   - Delete a tile with the *Delete* icon.
   - Insert a tile below using the *Add* (+) icon.
   - To regenerate the outline entirely, type a new prompt and click **Try again**.
7. **Choose a design**:
   - Hover over a design to preview it.
   - Click a design to select it.
   - Click **Create the draft video**.
8. After creation, you can continue editing and customising.

## 1. Generate a Video Clip from Scratch (Text Prompt)

**Important**: Generated clips are only available in landscape or portrait orientation.

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc) on your computer.
2. Select **Veo 3.1** from the start menu – or, in an existing video, click the Veo icon on the right.
3. In the side panel dropdown, choose **Create from Scratch**.
4. (Optional) Choose **Landscape** or **Portrait** aspect ratio.
5. Enter a **text prompt** with details: subject, location, action, camera, lighting, dialogue, sounds, tone.  
   **Example prompts**:
   - “A cat walking down a fashion week catwalk wearing designer clothes with electronic music in the background.”
   - “A dog driving a race car in Italy at sunset with loud engine roars.”
6. (Optional) Set a clip aspect ratio different from your canvas using the option at the bottom of the prompt window.
7. Click **Generate**.
8. To refine, edit the text prompt and regenerate.
9. Hover over the generated clip and click **Insert** to add it to your Vid.
10. The clip appears on its own timeline track at the bottom – you can control it there.

> **Tip**: Always insert the clip into your Vid to keep it accessible for later editing within the session.

## 2. Animate an Image into an 8‑Second Video Clip

**Important**: Only **Landscape (16:9)** or **Portrait (9:16)** images are accepted. If the uploaded image has a different ratio, Vids automatically adds **black bars**.

### Steps

1. Open [Google Vids](https://docs.google.com/videos/create?usp=hc).

5. Click **Add image** and upload a **.jpg** or **.png** image.
   - **Best practice**: Use a high‑quality, sharp, clear source image – its composition and style set the visual direction.
6. Enter a motion‑focused description (do **not** re‑describe the scene). Describe how elements move or how the camera moves.  
   **Example** (from an elephant image): “The elephant moves around naturally.”
7. Click **Create**. The clip appears in the “Generate video” panel when ready.
8. To adjust, click **Edit prompt** and refine.
9. Hover and click **Insert** to add the clip to your Vid; it gets its own timeline track.
